# War for Eternity

## Overview

*War for Eternity: The Return of Traditionalism and the Rise of the Populist Right* is [[Benjamin R. Teitelbaum]]'s 2020 book on the political afterlife of [[Traditionalism]]. It follows the way an esoteric anti-modern intellectual current became relevant to a cluster of contemporary right-populist figures, especially Steve Bannon in the United States, Aleksandr Dugin in Russia, and Olavo de Carvalho in Brazil.

For the vault, this is a source note rather than a concept note. Use it for Teitelbaum's evidence, interviews, and network mapping; use [[Traditionalism]] for the durable concept.

## Key Facts

- Author: [[Benjamin R. Teitelbaum]]
- Main title: *War for Eternity*
- Common subtitles: *The Return of Traditionalism and the Rise of the Populist Right*; US edition also circulated as *Inside Bannon's Far-Right Circle of Global Power Brokers*
- Year: 2020
- Main figures in the vault frame: Steve Bannon, Aleksandr Dugin, Olavo de Carvalho, Gabor Vona, Jason Jorjani, and adjacent right-populist intellectual networks
- Core method: ethnographic and interview-based reporting layered onto intellectual history of Traditionalism and radical-right politics

## Analytical Value

- Network map: connects the United States, Russia, Brazil, and Hungary through a shared but uneven interest in Traditionalist language.
- Concept transfer: shows how metaphysical ideas about decline, hierarchy, cyclical time, and anti-modernity can become political and geopolitical rhetoric.
- Brazil bridge: especially useful for understanding why Olavo de Carvalho appears in the same interpretive field as Bannon and Dugin.
- Metapolitical lens: helps explain why media, education, cultural production, and intellectual networks matter to movements that distrust formal liberal-democratic politics.

## Use Guidelines

- Attribute claims about influence to Teitelbaum unless independently corroborated.
- Treat the book as evidence of networks, self-descriptions, interviews, and interpretive links; do not treat it as proof that Traditionalism mechanically caused policy decisions.
- Keep the distinction between lowercase traditionalism and capital-T Traditionalism visible.
- Use alongside Mark Sedgwick's *Against the Modern World* when the question is the longer intellectual history rather than Bannon-era politics.
