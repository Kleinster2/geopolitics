# The Insider

## Overview

Russian-language investigative journalism outlet (theins.ru) founded by [[Roman Dobrokhotov]] in 2013, focused on Russian state crimes, intelligence-service operations, corruption, election interference, and disinformation. The Insider operates from exile after being declared an "undesirable organization" by Russian authorities in 2023; its journalists work primarily from Latvia and other EU jurisdictions. The English-language edition is led by editor-in-chief [[Michael Weiss]].

The outlet is best known for its collaboration with [[Bellingcat]] on a series of landmark investigations including the [[Sergei Skripal]] poisoning (March 2018), the [[Alexei Navalny]] poisoning (August 2020), and the identification of GRU Unit 29155 operatives across multiple European operations.

## Key Facts

- Founder: [[Roman Dobrokhotov]] (Russian journalist and former opposition activist)
- Founded: 2013
- Operating jurisdiction: Latvia (exile) and elsewhere in EU
- Russian government designation: "Undesirable organization" (designated 2023)
- English edition leadership: [[Michael Weiss]], editor-in-chief
- Investigation focus: GRU / FSB operations, poisonings, intelligence-service infiltration of European institutions, sanctions evasion, oligarch finance, disinformation
- Funding model: Subscriber-supported + grants; no Russian state funding
- Collaboration: Regular joint investigations with [[Bellingcat]], [[Der Spiegel]], [[The Guardian]], [[Le Monde]]

## Notable investigations

- Sergei Skripal poisoning attribution (with Bellingcat, 2018) — identified GRU Unit 29155 operatives Anatoly Chepiga ("Ruslan Boshirov") and Alexander Mishkin ("Alexander Petrov")
- Alexei Navalny poisoning (with Bellingcat, 2020) — identified FSB Kriminalistika Institute operatives who tracked Navalny for years
- 2024 Latvian MEP / FSB operative investigation — co-authored joint investigation revealing a long-serving Latvian Member of the European Parliament had been an FSB operative
- Ongoing coverage of Russian operations against European institutions, far-right party financing, sanctions-evasion networks

## Analytical Position

The Insider operates from an explicit anti-Kremlin editorial position — its mission is the documentation of state crimes by the Russian Federation. The investigative methodology is rigorous (document-based, with sourcing through Russian-language record databases, leaked communications, and inside-Russia sources). The interpretive framing in commentary pieces is opinionated; investigative pieces stand on the documentary evidence.

For vault purposes: investigative pieces from The Insider should be treated as primary-source-tier when the underlying documents or open-source identifications are visible in the piece itself. Commentary and op-ed pieces are interpretive analysis from a clearly anti-Kremlin position.

## Why it matters

The Insider is one of the few outlets producing substantive original investigations of Russian intelligence-service operations from a Russian-language base. Most Western coverage of GRU/FSB activity ultimately traces back to The Insider's documentary work (often jointly published with Bellingcat). For the vault's [[Russia]], [[Ukraine]], and intelligence-operations threads, it is one of the highest-density original-source publications.

The 2023 "undesirable organization" designation by Russian authorities is a recognition of the outlet's effectiveness, not its irrelevance — the designation criminalises engagement with The Insider from inside Russia, which is the standard Russian-state response to credible investigative outlets.
