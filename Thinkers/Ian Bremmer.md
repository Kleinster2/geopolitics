# Ian Bremmer

## Overview
Political scientist, entrepreneur, and public intellectual. Founder and president of [[Eurasia Group]], the world's largest political risk consultancy. Creator of the [[G-Zero]] concept describing a world without global leadership. Prolific author, speaker, and media presence through GZERO Media.

## Key Facts
- **Born**: 1969, Boston
- **Education**: PhD Political Science, Stanford University
- **Position**: President, Eurasia Group (founded 1998)
- **Media**: Founder, GZERO Media
- **Known for**: G-Zero concept, Top Risks report

## Career

### Academic Background
- Stanford PhD in political science
- Youngest-ever national fellow at Hoover Institution
- Faculty at Columbia University
- Transitioned from academia to business

### Eurasia Group
- Founded 1998 at age 28
- Built into largest political risk firm globally
- ~200 analysts worldwide
- Clients include Fortune 500, governments, investors

### GZERO Media
- Spinoff media platform
- Daily briefings, podcasts, video
- Broader public reach than consulting
- Free access model

## Key Concepts

### [[G-Zero]]
Central intellectual contribution:
- World without global leadership
- No single power or alliance providing public goods
- "Every nation for itself"
- Post-American, but not multipolar (no alternative leader)
- First articulated ~2011

### Technopolar World
- Technology companies as geopolitical actors
- Big Tech rivaling state power
- Platform governance as quasi-sovereignty
- Digital space as contested territory

### On the 2026 Iran war (April 8, 2026)

After Trump announced a two-week US-Iran ceasefire on the evening of April 7, Bremmer gave a sweeping post-war assessment in an interview with Fergus McGee on *The Trump Report*. Full interview analysis lives in [[2026 Iran conflict]] (April 7 entry). Key Bremmer positions:

**Overall verdict:**
> "This is the worst foreign policy mistake that Trump has made as president in either of his two terms... he has succeeded in not making it dramatically worse than it already has been through the announcement that he made yesterday evening."

> "He's the most incompetent policy practitioner as president that the Americans have ever seen."

**The ceasefire as a unilateral climbdown:**
- Bremmer framed the ceasefire as something Trump "should have done weeks ago." The war was "a war of choice" entirely in Trump's hands, so only Trump could end it: "He's the one that decided to do it, to launch it... and so therefore, he's the one that unilaterally needed to decide when it was over."
- "Trump has gotten over himself. That is the thing that has changed here." Not the Iranian position, not diplomacy — Trump's personal willingness to "lump those outcomes."
- Of all war goals — regime change, enriched uranium removal, opening the Strait, helping the Iranian people — none were achieved. Only partial degradation of Iran's conventional military succeeded.

**On Trump's "genocidal threat" tweet the same morning:**
- The April 7 morning tweet ending with "God bless the Iranian people" was "not internally coherent." Bremmer dismissed taking it seriously: "At no point was he going to blow up all of Iran's infrastructure capabilities in four hours in the evening... it would have been devastating for the United States and for him personally."
- Criticized press coverage for focusing exclusively on the threat portion while ignoring the incoherent context.
- Singled out Aaron Rupar (a Trump-watcher) for an earlier claim that Trump was going to "use a nuke": "Why are you saying that?... There's literally no circumstance in which that is remotely plausible."

**TACO vs FAFO scale** (see [[TACO vs FAFO]]):
Bremmer articulated a dynamic he had described earlier (January 2026): Trump "backs down when faced with an adversary that was prepared to cause more pain than he expected and to tolerate." When the adversary cannot impose cost (Venezuela), Trump gets short-term wins via the madman theory. When the adversary can (China on tariffs, Iran on Hormuz), Trump is "forced to the table."

**On Trump's personal management:**
> "He's a child... he refuses to listen to anyone outside that he's so confident because he knows better than anybody else and his own people won't give him their clear unvarnished views. They say they will and then when they show me their text to the president, it's so much more flattering and careful and cautious and they want to stay in his good graces. And this is the absolute worst way to manage somebody like this because it makes him think that he's God's gift."

**On long-term US damage:**
> "From an American perspective, the US is less reliable, less trusted, um, not standing with its allies, threatening many of its core allies, calling them cowards, saying they're not willing to stick up for NATO. This war was unpopular and strongly so from the perspective of almost every American ally in the world, and Trump did not care one iota."

**Alignment and misalignment with the American people:**
Bremmer distinguished between Trump's popular mandate and his self-interested departures:
- **Aligned** (should be supported): closing the border, tariffs (against free trade consensus), forcing allies to pay more for defense.
- **Misaligned** (should be impeached/convicted): promising no new wars then launching the Iran war; [[Greenland Crisis|Greenland]] "because he wanted to win." "When Trump is promoting a policy that benefits himself at the expense of the nation, that's treasonous."

**On Venezuela** (a [[Venezuela]] success from Bremmer's perspective):
- "I was delighted when Venezuela went as well as it did." The madman theory plays well when the US is "much more powerful than another actor."
- Iran was the opposite scenario.

**On China's role in reaching the ceasefire:**
- "I would say they're constructive. I wouldn't say that they were decisive in getting to yes. Again, that was over 90% in the personal decision-making capacity of Trump."
- China engaged; Trump publicly "appreciated" their role. The China-Pakistan five-point plan (March 2026) was "constructive."
- China is feeling supply-chain pain despite better stockpiles than most.

**Ironic effect on Iranian regime security:**
- The regime "may create some level of security assurance for Iran going forward." If Iran believes a US/Israeli "mow the grass" strike is no longer feasible, the war demonstrated a working deterrent-through-pain.
- But this does not make Iran a "winner" — untested leadership, destroyed industrial base (petrochemicals, railways), continued domestic governance failures (water, power), and dead pragmatic interlocutors (Larijani).

**Midterm projection:**
- "He's going to get destroyed in the midterms. I mean, he's going to lose the House and the Senate is a coin flip."
- Factors: Iran, ICE, affordability, Epstein.
- Expects ambitious younger Republicans (specifically [[JD Vance]]) to begin distancing themselves. Pointed to a then-recent [[Maggie Haberman]] / [[Jonathan Swan]] *NYT* profile as "a small data point" of Vance positioning post-Trump.

*Source: Ian Bremmer interview with Fergus McGee, "Trump is the 'most incompetent' President on policy the US has ever seen," *The Trump Report* ([YouTube](https://youtu.be/yHPyeAXZHDY), April 8, 2026)*
