# Farah Jan

## Overview

Alliance politics and nuclear proliferation scholar at UPenn whose "blank check" article formalized the 1914 analogy for the US-Israel relationship and the 2026 Iran war. Distinctive angle: her South Asian expertise (Pakistan nuclear program, Pakistan-Saudi partnership) gives her a perspective on proliferation cascades that most Middle East commentators lack.

## The Blank Check Thesis

**"The Blank Check for Israel and the War with Iran"** (*War on the Rocks*, March 2026)

Jan's most significant contribution to the 2026 debate. Core arguments:

**The 1914 parallel**: Germany gave Austria-Hungary "unconditional backing to crush Serbia, a regional rival that posed no direct threat to Germany." The result destroyed both empires. The United States has done the same with Israel.

**Alliance entrapment mechanism**: Invokes Glenn Snyder's alliance security dilemma — states in alliances face two risks pulling in opposite directions (abandonment vs. entrapment). An unconditional US commitment to Israel eliminated every reason for the junior partner to show restraint.

**How the US was drawn in**: The US "did not enter the war with Iran because it was attacked or was about to be attacked." It entered after concluding that once Israel moved, American involvement would be unavoidable. When [[Marco Rubio]] briefed congressional leaders three days before strikes, the debate was not whether to fight but whether to strike alongside Israel or wait until Iran retaliated against US forces in the region.

**Key formulations**:
- "Unconditional alliances cause wars that neither partner intended and neither can win"
- "Washington chose war over diplomacy not because diplomacy failed, but because the junior partner's objectives required more than any deal could deliver"
- "When a great power justifies entry into a conflict as unavoidable because an ally is acting, control over escalation and foreign policy has shifted"
- "Diplomacy cannot survive an alliance structure in which the junior partner holds a veto over the senior partner's exit ramp"

## On Proliferation Cascades

**"Want Iran to Get the Bomb? Try Regime Change"** (*Responsible Statecraft*, February 2026): Argued military action against Iran could trigger waves of nuclear proliferation. Cited the September 2025 Saudi Arabia-Pakistan Strategic Mutual Defense Agreement, in which Pakistan's defense minister stated nuclear capabilities "will be made available" to Saudi Arabia. Jan's South Asian expertise makes her one of the few commentators who can trace the Pakistan-Saudi nuclear link in detail.
