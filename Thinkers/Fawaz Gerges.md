# Fawaz Gerges

## Overview

Lebanese-American professor of international relations at the London School of Economics, specializing in the modern Middle East, US-Middle East relations, and political Islam. A frequent commentator on Middle East affairs in English-language media.

## Affiliations
- **London School of Economics** — Professor of International Relations; Chair of Contemporary Middle East Studies.
- Previously held positions at Sarah Lawrence College, Oxford, Columbia, Princeton.

## Focus Areas
- Modern Middle East history and politics
- US foreign policy toward the Middle East
- Political Islam, Al-Qaeda, ISIS
- The making of the modern Arab world
- Arab-Israeli relations

## Selected Works
- *The Making of the Arab World: Nasser, Qutb, and the Clash That Shaped the Middle East* (2018)
- *ISIS: A History* (2016)
- *Obama and the Middle East: The End of America's Moment?* (2012)
- *The Far Enemy: Why Jihad Went Global* (2005)
- *America and Political Islam: Clash of Cultures or Clash of Interests?* (1999)

## 2026 Iran War Commentary

Gerges has been one of the more prominent English-language voices diagnosing the [[2026 Iran conflict]] as a strategic inflection point for US power. Told Middle East Eye:

> "I think we are witnessing a Suez Crisis moment for the US."

See [[Suez Moment]] for the broader analytical framework and other analysts invoking the same comparison.
