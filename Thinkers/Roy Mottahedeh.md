# Roy Mottahedeh

## Overview

Roy Parviz Mottahedeh (1940-2024) was the Gurney Professor of History at Harvard and the most influential Western scholar of Iranian political culture in the late 20th century. His *The Mantle of the Prophet* (1985) shaped how a generation of policymakers, diplomats, and analysts understood the intellectual foundations of the Islamic Republic — the seminary system, how clerics think, how religious authority intersects with political power.

## Key Facts
- **Position**: Gurney Professor of History (later Emeritus), Harvard University
- **Died**: July 30, 2024 (age 84)
- **Education**: PhD, Harvard University
- **Directorships**: Center for Middle Eastern Studies, Harvard (1987-1990); founding director, Prince Alwaleed Bin Talal Islamic Studies Program, Harvard (2006-2011)
- **Nationality**: American (Iranian heritage — "Parviz" is his Persian middle name)

## Major Work

### *The Mantle of the Prophet: Religion and Politics in Iran* (1985)

Simon & Schuster (first edition). Revised editions by Oneworld Publications (2000, 2008 — the latter adding a chronology through the Ahmadinejad era). International bestseller, continuously in print for four decades. Translated into numerous languages.

**Structure**: The intellectual biography of a young mullah ("Ali Hashemi," a composite character based on real people) in the shrine city of Qom, set against the sweep of Persian and Shi'ite intellectual history from the Middle Ages through the 1979 Revolution.

**What it does**: Not a straight history of the revolution. Instead, it maps the deep currents of thought — from Zoroaster to Avicenna, from Kasravi to Khomeini — that shaped Iranian religious and political culture and made the revolution intelligible.

**Why it matters**: Before Mottahedeh, Western understanding of the Islamic Republic was thin — either demonizing or dismissive. *Mantle* gave policymakers and analysts a framework for understanding how the seminary system works, how clerical authority is constructed, and why the revolution's intellectual roots run much deeper than 1979.

**Recommended by**: Ali Vaez (Crisis Group Iran Project Director) on *The Ezra Klein Show* (March 14, 2026) — for understanding "the texture of post-revolutionary society."

## Significance for the Vault

Mottahedeh's framework is essential background for understanding:
- Why the Islamic Republic's institutional structure is more resilient than Western observers expect (see [[IRGC]], [[Ali Vaez]] succession analysis)
- How clerical authority legitimizes or constrains political power (relevant to Mojtaba Khamenei's succession)
- The intellectual depth behind Iran's revolutionary ideology — it is not reducible to "fanaticism" or "authoritarianism"
