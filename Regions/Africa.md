# Africa

## Overview
The world's youngest and fastest-growing continent, increasingly important in great power competition. Rich in resources but facing governance, security, and development challenges. Rising strategic significance in multipolar world.

## Key Characteristics
- **Population**: ~1.4 billion (projected 2.5 billion by 2050)
- **Countries**: 54 recognized states
- **GDP**: ~$3 trillion combined
- **Resources**: 30% of global mineral reserves
- **Youth**: Median age ~19 years

## Sub-Regions

### North Africa
- Egypt, Libya, Tunisia, Algeria, Morocco
- Mediterranean orientation
- Arab/Berber identity
- Oil/gas resources
- Migration gateway to Europe

### West Africa
- Nigeria dominant (200M+ population)
- ECOWAS regional body
- Sahel crisis spillover
- Democratic mixed record
- Coastal vs. Sahel divide

### East Africa
- Ethiopia, Kenya, Tanzania core
- Horn of Africa instability
- EAC integration
- Indian Ocean trade
- Great Lakes conflicts

### Central Africa
- DRC resource wealth
- Chronic instability
- Weak states
- ECCAS regional body
- Limited integration

### Southern Africa
- South Africa anchor
- SADC regional body
- Most developed sub-region
- Zimbabwe challenges
- Resource wealth

## Strategic Importance

### Resources
- Oil/gas (Nigeria, Angola, Libya)
- Critical minerals (DRC cobalt, rare earths)
- Agricultural potential
- Strategic metals

### Demographics
- Youngest population globally
- Labor force growth
- Consumer market expansion
- Migration pressures

### Geopolitics
- UN voting bloc (54 votes)
- Great power competition arena
- China's primary investment destination
- Counter-terrorism theater

## Great Power Competition

### China
- Largest trading partner
- BRI investments ($150+ billion)
- Infrastructure projects
- Debt relationships
- Non-interference appeal
- Resource access

### United States
- AFRICOM (military)
- Counter-terrorism focus
- AGOA trade preferences
- Democracy/governance emphasis
- Competing with China narrative

### Russia
- Wagner Group deployments
- Arms sales
- Mining concessions
- Anti-Western narrative
- Security partnerships (Mali, CAR, Sudan)

### European Union
- Colonial legacies
- Development aid
- Migration management
- France's diminishing role
- Trade relationships

### Others
- Turkey (expanding presence)
- UAE/Saudi (Horn investments)
- India (diaspora, trade)
- Japan (TICAD framework)

## Security Challenges

### Sahel Crisis
- Jihadist expansion
- State weakness
- Climate stress
- French withdrawal
- Wagner involvement

### Horn of Africa
- Ethiopia civil conflict
- Somalia/Al-Shabaab
- Eritrea isolation
- Sudan instability

### Great Lakes
- DRC eastern violence
- Rwandan involvement
- M23 and armed groups
- Resource conflicts

### Terrorism

- Al-Shabaab (Somalia)
- JNIM/ISGS (Sahel)
