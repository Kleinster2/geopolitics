# Asia-Pacific

## Overview
The world's most economically dynamic and strategically contested region. Home to rising powers, nuclear states, territorial disputes, and the central arena of US-China competition.

## Key Characteristics
- **Population**: ~4.5 billion (60% of world)
- **GDP**: ~$35 trillion (~40% of global)
- **Trade**: Most active shipping lanes
- **Military**: Multiple nuclear powers, large conventional forces

## Major Powers
- [[China]] - Regional hegemon aspirant
- [[United States]] - Offshore balancer, alliance hub
- [[Japan]] - Economic power, remilitarizing
- [[India]] - Rising power, China rival
- [[Russia]] - Diminished but present

## Sub-Regions

### Northeast Asia
- China, Japan, South Korea, North Korea, Taiwan
- Most economically developed
- Korean peninsula tensions
- Taiwan Strait flashpoint
- Historical animosities

### Southeast Asia
- [[ASEAN]] 10 members
- South China Sea disputes
- Economic growth hub
- Great power competition arena

### South Asia
- India-Pakistan rivalry
- China-India border tensions
- Afghanistan instability

### Oceania
- Australia, New Zealand, Pacific Islands
- AUKUS security partnership
- China's Pacific outreach

## Flashpoints

### Taiwan
Highest risk of great power conflict. China claims sovereignty; US committed to Taiwan's defense capability.

### South China Sea
Overlapping claims (China, Vietnam, Philippines, Malaysia, Brunei). Freedom of navigation operations.

### Korean Peninsula
Nuclear North Korea, armistice (not peace treaty), US forces in South.

### India-China Border
Disputed LAC, periodic clashes (Galwan 2020).

## Regional Architecture
- **US Alliance Network**: Japan, South Korea, Australia, Philippines, Thailand
- **ASEAN**: Regional cooperation forum
- **Quad**: US, Japan, Australia, India
- **AUKUS**: US, UK, Australia (nuclear subs)
- **RCEP**: Trade agreement (includes China)
- **CPTPP**: Trade agreement (excludes China, US)

## Key Trends
1. US-China strategic competition intensifying
2. Military modernization across region
3. Economic decoupling debates
4. Taiwan tensions rising
5. Alliance strengthening (US side)
6. ASEAN hedging strategies
