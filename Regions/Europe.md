# Europe

## Overview
Historically the center of world politics, now a prosperous but strategically dependent region navigating between US security guarantees and its own integration project. Russia's 2022 Ukraine invasion fundamentally altered European security.

## Key Characteristics
- **Population**: ~450 million (EU), ~750 million (geographic)
- **GDP**: ~$18 trillion (EU)
- **Integration**: Most advanced regional integration (EU)
- **Security**: NATO alliance, US extended deterrence

## Key Institutions

### European Union
- 27 member states
- Single market, common currency (Eurozone)
- Shared foreign policy aspirations
- Challenges: cohesion, competitiveness, defense

### NATO
- Transatlantic security alliance
- Article 5 collective defense
- Expanded post-2022 (Finland, Sweden)
- US provides bulk of capabilities

## Sub-Regions

### Western Europe
- France, Germany, UK, Benelux
- Economic/political core
- Franco-German engine of EU

### Northern Europe
- Nordics, Baltics
- High NATO commitment
- Russia threat perception

### Southern Europe
- Mediterranean states
- Migration pressures
- Economic challenges

### Central/Eastern Europe
- Post-communist states
- Frontline with Russia
- Strong Atlanticism

### Eastern Europe (non-EU)
- Ukraine, Belarus, Moldova
- Contested space
- Russia's claimed sphere

## Strategic Issues

### Russia Challenge
- Ukraine war ongoing
- Energy weaponization
- Hybrid threats
- Nuclear saber-rattling

### European Defense
- Strategic autonomy debate
- Burden-sharing with US
- Capability gaps
- Defense industrial base

### Transatlantic Relations
- US commitment questions
- China policy divergence
- Trade tensions
- Technology cooperation

### Internal Challenges
- Democratic backsliding (Hungary)
- Migration
- Economic competitiveness
- Energy transition
