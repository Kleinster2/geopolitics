# Middle East

## Overview
Strategically vital region due to energy resources, religious significance, and location connecting Europe, Asia, and Africa. Characterized by persistent conflicts, authoritarian governance, and great power involvement.

## Key Characteristics
- **Energy**: ~50% of global oil reserves, ~40% of gas
- **Religion**: Birthplace of Judaism, Christianity, Islam
- **Demographics**: Young, growing populations
- **Governance**: Predominantly authoritarian

## Major Powers

### Regional
- [[Saudi Arabia]] - Sunni leader, oil superpower
- [[Iran]] - Shia power, revolutionary state
- [[Israel]] - Military dominant, nuclear capable
- [[Turkey]] - NATO member, Ottoman nostalgia
- [[Egypt]] - Population weight, historical leadership

### External
- [[United States]] - Security guarantor, reduced engagement
- [[Russia]] - Syria intervention, arms sales
- [[China]] - Economic interests, diplomatic expansion

## Key Conflicts

### Israeli-Palestinian
- Decades-long occupation
- Two-state solution moribund
- Gaza conflicts
- Settlement expansion

### Iran vs. Gulf States/Israel
- Saudi-Iran rivalry (Sunni-Shia)
- Proxy wars (Yemen, Syria, Lebanon)
- Iran nuclear program
- Abraham Accords realignment

### Civil Wars/Instability
- Syria (ongoing since 2011)
- Yemen (humanitarian catastrophe)
- Libya (divided)
- Iraq (fragile state)

## Strategic Issues

### Energy Transition
- Oil demand peak concerns
- Economic diversification (Saudi Vision 2030)
- Renewable energy investments

### Nuclear Proliferation
- Iran nuclear program
- JCPOA collapse
- Saudi nuclear ambitions

### Great Power Competition
- US retrenchment
- China's BRI and oil imports
- Russia's military presence (Syria)

### Sectarianism
- Sunni-Shia divide
- Iranian influence network
- Saudi counter-mobilization

## Key Frameworks

### Alliances
- US-Gulf (informal)
- US-Israel
- Iran-Syria-Hezbollah-Hamas axis
- Abraham Accords (Israel-UAE-Bahrain-Morocco)

### Organizations
- Arab League (weak)
- GCC (Gulf Cooperation Council)
- Organization of Islamic Cooperation

2. Abraham Accords normalization
3. Saudi-Iran rapprochement attempts
4. China's growing role
5. Regional states seeking autonomy
