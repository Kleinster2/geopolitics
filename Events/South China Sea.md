# South China Sea

## Overview
Contested waters where China's expansive claims clash with Southeast Asian states and US freedom of navigation. Strategic waterway for global trade, resource-rich seabed, and arena for US-China competition.

## Strategic Importance
- **Trade**: $3+ trillion in annual shipping
- **Energy**: Oil/gas reserves (disputed estimates)
- **Fisheries**: Critical food source for region
- **Military**: Control = power projection capability

## Competing Claims

### Nine-Dash Line (China)
- Claims ~90% of South China Sea
- "Historic rights" basis
- Rejected by 2016 arbitration (China ignores)
- Island-building program

### Other Claimants
| Country | Claims |
|---------|--------|
| Vietnam | Paracels, Spratlys |
| Philippines | Spratlys, Scarborough Shoal |
| Malaysia | Southern Spratlys |
| Brunei | Southern area |
| Taiwan | Same as PRC claims |

### United States
- No territorial claim
- Freedom of navigation operations (FONOPs)
- Rejects excessive maritime claims
- Philippine treaty ally

## Key Features

### Paracel Islands
- China controls (seized 1974)
- Vietnam claims
- Military installations

### Spratly Islands
- Multiple claimants occupy features
- China's artificial islands
- Airstrips, missiles, radar

### Scarborough Shoal
- China controls since 2012
- Philippines claims (EEZ)
- Ongoing harassment

## China's Actions

### Island Building (2013-2016)
- Created 3,200 acres of land
- Military facilities
- Runways capable of fighters/bombers
- Missile systems
- Radars

### Gray Zone Operations
- Coast guard harassment
- Maritime militia ("little blue men")
- Water cannon incidents
- Fishing exclusion

## Legal Framework

### UNCLOS
- 200nm EEZ rights
- Freedom of navigation
- China signed but disputes application

### 2016 Arbitration (Philippines v. China)
- Tribunal rejected nine-dash line
- Ruled for Philippines
- China: "null and void"
- No enforcement mechanism

## US Response
- FONOPs (regular)
- Defense treaty with Philippines (Article IV)
- Increased presence
- Ally coordination
- EDCA bases in Philippines

## Code of Conduct Negotiations
- ASEAN-China negotiations
- Ongoing since 2002
- No binding agreement
- Stalling tactic?

## Escalation Risks
- Philippine resupply missions (Second Thomas Shoal)
- Coast guard confrontations
- Treaty invocation scenario
- Miscalculation potential

## Recent Developments
- Philippine-US alliance strengthening
- EDCA expanded sites
- Continued Chinese pressure
- International attention increasing
