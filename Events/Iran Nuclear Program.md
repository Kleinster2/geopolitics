# Iran Nuclear Program

## Overview
Iran's nuclear program stands at the threshold of weapons capability. With advanced enrichment (60% purity) and collapsed diplomacy (JCPOA), Iran can produce weapons-grade material in weeks. The program represents the most dangerous nuclear proliferation crisis, with potential for regional war.

## Current Status (2024-2025)

### Enrichment
- **60% purity**: Weapons-usable threshold
- **Stockpile**: 100+ kg at 60%
- **Capacity**: Thousands of centrifuges
- **Breakout time**: 1-2 weeks to weapons-grade (90%)

### Weaponization
- **Assessment**: Knowledge preserved
- **Delivery**: Ballistic missiles capable
- **Gap**: Warhead miniaturization (unclear status)
- **IAEA**: Cannot verify peaceful intent

## Technical Details

### Enrichment Process
```
Natural uranium (0.7% U-235)
    ↓ Centrifuge enrichment
Low enriched uranium (3.67% - reactor fuel)
    ↓ Further enrichment
Highly enriched uranium (20%+ - medical)
    ↓ Further enrichment
Weapons-grade (90%+ - bomb material)
```

### Key Facilities

| Facility | Function | Status |
|----------|----------|--------|
| Natanz | Main enrichment | Operating, hardened |
| Fordow | Underground enrichment | Operating, 60% |
| Isfahan | Conversion | Active |
| Arak | Heavy water reactor | Modified under JCPOA |
| Parchin | Suspected weaponization | Military, limited access |

## February 28, 2026: Strikes on Nuclear Facilities

**See main article: [[2026 Iran conflict]]**

US-Israeli Operation Epic Fury directly targeted nuclear infrastructure (confirmed, CENTCOM). B-2 stealth bombers used 2,000-lb bombs against hardened facilities.

**IAEA assessment (March 3-4, 2026):**
- Director General [[Rafael Grossi]]: "We never had information indicating that there was a structured, systematic program to build, to construct a nuclear weapon" (confirmed, CNN)
- "There wasn't to be a bomb tomorrow or the day after tomorrow"
- IAEA confirmed some damage to Natanz facility from the strikes (Al Jazeera)
- Arms Control Association: "[Iran's nuclear and missile programs did] not pose imminent threat" (March 2026)

**Significance:** The nuclear program was cited as a casus belli by the administration, but Secretary of State [[Marco Rubio]] stated on March 2 that the real objective was ballistic missile destruction and naval threat — not the nuclear program. IAEA confirmation that Iran had no weapons program undermines the nuclear justification for the strikes.

**Post-war nuclear risk:** [[Fred Kagan]] (AEI) and [[Ryan Bohl]] (RANE) both flag the danger that surviving Iranian leaders may now conclude "America and Israel attacked anyway, so why not get the bomb?" — hardliners now have a viable argument they lacked before June 2025.
