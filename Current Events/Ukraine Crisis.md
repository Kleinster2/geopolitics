# Ukraine Crisis

## Overview
Russia's 2022 full-scale invasion of Ukraine, the largest land war in Europe since WWII. Tests European security architecture, NATO unity, and limits of Western support. Outcome will shape international order for decades.

## Background

### Historical Context
- Soviet Ukraine (1922-1991)
- Independence 1991
- Orange Revolution 2004
- Euromaidan 2014
- Crimea annexation 2014
- Donbas war 2014-2022

### Russian Grievances (Claimed)
- NATO expansion
- "Denazification"
- Protect Russian speakers
- Ukraine as artificial state
- Security concerns

### Western/Ukrainian View
- Unprovoked aggression
- Violation of sovereignty
- Imperial revanchism
- War of conquest
- Genocide rhetoric

## Timeline

### 2014
- Euromaidan revolution
- Russia seizes Crimea
- Donbas separatist war begins
- Minsk agreements (failed)

### 2021-2022
- Russian buildup
- Failed diplomacy
- February 24, 2022: Full invasion

### War Phases
1. **Initial assault** (Feb-Mar 2022): Failed Kyiv offensive
2. **Eastern focus** (Apr-Aug 2022): Donbas fighting
3. **Ukrainian counteroffensive** (Sep-Nov 2022): Kharkiv, Kherson
4. **Attrition** (2023): Limited gains both sides
5. **Stalemate?** (2024+): Uncertain trajectory

## Military Situation

### Russia
- Initial failures (logistics, morale)
- Mobilization (300k+)
- Defensive posture
- Iran drones, North Korea ammunition
- Nuclear threats

### Ukraine
- Successful defense
- Western weapons critical
- Manpower challenges
- Infrastructure attacks
- Counteroffensive difficulties

## Western Response

### Military Aid
- US: $75+ billion committed
- EU/NATO: Major contributors
- Artillery, armor, air defense
- F-16s (pending/delivered)
- Escalation management

### Economic Sanctions
- Unprecedented on major economy
- SWIFT exclusion (partial)
- Asset freezes
- Energy restrictions
- Effectiveness debated

### Political Support
- NATO unity (remarkable)
- EU membership candidate status
- G7 coordination
- UN General Assembly votes

## Humanitarian Impact
- 10,000+ civilian deaths (likely undercount)
- 6+ million refugees
- Millions internally displaced
- Infrastructure destruction
- War crimes allegations

## Scenarios

### Ukrainian Victory
- Russian withdrawal
- Territorial restoration (including Crimea?)
- Unlikely without escalation

### Russian Victory
- Ukraine conquest/partition
- Regime change Kyiv
- Currently unlikely

### Frozen Conflict
- Korean-style armistice
- Territorial division
- No peace treaty
- Most likely?

### Negotiated Settlement
- Territorial compromises
- Security guarantees
- Currently no basis

## Global Implications
- European security transformed
- NATO revitalized
- Russia-West rupture
- China watching Taiwan lessons
- Global South ambivalence
- Energy/food security
- Nuclear taboo tested
