# EU-India Free Trade Agreement

## Overview
Free trade agreement between the [[European Union]] and [[India]], expected to be signed January 25-27, 2026 in New Delhi. Would be the EU's largest trade deal by population covered (~1/4 of global population).

## Timeline

| Date | Development |
|------|-------------|
| **2007** | Negotiations launched |
| **2013** | Talks stalled over autos, wine, IP, labor standards |
| **June 2022** | Negotiations relaunched |
| **January 2026** | Von der Leyen and Costa travel to New Delhi |
| **January 26, 2026** | EU leaders attend India's 77th Republic Day as guests of honor |
| **January 27, 2026** | FTA signing expected |

Von der Leyen at Davos: "We are on the cusp of a historic trade agreement. Some call it the mother of all deals. One that would create a market of 2 billion people, accounting for almost a quarter of global GDP."

## Scope

### Included
- Lower tariffs on European automobiles and wine
- Expanded market access for Indian textiles, electronics, chemicals
- Investment protection agreement
- Geographical indications agreement

### Excluded
- Agriculture (carved out from negotiations)
- Dairy products (confirmed)
- Sugar (confirmed)

### Outstanding Issues
- Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM)
- India seeking zero-duty access for textiles, leather
- EU seeking cuts on autos, wine, medical devices, meat, poultry
- Intellectual property regime

## Strategic Context

### Trump Factor
The deal accelerated after US protectionism intensified:
- August 2025: Trump doubled tariffs on Indian imports to 50% (among highest globally)
- Both EU and India hedging against US trade war
- Demonstrates alternatives to US market access

### Security Dimension
The 16th India-EU Summit (January 2026) expected to also deliver:
- Upgraded security and defense partnership
- Would place India's EU security ties on par with Japan and South Korea

### Geopolitical Significance
- EU diversifying trade relationships amid [[Trade War 2025-2026]]
- India balancing between US, EU, and maintaining strategic autonomy
- Counterweight to China's regional economic influence
- Example of middle powers/blocs finding alternatives to US-centric trade
