# Alaska Summit (August 2025)

## Overview
Summit meeting between President Trump and President Putin on August 15, 2025, at Joint Base Elmendorf-Richardson in Anchorage, Alaska. First time Putin was invited to a Western country since ordering the 2022 invasion of Ukraine. Ended without agreement, with Trump suggesting Ukraine should cede territory.

## Key Facts
- **Date**: August 15, 2025
- **Location**: Joint Base Elmendorf-Richardson, Anchorage, Alaska
- **Participants**:
  - US: Trump, Secretary of State Marco Rubio, Special Envoy Steve Witkoff
  - Russia: Putin, Foreign Minister Sergey Lavrov, adviser Yuri Ushakov
- **Notable absence**: Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy

## Outcome
- No agreement announced
- Trump suggested Ukraine should cede territory to end war
- European allies alarmed by exclusion from talks about European security
- Prompted emergency follow-up meeting

## Follow-Up: White House Multilateral Meeting

Three days later, on August 18, 2025, European leaders convened at the White House in a meeting organized in approximately 70 hours (per Victoria Coates, Heritage).

**Attendees:**
- European Commission President Ursula von der Leyen
- British PM Keir Starmer
- Finnish President Alexander Stubb
- French President Emmanuel Macron
- Italian PM Giorgia Meloni
- German Chancellor Friedrich Merz
- NATO Secretary General Mark Rutte

**Agenda:**
- Robust security guarantees for Ukraine (Article 5-like)
- Reasserting European/Ukrainian demands in any peace deal
- Countering momentum given to Kremlin by Trump's warm reception of Putin

**Context from Compass Points:**
Victoria Coates praised this meeting's rapid organization, noting the collective GDP represented (~$50 trillion) dwarfs Russia-China-Iran combined (~$20 trillion).

## Significance

### For Ukraine
- Exclusion from talks about its own future
- Pressure to accept territorial losses
- European allies attempting to reassert Ukrainian interests

### For Europe
- Marginalization in discussions about European security
- Prompted increased European defense coordination
- Highlighted dependence on US decisions

### For US-Russia Relations
- First high-level direct engagement since 2022
- Signal of Trump's willingness to negotiate with Putin
- Controversial domestically and with allies
