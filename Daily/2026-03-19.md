# 2026-03-19

## Key Developments
- **Munitions attrition picture (Bloomberg, March 10-11):** Iran had ~2,500 ballistic missiles pre-war, ~700 fired; 2,100+ Shaheds fired. US/allies burned through >1,000 PAC-3 interceptors (nearly 2x annual production). THAAD radar damaged in Jordan (first combat loss; only 8 globally), early warning radar destroyed in Qatar, 7+ MQ-9 Reapers lost. Iran's 358 SAM (IR-guided, no radar warning) prevented US air supremacy — a first in modern US warfare. Production gap: PAC-3 at 650/year (target 2,000 by 2030); Tomahawks <100/year; SM-6/SM-3 at 96/year. Precision Strike Missile used in combat for first time. Australia supplying UAE; Ukraine offered drone-defense systems; South Korea concerned about US pulling anti-missile systems.
- **Larijani killed (March 18):** Israel killed Ali Larijani (national security adviser), eliminating the regime's most pragmatic senior interlocutor. Ghalibaf now dominant civilian war leader.
- **Iran's ceasefire terms crystallize:** Guarantees against future attack + sanctions relief. Ghalibaf: "no threat of war anymore" before Iran stops. Vakil (Chatham House): "both very hard outcomes." Araghchi called truce claims "delusional."
- **IRGC partisan warfare pivot:** Abandoning bases, scattering, calibrating fire rate for sustained conflict. Comparing tactics to Hezbollah/Houthis.
- **Dubai International Airport struck** by Iran — highest-profile civilian infrastructure hit.
- **~8,000 US targets struck** (up from 6,000 at Day 14); Israel launched "similar number." Drone/missile capacity down 90%+.
- **Gabbard (DNI) testimony:** Iran "remains capable." If regime survives, will rebuild over years.
- **Mulder thesis:** Nicholas Mulder (Cornell) argues the era of US dominance in economic warfare is over — China (rare earths) and Iran (Hormuz) have deployed their own economic weapons, breaking the post-Cold War US monopoly. Iran's energy shock forced the US to temporarily relent on Russian oil sanctions.
