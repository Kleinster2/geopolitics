# 2026-03-25

## Key Developments

- **15-point ceasefire plan:** Trump administration sent Iran a 15-point ceasefire proposal via Pakistan (one-month ceasefire → negotiate terms). Includes: halt all enrichment, dismantle Natanz/Isfahan/Fordow, hand over 60% enriched uranium to IAEA, end proxy operations, keep Hormuz open, limit missiles. Israeli officials uneasy about a quick "framework agreement."
- **Iran's categorical rejection:** Lt. Col. Zolfaghari mocked US talks claims: "Don't dress up your defeat as an agreement. Someone like us will never come to terms with someone like you. Not now, not ever."
- **82nd Airborne deploying:** Pentagon ordered ~2,000-3,000 troops from 82nd Airborne to Middle East, plus two Marine Expeditionary Units (~5,000 Marines). Total US forces now ~50,000+.
- **Nuclear escalation spiral:** Natanz enrichment facility struck (March 22); Iran retaliated by firing at Dimona and Arad (near Israeli nuclear center). Bushehr NPP struck again (no damage per IAEA). UN rights chief Türk: "States are flirting with unmitigated catastrophe."
- **Oil:** Brent fell ~6% to $98.30 on ceasefire hopes. Had crashed from $112 to ~$100 on Monday when Trump announced talks. $500M in suspicious oil trades placed minutes before Trump's Truth Social post (insider trading concerns).
- **Lebanon:** Iran ambassador expelled; first Israeli death from Lebanese fire (woman killed in north).
- **Iran partial Hormuz offer:** told IMO "non-hostile" vessels may transit freely.
- **UK:** offered to host international summit on reopening Hormuz.
