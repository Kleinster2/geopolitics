# 2026-04-09 / 2026-04-10

## Key Developments

- **[[2026 Iran conflict]] ceasefire (announced April 7)**: Processed [[Ian Bremmer]] interview on *The Trump Report* (April 8) covering the two-week US-Iran ceasefire Trump announced the evening of April 7 and declared "total and complete victory." Bremmer framed the ceasefire as a "unilateral climbdown" — none of the war's original goals were achieved (no regime change, no removal of enriched uranium, no reopening of the Strait of Hormuz, more suffering for the Iranian people). Called it "the worst foreign policy mistake that Trump has made as president in either of his two terms." The announcement came the same day Trump had posted a "genocidal threat" on Truth Social in the morning.

- **Carve-outs in the ceasefire**: No agreement with [[Houthis]] or [[Hezbollah]]; Pakistani PM Shehbaz Sharif reportedly believed he had obtained a Hezbollah ceasefire from Trump but Netanyahu "very quickly" repudiated it. In the 12 hours between announcement and Bremmer interview, Israel launched "the most intense Israeli bombing of Lebanon since the war started." Early unconfirmed reports of UAE strikes on Iranian oil facilities and Iranian retaliation against the UAE — Bremmer flagged possible Middle East fragmentation where UAE and Israel alone were uncomfortable with ending the war.

- **New diplomatic interlocutor**: With [[Ali Larijani]] killed by Israel March 18, the senior Iranian participant in direct diplomacy is now [[Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf]] (parliament speaker, former IRGC). Bremmer: "not as capable as Larijani" but "speaks with authority." Facilitators: Pakistan and Oman.

- **TACO vs FAFO**: Bremmer articulated this as the driver of the climbdown — Trump "backs down when faced with an adversary that was prepared to cause more pain than he expected and to tolerate." Iran demonstrated durable retaliatory capacity including missiles "the Americans did not believe that they had stockpiled." Compared to [[Venezuela]] (where madman theory worked), Iran "was really disastrous."

- **China's role**: Trump publicly "appreciated" China's engagement. Bremmer: "constructive" but not "decisive" — "over 90% in the personal decision-making capacity of Trump." China's March China-Pakistan five-point plan was useful background.

- **Midterm projection**: Bremmer predicts Trump "going to get destroyed in the midterms. He's going to lose the House and the Senate is a coin flip." Expects [[JD Vance]] and other ambitious younger Republicans to begin distancing themselves — cited a recent [[Maggie Haberman]] / [[Jonathan Swan]] NYT profile of Vance as "a small data point."
