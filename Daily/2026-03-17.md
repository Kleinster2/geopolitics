# 2026-03-17

## Key Developments

- FT published comprehensive analysis of Iran's mine warfare threat at the Strait of Hormuz: pre-positioned seabed mines assessed "almost certain" (Nadimi, Washington Institute); Iran's 6,000+ mine stockpile; US mine countermeasures crisis (<1% of naval budget for decades, Avenger minesweepers retired, LCS replacements slow); first mine clearance under fire since Korean War; perception deterrence (Savitz, Rand)
- FT *Rachman Review* podcast (March 12): Sir Simon Gass — former UK ambassador to Iran, JIC chair, JCPOA lead negotiator — most credentialed Western analyst yet on the conflict. Key new insights: (1) Trump's Day 1 "free the Iranian people" rhetoric triggered Hormuz closure (existential trigger thesis); (2) Ali Khamenei's anti-nuclear weapons fatwa has lapsed with his death; (3) three sequential negotiation failures have killed US-Iran diplomacy; (4) nationalist backlash from Tehran contacts — even regime opponents angry at US bombing; (5) Gulf states questioning Abraham Accords and US security guarantee
- Rachman column (March 16): "Why Hormuz will haunt us long after this war ends" — Hormuz as permanent non-nuclear deterrent that outlasts the war; war aims collapsed to one objective (reopen strait); Trump was warned pre-war but "waved away" concerns; permanent incentive shift from regime-change rhetoric; Kharg Island occupation and ground troops mooted; allied reluctance from year of tariffs/insults
- FT (March 15-16): Trump threatens to delay Beijing summit with Xi (~late March) over Iran war — first concrete diplomatic casualty on US-China relations. Bessent met He Lifeng in Paris. China refused warship request; Global Times: "sharing the risk of a war Washington started." Trump specifically requested Japanese minesweepers — constitutional constraints. Feigenbaum (Carnegie): "bizarro world" of begging Beijing for expeditionary deployment
- FT (March 17): Emile Hokayem (IISS, director of regional security) — "Who is winning the Middle East war?" Assessment: operational metrics stunning (13,000+ strikes) but strategic success elusive. Iran retains advantages of geography, time, asymmetry, "superior pain tolerance." Key new insights: (1) MEU rushed from Okinawa, air defense systems redeployed from Korean peninsula — force posture strains; (2) Iran's deliberate targeting prioritization: UAE bore brunt, Israel deprioritized; (3) cruise missile reserve theory — Iran holding back cruise missiles for close combat at Hormuz; (4) one-third of Saudi-bound drones targeted Shaybah oil site; (5) sustainment burden unsustainable

- Bloomberg, The Mishal Husain Show (March 17): Vali Nasr interview — "last war" thesis (Iran seeking permanent change to US cost calculus, not just survival); Mojtaba Khamenei's IRGC relationship and "charisma through suffering"; new IRGC generation (Syria/Iraq vs Iran-Iraq War); war-vs-regime binary displacing regime-vs-people binary; nuclear door open (India-Pakistan model, fatwa dead with Khamenei); maximum pressure radicalization (JCPOA counterfactual: 35% middle class growth vs 20% poverty); "global guerrilla strategy" framework; Reza Pahlavi assessment (popularity without ground game, nationalism problem); Iranian-American community fractures; territorial integrity as unifying concern
