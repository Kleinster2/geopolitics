# Scott Bessent

## Overview
US Treasury Secretary in the Trump 2.0 administration. Former hedge fund manager, founder of Key Square Group. Previously worked at Soros Fund Management.

## Policy Positions

### On Greenland
At Davos (January 2026):
- Said Trump "believes that Greenland is essential for the Golden Dome Missile Shield"
- Called Denmark "irrelevant" - dismissive of a NATO ally that had the highest per capita death rate of coalition countries in Afghanistan

### On Europe
Part of Trump administration's confrontational posture toward European allies at Davos. His dismissive comments about Denmark reflect broader administration view of European allies as expendable.

## Significance
Initially seen by some as a potential "adult in the room" who might moderate Trump's economic policies. His Davos performance suggests he is instead amplifying rather than tempering the administration's approach to allies.

Per Ed Luce (Financial Times): "Our definition of an adult is way way lower than it was in 1.0... Scott Bessent has I think very undeservedly been defined by many as the adult in the room. In fact, he's just another part of the Greek chorus that is doing so much damage to relations with allies."

### On Iran — Operation Economic Fury announcement (April 14, 2026)

At the WSJ CEO Council, Bessent publicly branded the economic side of the Iran campaign as Operation Economic Fury. Key claims:

- The maximum pressure campaign has been running since March 2025 on presidential order. By December 2025, "one of their major banks going under" forced the [[Central Bank of Iran]] to print money → large inflation → street riots (see [[Iran Protests 2025-2026]]).
- Gulf partners ([[UAE]], [[Qatar]], [[Saudi Arabia]]) now willing to share Iranian regime bank account information held in their jurisdictions — a reversal from prior tolerance. Bessent framed this as consequence of ~40 days of Iranian missile fire on Gulf territories. He cited Qatar losing 14% of GDP and UAE absorbing "over 1,200 hits."
- Treasury "reaching out to banks including Chinese banks" to press for [[Iran Financial Sanctions|secondary sanctions]] compliance on dollar-settled Iranian oil purchases.

Significance: regional willingness to cooperate financially against Iran is a step-function change from prior posture. The military campaign ([[2026 Iran conflict|Operation Epic Fury]]) and the economic campaign (Operation Economic Fury) now run as distinct but parallel tracks. Bessent is the public face of the financial lever.

### On China — derisk not decouple

Bessent explicitly rejected "decouple" as the framing for [[US-China]] strategy: "I think the word decouple is wrong. I think derisk is a better word." Strategic sectors flagged for reshoring: [[Semiconductors]], shipbuilding, rare earths, pharmaceutical precursors (cited 80-90% of US medicine precursors come from [[India]] and [[China]]).

On the May summit: assessed the Busan agreement at "B+ A-minus on the Chinese side." Floated a "30 by 30 or 40 by 40 billion" mutual tariff-free purchase deal. Expects Chinese appetite for American oil and LNG given the Gulf chokepoint experience.

On Chinese imbalances: "Very tough for the world to have a China with a trillion dollar trade surplus." Estimated Chinese aggregate budget deficit at ~10% of GDP. Japan analogy on adjustment pace: 20 years from 1990 bubble peak to Abe reforms in 2012; "the Asian mentality is a bit slower moving than ours. Chinese are probably even slower than the Japanese."

### On the dollar and RMB

Dismissed the China-Iran reserve-currency effort as "coordination that doesn't exist." On RMB as potential reserve currency, offered a three-equilibria diagnosis (academic model / internal capital flight / expropriation tail risk) arguing the RMB's equilibrium price is unknowable as long as capital controls persist and "1.4 billion people want to get their money out of the country." See [[Dollar Dominance]].

### On the Fed transition

Said he is "confident that Kevin will be in situ by" mid-May. On [[Tom Tillis]] holdout: "Senator Tillis believes that Kevin Warsh is an excellent candidate" — but no vote commitment. On chair pro tem options if Warsh is not confirmed in time: Vice Chair [[Philip Jefferson]] or Governor Chris Waller "could be" — added that Powell remaining as chair pro tem is "unclear." "I think the president of the United States probably has a preference." Relevant to [[Federal Reserve Independence]].

### On the US-China AI gap

Bessent strongly disputed that the US AI lead is gone: "3 to 6 months ahead." Cited the Anthropic Claude Mythos model as a "step function change" to argue that learning improvements compound logarithmically and are hard to catch. US compute share: "in the 30s" historically, "in the 50s" now, 70-80% projected in coming years. TSMC dependence: "90% of the high value chips are still made on the island of [[Taiwan]]." On the Biden [[CHIPS Act]]: "No money went out the door. Nothing was created."
