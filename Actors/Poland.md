# Poland

## Overview
Central European power and NATO's eastern anchor. Largest military buildup in Europe, driven by existential threat perception from Russia. First NATO member to fire shots in Ukraine conflict (September 2025 drone incident). Leading advocate for robust Ukraine support and alliance defense spending.

## Key Facts
- **Capital**: Warsaw
- **Population**: 38 million (EU's 5th largest)
- **GDP**: ~$800 billion
- **Government**: Parliamentary republic
- **President**: Andrzej Duda (until 2025)
- **PM**: Donald Tusk (2023-)
- **NATO Member**: 1999
- **EU Member**: 2004

## Strategic Position

### Geography
- Eastern flank of NATO
- Borders Russia (Kaliningrad), Belarus, Ukraine
- Suwalki Gap vulnerability
- Historic invasion corridor

### Threat Perception
- Existential view of Russian threat
- Historical trauma (partitions, WWII, Soviet era)
- Ukraine war on doorstep
- Belarus as Russian proxy

## Military

### Massive Buildup
- Defense spending: **4%+ GDP** (NATO's highest ratio)
- Target: Largest land army in Europe
- $140 billion modernization program
- 300,000 active troops goal

### NATO Role
- Major US troop presence
- Logistics hub for Ukraine aid
- Exercises (Defender Europe)
- Forward defense advocate

## September 2025 Drone Incident

### What Happened
- Russian drones crossed into Poland during Ukraine strikes
- **Poland shot them down** - first NATO member to fire in conflict
- Warsaw denounced as "act of aggression"

### Response
- **Article 4 invoked** - NATO consultations
- Did not escalate to Article 5
- Tests Putin's red line probing
- Demonstrated willingness to defend airspace

### Implications
- Showed NATO spillover risks
- Poland's readiness to act
- Escalation management challenge
- Precedent for future incidents
