# People's Liberation Army

## Overview
The armed wing of the Chinese Communist Party and the central military instrument of [[China]]'s great-power strategy. The PLA is commanded through the Central Military Commission chaired by [[Xi Jinping]], not through a state-civilian chain of command in the Western sense.

## Strategic Role
- Defend CCP rule and territorial claims
- Prepare for Taiwan contingencies and US intervention
- Build coercive options below the threshold of war
- Integrate space, cyber, information, economic, and civilian systems into war planning

## May 2026: total war and systems confrontation

Howard Wang's 2026 RAND / Journal of Contemporary China work, discussed with [[Manoj Kewalramani]] on *The Great Power Show*, frames the PLA's current war concept around "systems confrontation." The PLA does not imagine modern war as military-versus-military alone. It imagines national system-versus-national system: finance, space, cyber, public messaging, industrial base, logistics, technology platforms, and alliance cohesion all become part of the fight.

The key claim is that China embedded "total war" into the 2021-2025 National Security Strategy and then carried it into a 2024 update of the PLA's military strategic guidelines. In this usage, total war does not mean unlimited destruction or regime-change war. It means peacetime mobilization: civilian institutions should be built so party leaders can turn them into warfighting advantage during conflict.

## Escalation control

Wang's useful distinction is that Chinese assertiveness and risk aversion are not opposites. The point of proactive pressure is to seize initiative, shape the battlefield, control crisis, and contain escalation. Ladakh is the example: cold weapons, no firearms, limited publicity around casualties, and a frozen negotiation process all created pressure while limiting the risk of rapid escalation. The same period allowed China to build roads and ISR-supporting infrastructure closer to the border.

## PLA purges and readiness

Wang reads the 2024-2026 PLA purges primarily as real corruption enforcement rather than factional infighting or simple pre-21st Party Congress consolidation. The likely core is strategic-level equipment and Rocket Force corruption. If even part of the reporting about missile quality and silo reliability is true, the problem touches nuclear deterrence credibility, not ordinary procurement leakage.

Short term, the purges likely damage expertise and readiness. Long term, a less corrupt PLA is more capable. The unresolved question is whether Xi's repeated campaign tools changed the incentive structure or merely intensified punishment after the same system reproduced corruption.

## Rocket Force silo-field defense (May 2026)

Reuters' May 2026 launch-pad imagery points to the PLA protecting the fixed side of the nuclear force. If the pads are for air-defense systems, the Rocket Force problem is no longer only missile count or silo reliability; it is integrated defense of the silo fields against conventional precision strike.
