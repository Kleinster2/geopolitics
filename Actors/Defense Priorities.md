# Defense Priorities

## Overview
Restraint-oriented think tank advocating for a more limited US foreign policy focused on core national security interests. Argues against military interventionism, forward deployment in Europe, and values-based foreign policy.

## Key Facts
- **Founded**: 2016
- **Location**: Washington, DC
- **Orientation**: Restraint / non-interventionism
- **Focus**: Military policy, defense spending, grand strategy

## Core Positions

### On US Force Posture
- US should pull back from Europe
- Europe is rich and can defend itself
- Forward deployment increases risk without proportionate benefit
- "Pivot home" rather than pivot to Asia

### On Threats
- Russia is **not** a major threat to the United States
- China is the most proximate challenge, primarily economic
- Threats in Western Hemisphere are law enforcement issues (drugs, cartels), not military

### On Values
- Values-based foreign policy does not serve US interests
- "Might makes right" has always been trueâ€”not new under Trump
- Rejects idea that US post-1945 order was fundamentally different

### On Military Power
- Opposes use of military for law enforcement challenges
- Critical of operations like Venezuela intervention
- Prefers restraint even when intervention might "succeed"

## Key Figures

### Jennifer Kavanagh
- **Position**: Senior Fellow, Director of Military Analysis
- **Background**: RAND Corporation
- **Views**: Restrainer, skeptical of European commitment, critical of military intervention for non-military threats
