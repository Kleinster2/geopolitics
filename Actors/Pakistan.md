# Pakistan

## Overview
Nuclear-armed state with history of military rule, defined by rivalry with India and strategic partnership with China. Faces chronic instability, terrorism challenges, and economic crises while maintaining significant military capabilities.

## Diplomatic Pivot (2025-2026)

Per [[Sushant Singh]] (Yale, April 2026): Pakistan has executed one of the most consequential reversals of its diplomatic posture in a generation. Despite economic distress, [[Imran Khan]] imprisoned, active border conflict with Afghanistan, and major terrorism crises in Balochistan and Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, [[Asim Munir|Field Marshal Asim Munir]] has positioned Islamabad as the central [[Donald Trump|Trump]]-Iran broker.

- White House meeting (June 2025) between Trump and Munir without civilian Pakistani leader — unprecedented for US president receiving non-political military ruler
- Hosted [[JD Vance|Vance]]-led delegation in Islamabad for US-Iran talks (March/April 2026); 21-hour negotiation collapsed April 10-11 but mediation continues
- Engagement with [[China]], Saudi Arabia, [[Iran]] simultaneously; only Israel, India, UAE not in dialogue
- Trump publicly calls Munir "my favorite Field Marshal"
- [[World Bank]] reclassified Pakistan from South Asia to MENA / Afghanistan-Pakistan group (April 2026), reinforcing Pakistan's identity pivot toward West Asian Islamic networks
- Continues Pakistani military leadership tradition of high-risk diplomatic plays (Yahya for Nixon-Mao 1970, Zia on Afghanistan, Musharraf post-9/11)

See [[Pakistan as US-Iran Mediator]] for full analysis. The position is structurally fragile — entirely dependent on Trump's personal favor — but in Singh's framing has demolished a decade of [[Narendra Modi|Modi]]'s isolation strategy.
