# Pakistan

## Overview
Nuclear-armed state with history of military rule, defined by rivalry with India and strategic partnership with China. Faces chronic instability, terrorism challenges, and economic crises while maintaining significant military capabilities.

## Key Facts
- **Capital**: Islamabad
- **Population**: ~230 million (5th largest)
- **GDP**: ~$350 billion
- **Nuclear Warheads**: ~170
- **Military**: Powerful political actor
- **Government**: Civilian (military influence)

## Geopolitical Position

### Defining Features
- India rivalry (existential framing)
- China "all-weather" partnership
- US relationship (transactional, strained)
- Afghanistan entanglement
- Nuclear deterrent priority

### Strategic Depth Doctrine
- Afghanistan as buffer against India
- Taliban relationship
- Now complicated by TTP blowback

## Strategic Priorities
1. India deterrence
2. Kashmir cause
3. China partnership
4. Economic survival
5. Afghanistan stability (selective)
6. Counter-terrorism (selective)
