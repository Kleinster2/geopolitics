# MBDA

## Overview
Europe's only fully integrated missile systems company. Joint venture combining British, French, Italian, and German missile capabilities. Produces air defense, anti-ship, and strike missiles. Key to European defense autonomy in guided weapons.

## Key Facts
- **Headquarters**: Paris, France (with major UK presence)
- **Founded**: 2001 (merger of European missile firms)
- **Employees**: ~15,000

- **Ownership**: Airbus (37.5%), BAE Systems (37.5%), Leonardo (25%)

## Core Products

### Air Defense
- **ASTER** family (SAMP/T system) - Medium/long range
- **CAMM** / Sea Ceptor - Short/medium range
- **Mistral** - MANPADS (man-portable)

### Air-to-Air
- **Meteor** - Beyond visual range (best in class)
- **MICA** - Short/medium range
- **ASRAAM** - Short range (UK)

### Strike/Cruise
- **Storm Shadow/SCALP** - Air-launched cruise missile
- **Exocet** - Anti-ship missile (legacy)
- **Brimstone** - Precision strike

### Anti-Ship
- **Exocet** - Classic anti-ship missile
- **MARTE** - Italian anti-ship
- **Sea Venom/ANL** - Helicopter-launched

## Strategic Significance

### European Capability
- Only European company covering full missile spectrum
- Reduces dependence on US (Raytheon, Lockheed)
- Enables European strategic autonomy
- Cross-border industrial integration model

### Ukraine War Role
- Storm Shadow/SCALP missiles to Ukraine
- Major long-range strike capability for Kyiv
- Expanding production for NATO stockpiles

### Air Defense Gap
- ASTER/SAMP/T for European air defense
- Part of NATO's layered defense concept
- Alternative to US Patriot system

## European Defense Model
- Shows successful cross-border integration
- Overcame national champion tendencies
- Model for other sectors?
- But still complexity in four-nation structure

## Challenges
- Scaling production for wartime demand
- US competition (especially Raytheon)
- National preference tensions
- Export policy coordination
