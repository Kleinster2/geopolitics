# South Korea (Republic of Korea)

## Overview
Technologically advanced democracy and major US ally facing existential threat from nuclear-armed North Korea. Economic powerhouse (Samsung, Hyundai) punching above its weight globally. Increasingly important in US-China competition and Indo-Pacific security.

## Key Facts
- **Capital**: Seoul (metro: 26 million)
- **Population**: ~52 million
- **GDP**: ~$1.7 trillion (12th largest)
- **Military Budget**: ~$50 billion
- **President**: Head of state and government
- **Unique**: Technically still at war with North Korea

## Geopolitical Position

### Strategic Situation
- Frontline state vs. North Korea
- US alliance cornerstone
- China economic dependence
- Japan complicated neighbor
- Rising middle power ambitions

### The Korea Dilemma
Caught between:
- Security (US alliance)
- Economy (China trade)
- History (Japan relations)
- Existential threat (North Korea)

## Security Environment

### North Korea Threat
- Nuclear weapons pointed at Seoul
- Massive artillery (Seoul within range)
- Missile development accelerating
- 28,500 US troops in Korea
- Combined Forces Command

### US Alliance
- Mutual Defense Treaty (1953)
- Extended nuclear deterrence
- Annual exercises
- USFK presence
- Wartime operational control (transition ongoing)

### Nuclear Debate
- Growing public support for own weapons
- Extended deterrence doubts
- Washington Declaration (2023)
- Nuclear Consultative Group
- NPT constraints

## Key Relationships

- Largest trading partner
