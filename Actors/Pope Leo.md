# Pope Leo

First American Pope. Ascended to the papacy in 2025 (as Leo XIV). Has become an increasingly outspoken moral voice on the [[2026 Iran conflict]], positioning the Vatican as a religious counterweight to the US-Israeli war effort.

## Intervention on the 2026 Iran war

### April 11, 2026 — Peace vigil at St. Peter's Basilica

Hosted a peace vigil on the evening of Saturday April 11, timed to the Pakistan-mediated US-Iran [[Islamabad talks]]. Basilica packed with thousands of worshippers; hundreds more outside the square following on big screens.

Key rhetorical moves (per CNN Vatican correspondent Christopher Lamb):

- Appealed for an "end to the madness of war"
- Called on leaders to "get around the negotiating table" to "avoid the delusion of omnipotence"
- Implicit rebuke of the US-Israeli framing that the war had been "won"

### April 12 — Sunday prayer

From St. Peter's noon-time prayer, appealed for a ceasefire in [[Lebanon]], citing the need to protect the civilian population — directly addressed to Israeli ongoing strikes on Hezbollah targets and Beirut suburbs.

### Religious-moral framing

Separately denounced "the use of God's name to justify the joint US-Israeli war on Iran." This is a pointed theological critique — it addresses not just the war's conduct but its invocation of religious legitimacy.

## Africa trip (April 13-22, 2026)

10-day trip to four African countries, starting in [[Algeria]] (Muslim-majority). Announced and departing one day after the Islamabad talks collapsed. The itinerary positions the Pope as seeking interfaith dialogue with the Muslim world precisely as the US is announcing a [[Strait of Hormuz]] naval blockade.

## Positioning as "moral voice"

Christopher Lamb (CNN): Leo "emerging as a moral voice on the world stage" during an "uncertain time." Lamb also reported a pre-Easter interview in which Leo said he "hopes President Trump can find an off-ramp to end the conflict."

Significance:
- First American Pope — his criticism of US policy has added resonance it would not have from a European pontiff
- Catholic Church historically tries to avoid direct confrontation with Washington; Leo's explicit framing ("madness," "delusion of omnipotence") marks a shift
- Compatible with [[Anthony Blinken]]'s "tactical success, strategic failure" critique and [[Ian Bremmer]]'s "worst foreign policy mistake" framing, but from a religious/moral rather than diplomatic/analytical register
- Complicates the domestic US political picture for an administration relying on Christian conservative support
