# ASEAN (Association of Southeast Asian Nations)

## Overview
Southeast Asia's primary regional organization, emphasizing consensus, non-interference, and economic integration. Navigates great power competition while maintaining "ASEAN centrality."

## Key Facts
- **Founded**: August 8, 1967 (Bangkok Declaration)
- **Members**: 10 (Brunei, Cambodia, Indonesia, Laos, Malaysia, Myanmar, Philippines, Singapore, Thailand, Vietnam)
- **Population**: ~680 million
- **Combined GDP**: ~$3.6 trillion
- **Headquarters**: Jakarta, Indonesia

## Core Principles ("ASEAN Way")
1. **Non-interference** in internal affairs
2. **Consensus decision-making**
3. **No use of force**
4. **Peaceful dispute settlement**
5. **Respect for sovereignty**

## Institutional Structure

### Summits
- ASEAN Summit (leaders)
- ASEAN Plus dialogues
- East Asia Summit (EAS)
- ASEAN Regional Forum (ARF)

### ASEAN-led Forums
- **ARF**: Security dialogue (27 members)
- **EAS**: Strategic forum (18 members)
- **ADMM+**: Defense ministers

## Economic Integration

### ASEAN Economic Community (AEC)
- Free flow of goods, services, investment
- Single market aspiration
- Implementation challenges

### Trade Agreements
- RCEP (includes China)
- ASEAN-China FTA
- ASEAN-India FTA
- Various bilateral FTAs

## Strategic Positioning

### ASEAN Centrality
- Hub of regional architecture
- Convening power
- Balancing great powers
- "Driver's seat" aspiration

### Great Power Management
- Engage all major powers
- Avoid choosing sides
- Benefit from competition
- Risk of being marginalized

## South China Sea Dynamics
- Multiple ASEAN members are claimants
- China's assertiveness challenges unity
- Code of Conduct negotiations (stalled)
- 2016 arbitration ruling (rejected by China)

## Challenges
- Consensus paralysis
- Myanmar crisis response
- China's influence (divide and rule)
- Capability gaps
- Relevance questions
