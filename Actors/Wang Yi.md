# Wang Yi

Wang Yi is China's foreign minister and senior party-state foreign-affairs official: a member of the CPC Politburo, director of the Office of the Central Commission for Foreign Affairs, and Minister of Foreign Affairs. His speeches are useful evidence for [[Chinese Foreign Policy]] because they come from both the public foreign-ministry channel and the party foreign-affairs apparatus.

## May 2026 UN Security Council presidency

On May 26, 2026, Wang chaired a [[United Nations]] Security Council high-level meeting under China's May presidency on "Upholding the Purposes and Principles of the U.N. Charter and Strengthening the U.N.-Centered International System." The Ministry of Foreign Affairs account says representatives from over 100 countries attended, including more than 20 foreign ministers or high-level representatives.

The speech was a compact statement of Beijing's order strategy. Wang framed the UN Charter around sovereign equality, non-interference, peaceful settlement of disputes, opposition to unilateral military action, opposition to unilateral sanctions beyond Security Council resolutions, more developing-country representation at the IMF and World Bank, and UN-centered governance for AI, outer space, polar regions, and cyberspace.

That makes the speech important beyond UN procedure. China is not presenting itself as anti-institutional; it is trying to make existing institutions carry Chinese definitions of legitimacy, especially sovereignty, development, and opposition to Western sanctions practice.
