# Mercosur

## Overview
Southern Common Market (Mercado Común del Sur). South American trade bloc and customs union. Fourth-largest trading bloc globally after EU, USMCA, and ASEAN.

## Key Facts
- **Founded**: 1991 (Treaty of Asunción)
- **Full members**: 5 (Argentina, Brazil, Paraguay, Uruguay, Bolivia)
- **Associate members**: Chile, Colombia, Ecuador, Guyana, Peru, Suriname
- **Combined GDP**: ~$2.4 trillion
- **Population**: ~300 million
- **Headquarters**: Montevideo, Uruguay

### Associate Members
Chile, Colombia, Ecuador, Guyana, Peru, Suriname - participate in free trade area but not customs union.

## Structure

### Institutions
- **Common Market Council (CMC)** - Highest decision-making body
- **Common Market Group (GMC)** - Executive body
- **Mercosur Parliament (Parlasur)** - Advisory legislative body
- **Trade Commission** - Trade policy coordination
- **Structural Convergence Fund (FOCEM)** - Development assistance for smaller members

### Decision-Making
Consensus required for all decisions. No supranational authority (unlike EU).

## Economic Integration

### Customs Union
- Common external tariff (CET) on imports from non-members
- Free movement of goods within bloc
- Exceptions and perforations remain (incomplete union)

### Limitations
- Numerous exceptions to common tariff
- Bilateral deals with outside partners contentious
- Brazil's economic dominance creates asymmetries
- Recurring trade disputes between members

## Strategic Significance

### Regional Integration
- Largest South American economic bloc
- Platform for regional coordination
- Reduces dependence on US/European markets

### Global Trade Context
- Alternative to US-centric trade arrangements
- Counterweight to Pacific Alliance (Chile, Colombia, Mexico, Peru)
- Potential partner for EU, China in diversification strategies

### Challenges
- Brazilian economic dominance
- Argentine economic instability
- Protectionist tendencies within bloc
- Competition from Pacific Alliance model

## Political Dynamics

### Left-Right Oscillation
Bloc cohesion varies with political alignment of member governments:
- **Left-leaning period** (2000s): Deeper integration push
- **Right-leaning period** (2015-2019): More open to external deals
- **Current mix**: Milei (Argentina) vs. Lula (Brazil) creates tensions

### Venezuela Suspension (2016)
Suspended for violating democratic clause. Demonstrates bloc's (limited) political conditionality.
