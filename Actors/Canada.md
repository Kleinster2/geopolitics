# Canada

## Overview
G7 member and close US ally with world's second-largest landmass. Extensive Arctic territory including disputed Northwest Passage. Five Eyes intelligence partner. Facing increasing pressure on defense spending and Arctic sovereignty as great power competition intensifies in the High North.

## Key Facts
- **Capital**: Ottawa
- **Population**: 40 million
- **GDP**: ~$2.1 trillion
- **Government**: Federal parliamentary constitutional monarchy
- **Monarch**: King Charles III
- **PM**: Mark Carney (2025-)
- **NATO Member**: Founding (1949)

## Strategic Position

### Geography
- Second-largest country by area
- Three ocean coastlines
- US border (world's longest)
- Vast Arctic territory

### Arctic
- Second-largest Arctic coastline (after Russia)
- Northwest Passage sovereignty claims
- Disputed by US (international strait vs. internal waters)
- Climate change opening new routes
- Resource potential

## Military

### Current State
- **Active Personnel**: ~68,000
- **Defense Spending**: ~1.4% GDP (below NATO 2% target)
- Under pressure to increase spending

### Key Assets
- CF-18 Hornets (F-35 on order)
- Halifax-class frigates
- Victoria-class submarines
- Arctic patrol vessels (new)

### NORAD
- Joint US-Canada aerospace defense
- North American airspace protection
- Early warning systems
- Modernization underway

### Challenges
- Underfunding chronic
- Equipment aging
- Arctic presence limited
- Recruitment difficulties
