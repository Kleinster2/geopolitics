# India

## Overview
World's most populous country and fastest-growing major economy. Pursues "strategic autonomy" while tilting toward US amid China tensions. Key swing state in great power competition.

## Key Facts
- **Capital**: New Delhi
- **Population**: ~1.4 billion (world's largest)
- **GDP**: ~$3.7 trillion (5th largest)
- **Military Budget**: ~$80 billion
- **Nuclear Warheads**: ~170
- **Leader**: Prime Minister Narendra Modi (BJP, since 2014)

## Geopolitical Position

### Strategic Autonomy
Traditional non-alignment evolved into multi-vector engagement:
- US defense partnership deepening
- Russia arms dependence (historical)
- China rivalry intensifying
- Global South leadership aspirations

### Regional Dominance
- Dominant South Asian power
- Indian Ocean primacy ambitions
- Neighborhood first policy
- Challenges: Pakistan, China encirclement

## Strategic Priorities
1. China border security (LAC)
2. Counter Pakistan terrorism
3. Indian Ocean dominance
4. Economic development
5. Technology self-reliance
6. Great power recognition

## Key Relationships

### Partners
- [[United States]] - Quad, defense agreements (BECA, LEMOA)
- [[Japan]] - Strategic partnership, investments
- [[Australia]] - Quad, defense ties
- [[France]] - Defense, strategic autonomy affinity
- [[Russia]] - Historical ties, arms dependency (declining)

### Rivals
- [[China]] - Border disputes, strategic competition
- [[Pakistan]] - Kashmir, terrorism, nuclear standoff

## Territorial Disputes
- **LAC with China**: Aksai Chin, Arunachal Pradesh
- **Kashmir**: Divided with Pakistan, claimed in full
- **Siachen Glacier**: World's highest battlefield

## Strengths
- Demographic dividend (young population)
- IT and services sector
- Large military
- Nuclear deterrent
- Democracy (legitimacy)
- Diaspora influence

## Vulnerabilities
- Infrastructure gaps
- Poverty and inequality
- Two-front threat (China + Pakistan)
- Internal divisions (religious, caste)
- Bureaucratic inefficiency

## Defense Posture
- No first use nuclear doctrine
- Army-centric force structure
- Indigenization push (Atmanirbhar Bharat)
- Major arms importer (diversifying sources)

## Economic Trajectory
- ~7% annual growth
- Manufacturing push ("Make in India")
- Digital transformation
- Still significant poverty
- Supply chain alternative to China
