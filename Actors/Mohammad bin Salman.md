# Mohammad bin Salman

## Overview
Crown Prince and Prime Minister of Saudi Arabia, de facto ruler. Known as "MBS." Pursuing ambitious modernization (Vision 2030) while consolidating authoritarian control. Key figure in [[Abraham Accords]] expansion efforts and Saudi-Iran dynamics. Controversial for Khashoggi assassination, Yemen war, but essential partner for US regional strategy.

## Key Facts
- **Born**: August 31, 1985
- **Position**: Crown Prince (2017-), Prime Minister (2022-)
- **Father**: King Salman (reigns since 2015)
- **Previous**: Defense Minister (2015-), Deputy Crown Prince (2015-2017)

## Domestic Policy

### Vision 2030
Economic diversification program:
- Reduce oil dependence
- NEOM mega-city project
- Entertainment sector opening
- Tourism development
- Women's rights reforms (driving, employment)
- Private sector growth

### Social Reforms
- Cinema, concerts, entertainment allowed
- Women permitted to drive (2018)
- Reduced religious police powers
- Mixed-gender events
- But: No political liberalization

### Political Consolidation
- Ritz-Carlton "anti-corruption" purge (2017)
- Rivals sidelined or arrested
- Centralized decision-making
- Replaced elderly leadership with loyalists

## Foreign Policy

### Iran Rivalry
- Principal Saudi-Iran antagonism
- Yemen intervention (2015-)
- Regional proxy competition
- But: China-brokered rapprochement (2023)

### US Relations
- Strained under Biden (Khashoggi, oil)
- Warmer under Trump 2.0
- Defense relationship essential
- Abraham Accords normalization conditional
- Nuclear and defense guarantees sought

### Israel Normalization
- Potential [[Abraham Accords]] expansion
- Conditions: Palestinian state path, US defense treaty, nuclear cooperation
- October 7 complicated but didn't end talks
- Prize for regional realignment

### China Relations
- Growing economic ties
- Yuan oil sales
- Technology partnerships
- Hedging against US

### Yemen War
- Saudi-led intervention (2015-)
- Humanitarian catastrophe
- Military stalemate
- Ceasefire efforts ongoing

## Controversies

### Khashoggi Assassination (2018)
- Washington Post journalist killed in Istanbul consulate
- US intelligence: MBS ordered operation
- International condemnation
- Biden initially isolated him, later re-engaged

### Yemen
- Civilian casualties
- Blockade and famine
- War crimes allegations

### Repression
- Activist imprisonments
- Women's rights activists jailed despite reforms
- No political opposition tolerated

## Key Characteristics

### Leadership Style
- Bold, risk-taking
- Centralized control
- Modernization + authoritarianism
- Generational change in Saudi leadership

### Assets
- Oil wealth
- Youth demographic
- Strategic location
- Mecca/Medina custodianship

### Vulnerabilities
- Oil dependence despite diversification
- Yemen quagmire
- Reputation (Khashoggi)
- Succession stability
