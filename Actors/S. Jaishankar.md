# S. Jaishankar

## Overview
Subrahmanyam Jaishankar — External Affairs Minister of [[India]] since May 2019. Career diplomat turned political appointee under [[Narendra Modi]]. Architect of India's "multi-alignment" foreign policy and a primary public-facing voice for the Modi government's claim that India has achieved great-power status. The Pakistan-mediator turn during the [[2026 Iran conflict]] has put his framework — and his composure — under unprecedented strain.

## Career
- Indian Foreign Service career officer
- Ambassador to [[China]] (2009-2013) — longest-serving Indian envoy to Beijing
- Ambassador to the [[United States]] (2013-2015)
- Foreign Secretary (2015-2018)
- Joined BJP after retirement; appointed External Affairs Minister May 2019
- Rajya Sabha member (Gujarat)
- Author, *The India Way* (2020) and *Why Bharat Matters* (2024)

## Foreign-Policy Posture
- "Multi-alignment" — simultaneous strategic partnerships with US, Russia, Israel, Gulf states; engagement with China
- Public posture: India is a confident, indispensable pole in a multipolar world
- Sharp, often combative debating style at Munich, Davos, Doha, Raisina
- Known for one-liners directed at European interlocutors ("Europe must grow out of the mindset that Europe's problems are the world's problems")

## The "Dalal" Comment (April 2026)

In an all-party meeting in Delhi (April 2026), Indian opposition politicians pressed Jaishankar on India's apparent diplomatic marginalization during the [[2026 Iran conflict]] — specifically why [[Pakistan]] had become the [[Donald Trump|Trump]]-Iran broker while India was "missing from all this." Jaishankar responded that Pakistan has a "history of being a *dalal* country."

### What "dalal" means
Per [[Sushant Singh]]: in Hindi/Urdu, *dalal* literally means "broker" or "mediator," but in colloquial usage carries the strongly degrading connotation of "pimp." Jaishankar's intent, per Singh, was the more pejorative reading — a snub.

### What it revealed
Singh frames the comment as simultaneously:
1. **Frustration** — Modi's decade-long policy of isolating Pakistan has unraveled in twelve months
2. **Damage control** — domestic Indian audience needed a put-down to rationalize being bypassed
3. **Strategic miscalculation** — labeling a country with active Trump access as a "pimp" risks further marginalizing India

A Pakistani government figure reportedly responded on social media that India was "acting as a *dalal* for [[Benjamin Netanyahu]]" — a calibrated counter-snub that linked the term back to the deepening [[India]]-[[Israel]] alignment.

### Why it matters
Per Singh: "A lot of [Modi's] domestic standing depends on the fact that he, his supporters and the Indian people are being told that he is this great world leader. So this becomes a particularly embarrassing fact politically." The MEA — represented by Jaishankar — is being asked to defend a strategic posture that has lost its primary external backer (the [[Quad]]) while its main rival has acquired one.

## On the US Relationship
Jaishankar publicly maintains the Modi-Trump partnership remains strong, but the operational signals diverge:
- India received among the highest US tariffs under Trump 2.0
- Modi has actively avoided shared multilateral venues to avoid Trump publicly claiming credit for the [[India-Pakistan Four-Day War (May 2025)|May 2025 ceasefire]]
- Jaishankar's task: salvage and stall through Trump's term, with Indian elite hope that midterm Democratic gains restore some balance

## Strategic Themes
- Strategic autonomy and "civilizational state" framing of India's foreign policy
- Defense of India's continued Russian energy purchases and arms imports
- Hard line on China while pursuing parallel commercial normalization
- Hindu-nationalist framing of foreign-policy success as Modi's personal achievement
