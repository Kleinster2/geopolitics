# The Soufan Center

## Overview

Nonpartisan nonprofit research organization founded in 2017 by Ali Soufan, a former FBI Supervisory Special Agent known for investigating the East Africa embassy bombings, USS Cole attack, and 9/11. Headquartered in New York (156 West 56th Street). Produces independent analysis on global security challenges — counterterrorism, violent extremism, armed conflict, and foreign policy. Distinct from The Soufan Group, Ali Soufan's for-profit intelligence consultancy founded in 2005.

## Key facts

- **Founded**: 2017
- **Founder/Chairman**: Ali Soufan

## Leadership

### Ali Soufan
Born in Beirut (1971). FBI Supervisory Special Agent who investigated major al-Qaeda operations. Left FBI in 2005, founded The Soufan Group (private intelligence firm). Published "The Black Banners: The Inside Story of 9/11 and the War Against al-Qaeda" (2011) and "Anatomy of Terror" (2017). Special Adviser on Investigations for the International Criminal Court. Kennedy Award (2018), Carnegie Corporation Great Immigrant Award (2023).

### Colin P. Clarke
Ph.D., previously Director of Research at The Soufan Group, senior political scientist at [[RAND Corporation]] (decade of terrorism/insurgency research), professor at Carnegie Mellon.

## Research programs

### September 10th Project
Addresses the "failure of imagination" cited by the 9/11 Commission. Publishes threat reports, expert briefs. Hosts the annual Global Summit on Terrorism and Political Violence (GSTPV) in New York.

### Foreign fighters research
Tracking recruitment, radicalization, and returnee threats. Foundational report: "Beyond the Caliphate: Foreign Fighters and the Threat of Returnees" (October 2017).

### Violent far-right extremism outlook
Research on far-right narratives, propaganda networks, antisemitism, Islamophobia, and transnational movements.

### Violent Non-State Actor (VNSA) snapshots
Concise organizational profiles of terrorist and armed groups — background, leadership, objectives, threat assessment.

### Securing the Future Initiative
Independent review of UN Security Council counterterrorism activities over two decades.

## Publications

### IntelBriefs
Daily analytical bulletins covering security and geopolitical developments. 233 published in 2023 alone. Covers individual country analyses and thematic issues (terrorism, migration, climate, justice, conflict).

### Major reports
- Foreign fighters in Syria series (ongoing since 2014)
- "Beyond the Caliphate" (2017)
- IS-Somalia assessments
- Wagner Group/Africa Corps operations
- Terrorism trends analysis

## Analytical approach

Three-pillar model: Inform (rigorous research), Inspire (events and strategic dialogue), Impact (policy change). Emphasizes independence from consensus — "We do not follow the consensus; we set the agenda." Integrates human rights and human security perspectives as foundational to sustainable solutions. Prevention-focused.
