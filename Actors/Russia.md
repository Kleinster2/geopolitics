# Russia

## Overview
Largest country by territory, wielding significant military power (largest nuclear arsenal) but modest economic weight. Under Putin, pursues restoration of great power status and resistance to Western/NATO expansion.

## Key Facts
- **Capital**: Moscow
- **Population**: ~144 million
- **GDP**: ~$2.2 trillion
- **Military Budget**: ~$100 billion (significant recent increases)
- **Nuclear Warheads**: ~5,900 (world's largest)
- **Leader**: Vladimir Putin (President since 2000, with interruption)

## Geopolitical Position

### Strategic Worldview
- Post-Cold War settlement was unjust humiliation
- NATO expansion threatens Russian security
- Russia deserves sphere of influence in "near abroad"
- Multipolar world preferable to US hegemony

### Key Objectives
1. Prevent NATO/EU expansion to Ukraine, Georgia
2. Maintain influence over former Soviet states
3. Great power status recognition
4. Regime security
5. Undermine Western unity

## Military Capabilities
- World's largest nuclear arsenal
- Significant conventional forces
- Modernized strategic systems
- Cyber/hybrid warfare capabilities
- Wagner Group and proxy forces

## Economic Factors
- Energy exports (oil, gas) primary revenue
- Sanctioned economy since 2014, intensified 2022
- Resource-rich but undiversified
- Brain drain and capital flight

## Regional Influence
- **CIS/CSTO**: Formal alliance structures
- **Belarus**: Close integration
- **Central Asia**: Security presence
- **Caucasus**: Armenia-Azerbaijan mediation

## Key Relationships

### Partners
- [[China]] (strategic partnership, junior partner)
- [[Iran]] (defense cooperation)
- [[North Korea]] (renewed ties)
- [[Belarus]] (union state)

### Adversaries
- [[NATO]]/[[United States]]/[[European Union]]
- [[Ukraine]] (active conflict)

## Strengths
- Nuclear deterrent
- Energy resources
- Military-industrial base
- Veto power (UN Security Council)
- Willingness to use force

## Vulnerabilities
- Economic weakness and sanctions
- Demographics (declining population)
- Dependence on China
- Brain drain
- Military losses and equipment depletion

## Current Issues
- Ukraine war (since 2022)
- Western sanctions regime
- Relations with China
- Internal stability
- Military reconstitution
