# United Nations

## Overview
The world's primary international organization, founded to maintain peace, develop friendly relations, and promote human rights. Central to international law and diplomacy, though constrained by great power politics.

## Key Facts
- **Founded**: October 24, 1945 (UN Charter)
- **Members**: 193 member states
- **Headquarters**: New York City
- **Secretary-General**: (rotating leadership)
- **Budget**: ~$3 billion (regular) + peacekeeping

## Principal Organs

### Security Council
- **Function**: Peace and security, binding resolutions
- **Permanent 5 (P5)**: US, UK, France, Russia, China (veto power)
- **Non-permanent**: 10 elected members (2-year terms)
- **Challenge**: Veto paralyzes action on great power interests

### General Assembly
- **Function**: Deliberation, recommendations
- **Membership**: All 193 states (one vote each)
- **Sessions**: Annual regular session, special sessions
- **Resolutions**: Non-binding but politically significant

### Secretariat
- Led by Secretary-General
- International civil service
- Implements decisions, provides analysis

### International Court of Justice
- Principal judicial organ
- Located in The Hague
- Settles disputes between states
- Advisory opinions

### Economic and Social Council (ECOSOC)
- Coordinates economic/social work
- Oversees specialized agencies

### Trusteeship Council (Suspended)
- Administered trust territories
- Mission completed

## Key Functions

### Peace and Security
- Peacekeeping operations (~100,000 personnel)
- Conflict mediation
- Sanctions regimes
- Arms control treaties

### Human Rights
- Universal Declaration of Human Rights
- Human Rights Council
- Treaty bodies
- Special procedures

### Development
- Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)
- UNDP, UNICEF, WFP operations
- Technical assistance

### International Law
- Treaty repository
- International Law Commission
- Codification and development

## Limitations
- P5 veto blocks action on major conflicts
- No enforcement capacity without state consent
- Underfunded and overstretched
- Bureaucratic inefficiency
- Reform paralysis

## Reform Debates
- Security Council expansion (G4: Germany, Japan, India, Brazil)
- Veto limitation
- General Assembly empowerment
- Efficiency improvements
