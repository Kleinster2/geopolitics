# Joseph Votel

## Overview

Gen. Joseph L. Votel (US Army, retired) served as commander of both [[CENTCOM]] (2016-2019) and [[SOCOM]] (2014-2016), overseeing US military operations across the Middle East and global special operations. Now serves as Distinguished Military Fellow at the [[Middle East Institute]].

## Key Facts
- **Rank**: General (four-star), US Army, retired
- **Commands**: CENTCOM (2016-2019), SOCOM (2014-2016)
- **Current role**: Distinguished Military Fellow, [[Middle East Institute]]

## Significance

As former CENTCOM commander, Votel has direct operational experience with the theater, forces, and adversaries involved in the [[2026 Iran conflict]]. His assessments carry weight because of institutional knowledge of US force posture, allied capabilities, and Iranian military doctrine in the Persian Gulf region.

## Analysis — 2026 Iran conflict

### MEI briefing (March 8, 2026)

In a [[Middle East Institute|MEI]] virtual briefing moderated by [[Ken Pollack]], Votel assessed the military situation approximately one week into Operation Epic Fury:

**US military objectives — three thrusts:**
1. Paralyze Iranian command and control — opening strikes designed as decapitation
2. Destroy means of retaliation — 80-90% degraded per Admiral Cooper, but 10% still operational
3. Degrade regime's ability to control population — targeting expanded to provinces

**Key operational concerns:**
- **Mine warfare** is the biggest threat at the [[Strait of Hormuz]]. Iran has studied this for decades. US mine-sweeping capability is limited.
- **Munitions sustainability** — not just US stocks but all Gulf allies dependent on same US-supplied interceptor supply chain. "There's a lot of pressure on this system."
- **Force sustainability** — "There's no free chicken." Two carrier strike groups in theater were not projected to be there. Deferred maintenance costs accumulate.
- Binding constraints: munitions and American public patience.

**Three likely outcomes (ranked):**
1. Theocratic succession (most likely)
2. Military/IRGC-led takeover (plausible)
3. Popular opposition takeover (improbable)

**On unconventional warfare:** Building effective UW capability takes months to years, not weeks. Regime change is a political objective, not military.

**On Russia:** Providing targeting intelligence to Iran — "directly goes after protection of our forces."
