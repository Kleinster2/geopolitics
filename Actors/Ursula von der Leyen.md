# Ursula von der Leyen

## Overview
President of the [[European Commission]] since 2019, re-elected for second term in 2024. First woman to hold the position. Former German Defense Minister (2013-2019).

## Key Facts
- **Born**: 1958, Brussels (father was EU official)
- **Party**: CDU (Germany), EPP (European)
- **Commission President**: 2019-present
- **Previous role**: German Defense Minister (2013-2019)

## Commission Presidency

### First Term (2019-2024)
- **European Green Deal**: Flagship climate policy
- **COVID response**: Joint vaccine procurement, recovery fund
- **Ukraine support**: Sanctions on Russia, military aid coordination
- **Digital regulation**: Digital Services Act, Digital Markets Act

### Second Term (2024-present)
- **Trade pivot**: Accelerating deals amid US protectionism
- **Defense push**: European defense industrial strategy
- **China policy**: "De-risking" approach

## Trade Policy

### EU-India FTA
Leading EU delegation to New Delhi (January 25-27, 2026) alongside [[António Costa]] for signing ceremony. Attending India's Republic Day as guest of honor.

### EU-Mercosur FTA
- Pushed for deal conclusion (December 2024)
- Defended "split deal" approach
- Facing Parliament backlash and Court referral

### Trump Response
Coordinating EU response to [[Trade War 2025-2026]]:
- Preparing counter-tariffs
- Accelerating alternative trade deals
- Balancing unity with member state divisions

## Political Style

### Centralization
Critics argue she has concentrated power in Commission presidency:
- Direct control over key files
- Bypassing normal Commissioner processes
- Personal involvement in major negotiations

### Institutional Tensions
- Parliament: Mercosur controversy, censure threats
- Member states: Divisions over trade, defense spending
- Council: Friction over agenda-setting

## Background

### German Politics
- Medical doctor by training
- Seven children (often mentioned in profiles)
- Rose through CDU under Merkel
- Defense Minister during controversial period (equipment shortages)

### EU Career
- Surprise nomination for Commission President (2019)
- Narrow Parliament approval (383-327)
- Stronger mandate in 2024 re-election
