# Ethiopia

## Overview
Africa's second-most populous country with ancient civilization and unique non-colonial history. Strategic Horn of Africa location. Recently emerged from devastating civil war (Tigray). Rising power facing internal ethnic tensions and regional rivalries.

## Key Facts
- **Capital**: Addis Ababa (AU headquarters)
- **Population**: ~120 million (2nd in Africa)
- **GDP**: ~$155 billion (PPP much higher)
- **PM**: Abiy Ahmed (2018-present, Nobel Peace Prize 2019)
- **Unique**: Never colonized (except brief Italian occupation)

## Geopolitical Position

### Regional Significance
- Horn of Africa anchor
- AU headquarters host
- Landlocked (port access critical)
- Nile waters upstream
- Regional mediator (historically)

### Strategic Location
- Red Sea proximity
- Gulf of Aden access need
- Djibouti port dependence
- Eritrea peace (2018, fragile)
- Somalia border

## Tigray War (2020-2022)

### Background
- TPLF vs. federal government
- Ethnic federalism tensions
- Abiy's centralization
- November 2020 outbreak

### Devastation
- Hundreds of thousands killed
- Millions displaced
- Famine conditions
- War crimes allegations (all sides)
- Infrastructure destruction

### Cessation of Hostilities (Nov 2022)
- Pretoria Agreement
- Fragile peace
- Humanitarian access
- Disarmament (partial)
- Reconciliation challenges
