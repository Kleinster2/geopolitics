# Mark Carney

## Overview
Prime Minister of Canada (2025-present). Former Governor of the Bank of England (2013-2020) and Bank of Canada (2008-2013). Economist navigating Canada-US relations under Trump 2.0 tariff pressure.

## Positions

### Background
- **Governor, Bank of England** (2013-2020) - First non-British holder in modern era
- **Governor, Bank of Canada** (2008-2013) - Steered through financial crisis
- **UN Special Envoy for Climate Action and Finance** (2019-2023)

### Board of Peace Response
Accepted "in principle" but stated Canada won't pay the $1 billion fee; terms need discussion. Trump revoked Canada's invitation on January 23, 2026, following Carney's conditional response.

### US-Canada Relations
- Managing Trump tariff threats under [[Trade War 2025-2026]]
- Balancing sovereignty with economic dependence on US

### Davos Middle-Power Doctrine (January 2026)
In a January 20, 2026 special address to the World Economic Forum titled "Principled and Pragmatic: Canada's Path," Carney argued the post-Cold-War rules-based order had undergone "a rupture, not a transition" and called on middle powers — Canada among them — to cooperate against great-power economic coercion rather than depend on the US (confirmed; WEF, Canadian PMO, CBC). The address, which drew an unusual standing ovation, framed Canada as a middle power that must pool capability and diversify trade because it cannot match great-power scale alone, citing Canada's defense investments (over-the-horizon radar, submarines, aircraft) in that frame.

The framing has travelled beyond international relations: Brazilian computer scientist [[Silvio Meira]] (co-founder of Porto Digital) invoked Carney's middle-power doctrine (Manhattan Connection podcast, June 2026) to argue Brazil should play the same tier in artificial intelligence — diffusing the technology across its economy rather than racing the frontier (Meira's characterization, applied to AI strategy).
