# Dassault Aviation

## Overview
France's premier combat aircraft manufacturer and a pillar of French defense independence. Producer of the Rafale fighter jet and partner in the troubled FCAS sixth-generation fighter program. Symbol of France's defense industrial sovereignty.

## Key Facts
- **Headquarters**: Paris, France
- **Founded**: 1929 (as Société des Avions Marcel Dassault)
- **Employees**: ~13,000
- **Revenue**: ~€7 billion
- **Ownership**: Dassault family + Airbus minority stake

## Core Products

### Combat Aircraft
- **Rafale** - 4.5-gen multirole fighter (France's frontline jet)
- **Mirage 2000** - Previous generation (still exported)
- **nEUROn** - Stealth combat drone demonstrator

### Business Aviation
- **Falcon** jet series (significant revenue source)
- Dual-use technology development

### Future Programs
- **FCAS/SCAF** - 6th-gen fighter (with Airbus) - troubled

## Strategic Significance

### French Independence
- Enables France to build own fighter jets
- Not dependent on US aircraft (unlike most NATO)
- Nuclear delivery capability (Rafale)
- Symbol of Gaullist defense autonomy

### Export Success
- Rafale sold to: India, Egypt, Qatar, UAE, Greece, Indonesia, Serbia
- Major revenue and influence tool
- Competes with F-35, Eurofighter, Gripen

## FCAS Crisis

### The Program
- Future Combat Air System (SCAF in French)
- 6th-generation fighter to replace Rafale/Eurofighter
- Joint with Germany (Airbus) and Spain

### The Problem
- Years of failed negotiations on workshare
- Dassault vs Airbus disputes
- Program "about to fall apart" (2026)
- French concerns about German dominance
- See: [[Franco-German Relations]]

### Implications
- Could force France to go alone
- Would fragment European defense industry
- Massive investment at risk

## France vs F-35 Debate
- France chose Rafale over US F-35
- Germany bought F-35 (French frustration)
- Different philosophies: autonomy vs. interoperability
- Impacts European defense cooperation
