# France

## Overview
Europe's foremost military power with global reach, permanent UN Security Council seat, and independent nuclear deterrent. Champions "strategic autonomy" for Europe while maintaining Atlanticist commitments. Africa's former colonial power remains engaged on continent.

## Key Facts
- **Capital**: Paris
- **Population**: ~68 million
- **GDP**: ~$3 trillion (7th largest)
- **Military Budget**: ~$55 billion
- **Nuclear Warheads**: ~290
- **President**: Head of state with strong executive powers

## Geopolitical Position

### Strategic Culture
- Gaullist tradition (independence)
- Great power self-image
- Nuclear deterrent
- Interventionist capacity
- "Strategic autonomy" advocate

### European Leadership
- Franco-German engine
- EU defense integration push
- European sovereignty champion
- Occasional US friction

## Strategic Priorities
1. European strategic autonomy
2. Mediterranean/Africa security
3. Indo-Pacific presence
4. Nuclear deterrent credibility
5. Counter-terrorism
6. Climate/energy

## Military Capabilities
- Nuclear triad (air, submarine)
- Power projection (carriers, overseas bases)
- Rapid intervention force
- Defense industry (Dassault, Naval Group)
- Operations: Sahel, Middle East, Pacific

### Overseas Presence
- Indo-Pacific territories (New Caledonia, Polynesia)
- Indian Ocean (Réunion, Mayotte)
- Caribbean (Martinique, Guadeloupe)
- Africa bases (Djibouti, Senegal, Gabon)
