# Iran

## Overview
Theocratic regional power pursuing nuclear capabilities and regional influence through proxy networks. Under severe sanctions, maintains strategic defiance against US/Israel while deepening ties with Russia and China.

## Key Facts
- **Capital**: Tehran
- **Population**: ~87 million
- **GDP**: ~$400 billion (sanctioned economy)
- **Supreme Leader**: Ayatollah Ali Khamenei (since 1989)
- **President**: Elected but subordinate to Supreme Leader
- **Military**: IRGC (Revolutionary Guards) + regular armed forces

## Geopolitical Position

### Revolutionary Ideology
- Export of Islamic Revolution
- Opposition to Israel ("Zionist entity")
- Resistance against US hegemony ("Great Satan")
- Support for "oppressed" Muslims

### Regional Strategy
"Axis of Resistance" - network of allied militias:
- Hezbollah (Lebanon) - most capable
- Hamas/Islamic Jihad (Palestine)
- Houthis (Yemen)
- Iraqi militias (PMF)
- Syrian alliance

## Nuclear Program
- Advanced enrichment (60% purity)
- Breakout time: weeks
- JCPOA (2015) collapsed after US withdrawal (2018)
- No nuclear weapons (claimed)
- Israel/US consider military options

## Key Relationships

### Allies/Partners
- [[Russia]] - Defense cooperation, drones, strategic partnership
- [[China]] - 25-year strategic agreement, oil buyer
- [[Syria]] - Assad regime support
- Hezbollah - primary proxy

### Adversaries
- [[Israel]] - Existential enmity, shadow war
- [[United States]] - Sanctions, regime pressure
- [[Saudi Arabia]] - Regional rivalry (recent thaw)
- Gulf states - Threat perception

## Military Capabilities
- Large missile arsenal (largest in ME)
- Drone technology (Shahed)
- IRGC Quds Force (external operations)
- Asymmetric/hybrid warfare
- Navy: anti-access in Persian Gulf

## Sanctions Impact
- Oil exports restricted
- Banking isolation
- Economic hardship
- Inflation, currency collapse
- Black market economy

## Internal Dynamics
- Regime stability concerns
- Youth discontent (Woman, Life, Freedom protests)
- Economic grievances
- Succession questions (Khamenei aging)
- Hardliner dominance

## Strategic Dilemmas
- Nuclear threshold vs. strike risk
- Proxy escalation management
- Economic survival
- Generational change pressure
- Russia/China dependence
