# BRICS

## Overview
A grouping of major emerging economies originally coined as investment category (2001), evolved into political forum challenging Western-dominated institutions. Significantly expanded in 2024.

## Key Facts
- **Original acronym**: Brazil, Russia, India, China (BRIC, 2001)
- **South Africa added**: 2010 (BRICS)
- **2024 expansion**: Egypt, Ethiopia, Iran, UAE, Saudi Arabia
- **Combined GDP**: ~$30 trillion (~30% of global)
- **Population**: ~3.5 billion (~45% of global)

## Evolution

### Phase 1: Investment Category (2001)
Jim O'Neill (Goldman Sachs) coined "BRIC" for fast-growing emerging markets.

### Phase 2: Political Forum (2009)
First summit in Yekaterinburg, Russia. Coordination on global governance reform.

### Phase 3: Institution Building (2014-2015)
- New Development Bank (Shanghai)
- Contingent Reserve Arrangement

### Phase 4: Expansion (2024)
Major expansion signaling alternative to Western order.

## Member Profiles

| Country | Role | Interests |
|---------|------|-----------|
| China | Dominant economy | Multipolarity, RMB internationalization |
| India | Balancing power | Development, strategic autonomy |
| Russia | Isolated power | Sanctions evasion, Western counter |
| Brazil | Regional power | South-South cooperation, trade |
| South Africa | African voice | Development, representation |
| New members | Diverse | Energy, trade, alternative institutions |

## Institutions

### New Development Bank (NDB)
- Infrastructure financing
- Alternative to World Bank/IMF
- $100 billion authorized capital

### Contingent Reserve Arrangement
- $100 billion currency reserve
- Balance of payments support
- Alternative to IMF

## Strategic Significance

### Challenges to Western Order
- Alternative to IMF/World Bank
- De-dollarization discussions
- UN Security Council reform advocacy
- South-South cooperation platform

### Limitations
- Internal contradictions (India-China rivalry)
- Diverse political systems and interests
- Limited institutional depth
- Economic disparities among members

## Key Issues

### De-dollarization
- Local currency trade settlement
- BRICS currency discussions
- Limited practical progress

### Global Governance Reform
- UN Security Council expansion
- IMF/World Bank voting reform
- Alternative institutions

### Economic Cooperation
- Trade facilitation
- Investment coordination
- Technology sharing

## Internal Tensions
- China-India border disputes and competition
- Russia's isolation and dependence
- Saudi-Iran historical rivalry
- Brazil's political shifts
- Diverse regime types

## Significance Assessment
**What BRICS is:**
- Platform for emerging economy coordination
- Symbol of shifting global power
- Forum for non-Western voices

**What BRICS is not:**
- Military alliance
- Unified bloc
- Replacement for Western institutions (yet)
