# Kevin Rudd

## Overview
Former Australian Prime Minister (2007-2010, 2013) and diplomat who served as Ambassador to the United States (2023-2026). Leading Western expert on China - speaks Mandarin, wrote doctoral thesis on Xi Jinping. Resigned ambassadorship early amid tensions with Trump administration.

## Key Facts
- **Born**: September 21, 1957
- **Current Role**: President, Asia Society (2026-)
- **Previous**: Australian Ambassador to US (2023-2026)
- **PM Terms**: 26th Prime Minister (2007-2010, 2013)
- **Education**: PhD on Xi Jinping's worldview
- **Languages**: Fluent Mandarin

## Diplomatic Career

### US Ambassador (2023-2026)
- Appointed under Biden administration (March 2023)
- Resigned early, ending March 2026 (originally planned for 2027)
- Key achievements:
  - Critical technology agreement negotiations
  - Secured $25B+ US investment (Microsoft, Amazon)
  - Julian Assange's return to Australia (2024)
  - Global Entry/TSA access for Australians

### Trump Tensions
- Pre-ambassador social media posts called Trump:
  - "Most destructive president in history"
  - "Traitor to the West"
- Posts deleted after Trump's 2024 election win
- **October 2025 incident**: At White House bilateral meeting, Trump publicly confronted Rudd: "I don't like you either. And I probably never will."
- Trump called Rudd "a little bit nasty" and "not the brightest bulb"
- Resigned weeks after public confrontation

## China Expertise

### Academic Work
- PhD thesis on Xi Jinping's worldview
- Fluent Mandarin speaker
- Decades of China engagement
- Book: *The Avoidable War: The Dangers of a Catastrophic Conflict Between the US and Xi Jinping's China*

### Asia Society
- President (2021-2023, returning 2026)
- Heads Centre for China Analysis
- Leading voice on US-China competition
- "Managed strategic competition" concept

## Key Views

### On US-China Relations
- War is "avoidable" but requires active management
- Both sides must understand each other's red lines
- Advocates structured strategic competition
- Critical of both American hawkishness and Chinese assertiveness

### On Australia's Position
- Strong US alliance essential
- But must manage China relationship pragmatically
- AUKUS supporter
- Regional stability focus

## Political Career

### Prime Minister (First Term: 2007-2010)
- Ratified Kyoto Protocol
- Formal apology to Indigenous Australians
- Global Financial Crisis response
- Ousted by Julia Gillard in party coup

### Prime Minister (Second Term: 2013)
- Brief return after defeating Gillard
- Lost 2013 election to Tony Abbott

## Significance

### As China Expert
- One of few Western leaders fluent in Mandarin
- Direct relationships with Chinese leadership
- Academic credibility on Xi Jinping
- Bridge between policy and scholarship

### AUKUS Context
- Ambassador during AUKUS implementation
- Navigated submarine deal complexities
- Managed US-Australia defense deepening
