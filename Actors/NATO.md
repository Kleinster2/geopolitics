# NATO (North Atlantic Treaty Organization)

## Overview
The world's most powerful military alliance, linking North America and Europe in collective defense. Founded to counter Soviet threat, adapted post-Cold War, now revitalized by Russia's aggression.

## Key Facts
- **Founded**: April 4, 1949 (Washington Treaty)
- **Members**: 32 (as of 2024)
- **Headquarters**: Brussels, Belgium
- **Military HQ**: SHAPE, Mons, Belgium
- **Secretary General**: (rotating leadership)

## Core Principles

### Article 5 - Collective Defense
> An armed attack against one shall be considered an attack against all.

Invoked once: after 9/11 attacks.

### Article 4 - Consultations
Members consult when territorial integrity, political independence, or security is threatened.

## Membership Evolution
| Era | Members |
|-----|---------|
| 1949 | 12 founding members |
| Cold War | Added Greece, Turkey, Germany, Spain |
| 1999-2004 | Eastern European expansion |
| 2009-2020 | Balkans, Montenegro, North Macedonia |
| 2023-2024 | Finland, Sweden |

## Military Capabilities
- Combined defense spending: ~$1 trillion
- US provides ~70% of capabilities
- Nuclear sharing arrangements
- Integrated command structure
- Rapid reaction forces (NATO Response Force)

## Strategic Concepts
- **Collective defense**: Core mission
- **Crisis management**: Out-of-area operations
- **Cooperative security**: Partnerships

## Key Operations (Historical)
- Bosnia (IFOR/SFOR, 1990s)
- Kosovo (KFOR, 1999-present)
- Afghanistan (ISAF, 2001-2014)
- Libya (2011)
- Air policing (Baltic, Iceland)

## Current Priorities
1. Deterrence and defense vs Russia
2. Eastern flank reinforcement
3. Defense spending (2% GDP target)
4. Resilience and preparedness
5. Emerging challenges (cyber, space, climate)

## Challenges
- Burden-sharing debates
- US commitment questions
- Turkey's independent policies
- Consensus decision-making
- Adapting to China challenge
