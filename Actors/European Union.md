# European Union

## Overview
The world's most advanced regional integration project, combining economic union with emerging political and security dimensions. Navigates between deeper integration and member state sovereignty.

## Key Facts
- **Founded**: 1993 (Maastricht Treaty); roots in 1957 EEC
- **Members**: 27 states
- **Population**: ~450 million
- **GDP**: ~$18 trillion (2nd largest economy)
- **Headquarters**: Brussels (main), Strasbourg, Luxembourg
- **Currency**: Euro (20 members in Eurozone)

## Institutional Structure

### European Commission
- Executive body
- Proposes legislation
- Enforces treaties
- President: Head of Commission

### European Council
- Heads of state/government
- Sets strategic direction
- Consensus decisions

### Council of the EU
- National ministers
- Co-legislator with Parliament
- Rotating presidency

### European Parliament
- Directly elected (705 MEPs)
- Co-legislator
- Budgetary authority

### European Court of Justice
- Interprets EU law
- Supremacy of EU law

## Core Policies

### Single Market
- Free movement: goods, services, capital, people
- Common regulations
- Competition policy

### Economic & Monetary Union
- Eurozone (common currency)
- European Central Bank
- Fiscal rules (Stability Pact)

### Common Policies
- Trade (exclusive competence)
- Agriculture (CAP)
- Fisheries
- Regional development

### Emerging Areas
- Foreign policy (CFSP)
- Security/defense (CSDP)
- Migration and asylum
- Digital regulation

## Strategic Challenges

### Strategic Autonomy Debate
- Reduce dependence on US
- Defense capabilities
- Technology sovereignty
- Energy security

### Russia Challenge
- Ukraine war response
- Sanctions coordination
- Energy decoupling
- Defense awakening

### China Policy
- "Systemic rival" designation
- Economic dependence
- Human rights concerns
- Technology competition

### Internal Cohesion
- Rule of law (Hungary, Poland)
- North-South economic divide
- East-West political divide
- Migration burden sharing
