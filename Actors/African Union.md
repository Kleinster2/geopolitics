# African Union

## Overview
Continental organization succeeding the OAU, seeking African solutions to African problems. Ambitious integration agenda faces capacity constraints and sovereignty sensitivities.

## Key Facts
- **Founded**: 2002 (replacing OAU, est. 1963)
- **Members**: 55 states (all Africa)
- **Population**: ~1.4 billion
- **Combined GDP**: ~$3 trillion
- **Headquarters**: Addis Ababa, Ethiopia
- **Chairperson**: Rotating leadership

## Institutional Structure

### Assembly
- Heads of state/government
- Supreme organ
- Annual summits

### Executive Council
- Foreign ministers
- Prepares Assembly decisions

### AU Commission
- Secretariat
- Chairperson leads
- Implements decisions

### Peace and Security Council (PSC)
- 15 members
- Conflict prevention/management
- Can authorize interventions

### Pan-African Parliament
- Advisory body
- Limited powers

## Core Objectives

### Agenda 2063
- "The Africa We Want"
- 50-year development vision
- Integration, peace, prosperity
- 7 aspirations, flagship projects

### African Continental Free Trade Area (AfCFTA)
- Launched 2021
- World's largest free trade area by countries
- Potential: $3.4 trillion market
- Implementation challenges

## Peace and Security

### African Peace and Security Architecture (APSA)
- Continental Early Warning System
- African Standby Force (ASF)
- Panel of the Wise
- Peace Fund

### Interventions
- Darfur (AMIS, then AU-UN hybrid)
- Somalia (AMISOM/ATMIS)
- Mali, CAR, DRC support

### Principles Evolution
- OAU: Non-interference
- AU: "Non-indifference" to atrocities
- Right to intervene (Article 4h)
- Practical limits

## Challenges

### Capacity
- Limited funding (member contributions)
- Dependence on external donors
- Implementation gaps
- Institutional weakness

### Political
- Sovereignty sensitivities
- Unconstitutional government changes
- Democratic backsliding
- Consensus difficulties

### Security
- Sahel crisis
- Great Lakes instability
- Horn of Africa conflicts
- Terrorism spread

## Regional Economic Communities (RECs)
- ECOWAS (West Africa)
- SADC (Southern Africa)
- EAC (East Africa)
- IGAD (Horn of Africa)
- ECCAS (Central Africa)
- Building blocks for integration
