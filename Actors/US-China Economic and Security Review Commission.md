# US-China Economic and Security Review Commission

Bipartisan US congressional advisory commission (USCC), created in 2000, mandated to monitor and report to Congress on the national-security implications of the US-China economic relationship. Its annual report is one of the most-cited official US assessments of Chinese economic statecraft.

## Notable positions in this vault

- **2024 Annual Report**: flagged China's recognition of [[Data as a Factor of Production|data as a balance-sheet asset]] and the use of data as loan collateral by indebted local government financing vehicles, noting the assets' market value remains "difficult to determine."
