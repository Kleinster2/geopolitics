# Xi Jinping

## Overview
General Secretary of the Chinese Communist Party (2012-), President of the People's Republic of China (2013-), Chairman of the Central Military Commission. Most powerful Chinese leader since Mao Zedong. Removed term limits (2018), secured unprecedented third term (2022). Architect of assertive foreign policy, "Chinese Dream" nationalism, and tightened party control.

## Key Facts
- **Born**: June 15, 1953
- **In office**: 2012- (General Secretary), 2013- (President)
- **Party role**: CCP General Secretary, CMC Chairman
- **Background**: "princeling" (father Xi Zhongxun was revolutionary leader)

## Domestic Policy

### Political
- Anti-corruption campaign (targeted rivals, consolidated power)
- Removed term limits (2018 constitutional amendment)
- "Xi Jinping Thought" enshrined in constitution
- Tightened ideological control, media censorship
- Zero-COVID policy (abandoned 2022)

### Economic
- State intervention increasing
- Tech sector crackdown (2020-2021)
- "Common prosperity" wealth redistribution rhetoric
- Dual circulation strategy
- Property sector crisis management

## Foreign Policy

### Vision
- "[[Community with a Shared Future]]" (人类命运共同体)
- "Chinese Dream" of national rejuvenation
- By 2049 (PRC centenary): Great power status restored

### Key Initiatives
- [[Belt and Road Initiative]] (2013-)
- [[Comprehensive Strategic Partnership]] network
- Military modernization (world-class by 2049)
- South China Sea assertiveness
- [[Taiwan]] "reunification" as core interest

### US Relations
- [[Great Power Competition]] framing
- Trade war, technology decoupling
- Managed competition rhetoric
- No return to pre-Trump engagement

### Russia
- "No limits" partnership (February 2022)
- Coordination on international order
- Strategic alignment without alliance

## Taiwan Policy
- "Reunification" as "historic mission"
- Hasn't ruled out force
- Increased military pressure (exercises, incursions)
- "1992 Consensus" basis for talks

## Key Characteristics

### Leadership Style
- Centralized decision-making
- Personality cult elements
- Risk tolerance unclear
- Long-term strategic patience

### Strengths
- Consolidated control
- Nationalist legitimacy
- Economic resources
- Military modernization

### Vulnerabilities
- Economic slowdown
- [[Demographics|Demographic]] crisis
- COVID response criticism
- Elite opposition unclear
