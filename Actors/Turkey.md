# Turkey

## Overview
Strategic NATO member bridging Europe and Asia, controlling Black Sea access. Under Erdogan, pursues independent foreign policy balancing East and West, frustrating allies while maximizing leverage. Regional power with Ottoman nostalgia.

## Key Facts
- **Capital**: Ankara
- **Population**: ~85 million
- **GDP**: ~$1 trillion
- **Military**: NATO's second-largest army
- **President**: Recep Tayyip Erdogan (2014-present, PM 2003-2014)
- **Strategic Assets**: Bosphorus, Incirlik Air Base

## Geopolitical Position

### Strategic Geography
- Controls Turkish Straits (Bosphorus, Dardanelles)
- Bridge between Europe and Asia
- Black Sea/Mediterranean access
- Borders Syria, Iraq, Iran
- NATO's southeastern flank

### Erdogan's Foreign Policy
- "Strategic autonomy" pursuit
- Neo-Ottoman regional ambitions
- Transactional relationships
- Leverage maximization
- Domestic nationalism driver

## Strategic Priorities
1. Kurdish threat neutralization
2. Regional influence expansion
3. Economic stability
4. Defense industry development
5. Migration leverage
6. Great power balancing

## Key Relationships

### Complex Alliance: NATO
- Member since 1952
- Second-largest military
- Incirlik Air Base (US nuclear weapons)
- S-400 purchase (sanctions trigger)
- Sweden/Finland accession delays
- Increasingly difficult partner

### Russia
- Energy dependence (gas, nuclear plant)
- S-400 purchase
- Syria coordination
- Trade partner
- Not aligned on Ukraine
- Careful balancing

### United States
- Alliance strains (S-400, Syria, Gulen)
- Sanctions (CAATSA)
- F-35 program ejection
- Intermittent cooperation
- Strategic value acknowledged

### Middle East
- Syria: Anti-Kurdish operations, refugees
- Libya: Military intervention
- Gulf: Balanced ties
- Israel: Normalized (fluctuating)
- Egypt: Rivalry, recent thaw

## Military Capabilities
- Large conventional forces
- Growing defense industry (Bayraktar drones)
- Regional intervention capacity
- NATO interoperability (eroding)
- Seeking F-16s (F-35 alternative)

### Defense Industry
- Bayraktar TB2 drones (Ukraine fame)
- Domestic production push
- Export growth
- Technology gaps remain

## Kurdish Issue
- PKK insurgency (40 years)
- Syrian Kurds (YPG/SDF)
- Cross-border operations
- NATO friction point
- Existential framing

## Economic Challenges
- Currency crisis
- High inflation
- Unorthodox monetary policy
- Youth unemployment
- Brain drain

## Internal Dynamics
- Erdogan dominance
- Opposition coalition
- Authoritarian slide
- Media control
- Judicial independence concerns

## Strategic Dilemmas
- NATO membership vs. independence
- Russia energy vs. Western alignment
- Kurdish operations vs. ally relations
- Economic needs vs. political choices
