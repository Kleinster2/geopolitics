# AUKUS

## Overview
Trilateral security partnership between Australia, United Kingdom, and United States focused on nuclear-powered submarine acquisition for Australia and advanced technology sharing. Major shift in Indo-Pacific strategic balance.

## Key Facts
- **Members**: Australia, United Kingdom, United States
- **Announced**: September 15, 2021
- **Focus**: Nuclear submarines + advanced capabilities
- **Cost**: $268+ billion (submarine program)
- **Timeline**: 2030s for operational submarines

## Two Pillars

### Pillar 1: Nuclear-Powered Submarines
Australia to acquire nuclear-powered attack submarines (SSNs):

**Optimal Pathway**:
1. **Near-term** (2023-2027): Increased US/UK submarine visits to Australia
2. **Transition** (2027-2030s): US Virginia-class submarines based in Australia
3. **Long-term** (2030s): Australia acquires 3-5 Virginia-class
4. **Indigenous** (2040s): SSN-AUKUS built in Australia (UK design + US tech)

### Pillar 2: Advanced Capabilities
Technology cooperation in:
- Hypersonic weapons
- Electronic warfare
- Cyber capabilities
- Artificial intelligence
- Quantum computing
- Undersea capabilities
- Space

### Nuclear-Powered Advantages
- Unlimited range (no diesel refueling)
- Underwater endurance (weeks vs. days)
