# RANE

## Overview

Network-based risk intelligence company that connects organizations with expert analysts and risk professionals. Acquired [[Stratfor]] in February 2020, integrating its geopolitical analysis capabilities into RANE's broader risk platform. Now operates Stratfor's analysis arm as "RANE Worldview" (worldview.stratfor.com).

## Key Facts

- **Full name**: Risk Assistance Network + Exchange
- **Founded**: 2011
- **Headquarters**: New York
- **Model**: Network of expert analysts + subscription intelligence platform
- **Investor**: Teakwood Capital (Dallas-based PE firm, invested at Stratfor acquisition)
- **Website**: ranenetwork.com

## Business model

RANE operates as a **network-based consultancy** — rather than maintaining a single large in-house team, it connects clients with a network of subject-matter experts across geopolitics, security, legal, cyber, and compliance. Think of it as a curated marketplace for risk intelligence.

Revenue streams:
- **Worldview subscriptions**: Geopolitical analysis platform (inherited from Stratfor) — daily briefs, forecasts, regional assessments
- **Expert consultations**: On-demand access to specialist analysts
- **Custom research**: Bespoke intelligence for corporate clients
- **Essential Geopolitics podcast**: Free content / marketing channel

## Stratfor acquisition (February 2020)

RANE acquired Stratfor Enterprises (Austin, TX) to add geopolitical forecasting to its risk network. Key details:
- Stratfor had 20+ years of geopolitical analysis and a loyal subscriber base
- George Friedman had already departed in 2015 to found [[Geopolitical Futures]]
- Stratfor brand continues via Worldview platform
- Stratfor's analytical team (including [[Ryan Bohl]], Middle East desk) stayed on
