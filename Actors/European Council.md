# European Council

## Overview
The EU institution comprising heads of state or government of member states, plus its own President and the [[European Commission]] President. Sets the EU's political direction and priorities. Not to be confused with the [[Council of the EU]] (ministers) or the Council of Europe (non-EU body).

## Composition

### Members
- 27 heads of state or government
- European Council President (chairs meetings)
- European Commission President (participates, no vote)
- EU High Representative (participates in foreign affairs items)

### Current Leadership
- **President**: [[António Costa]] (December 2024-)
- Serves 2.5-year term, renewable once
- Previous: Charles Michel (2019-2024)

## Functions

### Political Direction
- Defines EU's general political directions and priorities
- Sets strategic agenda
- Does NOT legislate (that's Council of EU + Parliament)

### Key Decisions
- Treaty changes
- Enlargement
- Foreign policy strategic guidelines
- Institutional appointments (Commission President, ECB, High Representative)
- Crisis response coordination

### Voting
- Usually consensus
- Qualified majority for some appointments
- Unanimity for foreign policy, taxation, some other areas

## Relationship to Other Institutions

| Institution | Role | Relationship |
|-------------|------|--------------|
| **European Council** | Strategic direction | Sets agenda |
| [[Council of the EU]] | Legislation (with Parliament) | Implements direction |
| [[European Commission]] | Executive, proposes legislation | Commission President participates |
| [[European Parliament]] | Legislation, oversight | Elects Commission President on Council's proposal |

## Meetings

### Summit Format
- Meets at least 4 times per year
- Additional emergency summits as needed
- Brussels (Europa building)
- Conclusions adopted by consensus

### Process
- Prepared by General Affairs Council
- President circulates draft conclusions
- Leaders negotiate, often through the night
- Final conclusions shape EU policy

## Historical Development

### Origins
- Informal summits began 1961
- Formalized 1974
- Given legal status by Lisbon Treaty (2009)
- President role created 2009

### Evolution
- From occasional summits to regular governance
- Crisis management role expanded (Euro crisis, migration, COVID, Ukraine)
- Increasingly central to EU decision-making

## Recent Priorities (2024-2026)
- Ukraine support and European security
- Economic competitiveness
- Migration
- Climate/energy transition
- Strategic autonomy
- Enlargement (Ukraine, Western Balkans)

## Limitations
- Consensus requirement can block action
- National interests often dominate
- Democratic accountability questions
- Slow response to crises requiring unanimity
