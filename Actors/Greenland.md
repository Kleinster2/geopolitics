# Greenland

## Overview
World's largest island, an autonomous territory of Denmark with increasing self-governance since 2009. Strategic location in the Arctic and vast mineral resources make it increasingly important as climate change opens new shipping routes and resource extraction opportunities. Subject of unprecedented US annexation threats in 2026.

## Key Facts
- **Status**: Autonomous territory of Denmark
- **Capital**: Nuuk
- **Population**: ~57,000 (mostly Inuit)
- **Area**: 836,330 sq mi (world's largest island)
- **Ice Coverage**: ~80% covered by ice sheet
- **Self-Rule**: Since 2009
- **Currency**: Danish Krone
- **Defense**: Denmark (NATO)

## Strategic Value

### Geographic Position
- Controls North Atlantic approaches
- Arctic shipping route access
- Proximity to North America and Europe
- Critical for Arctic monitoring

### Military Infrastructure
- **Thule Air Base** (Pituffik Space Base)
  - US Space Force presence
  - Ballistic missile early warning
  - Satellite tracking station
  - Established 1951

### Resources
- **Rare Earth Elements**: Critical minerals
- **Uranium**: Significant deposits
- **Oil & Gas**: Offshore potential
- **Zinc, Lead, Iron**: Mining potential
- **Fresh Water**: Massive ice sheet reserves

### Climate Change Impact
- Ice sheet melting accelerating
- New shipping routes opening
- Resources becoming accessible
- Strategic importance rising

## Governance

### Relationship with Denmark
- Part of Danish Realm (with Faroe Islands)
- Foreign policy and defense: Copenhagen
- Domestic affairs: Self-rule government
- Annual block grant from Denmark (~$500M)
- Independence referendum possible

### Self-Rule Government
- **Naalakkersuisut** (executive)
- **Inatsisartut** (parliament, 31 seats)
- Controls natural resources since 2009
- Growing independence movement

## Historical US Interest

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1867 | Secretary Seward explored purchase |
| 1946 | Truman offered $100 million (rejected) |
| 1951 | Defense agreement, Thule base established |
| 2019 | Trump floated purchase (Denmark refused) |
| 2025-26 | Escalated to annexation threats |

## 2026 Crisis

### Trump's Position
- Openly threatening **forcible annexation**
- Claims essential for US national security
- Cites strategic Arctic importance
- Willingness to use military force implied
- "Greenland is vital to America's future"

### Denmark's Response
- PM Frederiksen: "Greenland is not for sale"
- Rejects any transfer of sovereignty
- Deploying Danish military assets
- Hosting European allied forces
- Warning: Would end NATO if US attacks

### European Military Deployments (January 2026)
| Country | Deployment |
|---------|------------|
| **France** | Military team on ground, reinforcing air/sea/land |
| **Finland** | Military liaison officers |
| **Denmark** | Joint exercises with allies |

### Greenlandic Position
- Population divided on independence
- Some openness to US relationship
- Reject being "traded" without consent
- Want self-determination respected

## Arctic Competition

### Key Players
- **United States**: Thule base, annexation claims
- **Russia**: Militarizing Arctic, Northern Sea Route
- **China**: "Near-Arctic state" claims, investment interest
- **Canada**: Northwest Passage disputes
- **Norway**: Svalbard, Arctic Council

### Opening Routes
- **Northwest Passage**: Through Canadian Arctic
- **Northern Sea Route**: Along Russian coast
- **Transpolar Route**: Direct across pole (future)

## Economy
- Fishing (shrimp, halibut) - main export
- Mining - developing
- Tourism - growing
- Heavily subsidized by Denmark
- Small, isolated economy

## Demographics
- ~90% Greenlandic Inuit
- ~10% Danish/European
- Youngest population in Danish Realm
- Urbanization increasing
- Brain drain to Denmark

## Challenges
- Climate change impacts
- Economic dependency on Denmark
- Limited infrastructure
- Social issues (alcoholism, suicide rates)
- Balancing development and environment

## Independence Question

### For Independence
- Cultural distinctiveness
- Resource wealth potential
- Self-determination desire
- Young population support

### Against Independence
- Economic viability concerns
- Small population
- Defense capacity
- Danish welfare state benefits

### Current Status
- Independence referendum legally possible
- No immediate timeline
- Economic sustainability key question
- 2026 crisis complicates picture
