# Chatham House

## Overview
Britain's premier foreign affairs think tank, formally the Royal Institute of International Affairs. Founded alongside CFR after WWI, represents the UK foreign policy establishment. Origin of the famous "Chatham House Rule" governing off-the-record discussions. Global influence on international affairs discourse.

## Key Facts
- **Founded**: 1920
- **Formal Name**: Royal Institute of International Affairs
- **Headquarters**: London (St James's Square)
- **Director**: Bronwen Maddox (2022-)
- **Patron**: King Charles III

## The Chatham House Rule

### Definition
> "When a meeting, or part thereof, is held under the Chatham House Rule, participants are free to use the information received, but neither the identity nor the affiliation of the speaker(s), nor that of any other participant, may be revealed."

### Purpose
- Enable candid discussion
- Protect sources
- Encourage openness
- Now globally adopted

### Usage
- Diplomatic meetings
- Corporate discussions
- Academic conferences
- Media briefings
