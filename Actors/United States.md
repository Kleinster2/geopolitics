# United States

## Overview
The world's preeminent superpower since 1991, wielding unmatched military, economic, and cultural influence. Central to the post-WWII international order through alliance networks, institutions, and forward military presence.

## Key Facts
- **Capital**: Washington, D.C.
- **Population**: ~335 million
- **GDP**: ~$27 trillion (largest nominal)
- **Military Budget**: ~$850 billion (largest globally)
- **Nuclear Warheads**: ~5,500

## Geopolitical Position

### Global Role
- Sole superpower since Cold War end
- Hub of alliance networks (NATO, bilateral Asian alliances)
- Leading role in international institutions (UN, IMF, World Bank)
- Global military presence (750+ overseas bases)

### Strategic Priorities
1. Great power competition with [[China]] and [[Russia]]
2. Indo-Pacific primacy
3. European security via [[NATO]]
4. Middle East stability
5. Counter-terrorism
6. Technology competition (AI, semiconductors)

## Alliances & Partnerships
- **NATO**: Foundational European security commitment
- **Indo-Pacific**: Japan, South Korea, Australia, Philippines
- **Five Eyes**: Intelligence sharing (UK, Canada, Australia, NZ)
- **Quad**: US, Japan, India, Australia
- **AUKUS**: US, UK, Australia

## Key Interests
- Maintain global primacy
- Freedom of navigation
- Non-proliferation
- Open international economic system
- Democratic governance promotion

## Current Challenges
- Rise of China
- Russia's European revisionism
- Domestic political polarization
- Debt and fiscal sustainability
- Alliance management
