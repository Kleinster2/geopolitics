# Bank Melli Iran

## Overview
Iran's oldest and largest bank, state-owned and heavily sanctioned. Serves as a key pillar of the Iranian financial system but cut off from international banking due to US and EU sanctions.

## Key Facts
- **Persian name**: بانک ملی ایران
- **Meaning**: "National Bank of Iran"
- **Established**: 1927
- **Type**: State-owned commercial bank
- **Status**: Operating but heavily sanctioned

## Role in Iranian Economy
- Largest bank by assets
- Primary bank for government transactions
- Extensive branch network domestically
- Historically handled Iran's international trade

## Sanctions

### US Sanctions
Designated under multiple sanctions programs:
- Proliferation (nuclear program support)
- Terrorism (alleged Hezbollah financing)
- Human rights violations

### EU Sanctions
Also sanctioned by European Union, limiting European business ties.

### Impact
- Cannot access dollar system
- Cut off from SWIFT (largely)
- Foreign assets frozen
- Cannot conduct normal international banking
- Must use workarounds for trade

## Current Status (2026)
Operating domestically but under severe strain from:
- [[Iranian Economic Crisis]]
- Currency collapse affecting balance sheet
- Sanctions limiting international operations
- General banking system fragility
