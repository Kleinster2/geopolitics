# Marco Rubio

## Overview
US Secretary of State in Trump 2.0 (confirmed 2025). Former US Senator from Florida (2011-2025). Known for hawkish positions on China and Latin America. Member of the [[Board of Peace]] founding executive board.

## Positions

- **Secretary of State** (2025-present)
- Member, Board of Peace founding executive board

### Previous

- Senate Foreign Relations Committee

## Policy Positions (On-the-Record)

### China
- Long-standing China hawk
- Advocated sanctions on Chinese officials over Hong Kong, Xinjiang
- Supported Taiwan defense measures

### Latin America
- Hard line on Venezuela, Cuba, Nicaragua
- Supported maximum pressure on Maduro regime

## 2026 Iran War: Capitol Hill Admissions (March 2-3)

During the [[2026 Iran conflict]], Rubio gave a press conference on Capitol Hill that caused significant political fallout.

### March 2 statements (confirmed, State Dept transcript)

**On Israel driving the decision:**
> "The Department of War assessed that if we did that, if we waited for them to hit us first after they were attacked and by someone else, Israel attacked them, they hit us first and we waited for them to hit us, we would suffer more casualties and more deaths. We went proactively in a defensive way to prevent them from inflicting higher damage."

This was the first time a Trump official explicitly acknowledged Israel as a driving force behind the war (The Intercept). Axios reported it "blew open MAGA's Israel divide."

**On the real objective — missiles, not nukes:**
> "Our mission and our focus is the destruction of their ballistic missile capabilities and their ability to manufacture them as well as the threat posed by their Navy to global shipping."

Rubio cited Iran producing 100+ short-range ballistic missiles per month vs. only 6-7 interceptors that could be built monthly. Warned Iran could reach "immunity" within 12-18 months. Referenced 20% of global energy transiting through Iranian-controlled waterways.

### March 3 walkback (reported, Jewish Insider)
Rubio returned to Capitol Hill and partially walked back the Israel framing, insisting "this had to happen anyway" and that his prior comments had been selectively clipped.

### Significance
- Confirmed Israeli priority was missiles, not nuclear program — consistent with IAEA head Grossi's statement that Iran had no nuclear weapons program
- Created a "horseshoe" political alignment: anti-war left and anti-war right both seized on the admission that Israel dictated US policy ([[Trita Parsi]], Quincy Institute)
- The walkback itself was newsworthy — suggesting the administration recognized the political damage
