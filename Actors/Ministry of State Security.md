# Ministry of State Security

The Ministry of State Security is China's main civilian intelligence and state-security service. It is relevant to geopolitics because it sits behind many China-related counterintelligence, foreign-agent, influence, and state-security cases, while also serving the party-state's broader political-security priorities.

## May 2026 Pauken case

NPR's May 27, 2026 interview with Politico's Josh Gerstein reports that US prosecutors alleged Thomas Pauken II acted as an unregistered agent for China and worked with a handler the FBI believed was tied to the MSS. This remains an allegation and should not be written as a conviction.

The case matters because it shows how influence operations can sit between visible media work and alleged clandestine direction. Pauken's prior activity around [[Xinhua]], CGTN, and other Chinese state-media channels does not itself prove intelligence work; the legal significance comes from prosecutors' allegation of direction or control by a foreign government-linked handler.
