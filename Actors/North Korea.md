# North Korea (DPRK)

## Overview
Isolated totalitarian state with advancing nuclear and missile capabilities. Regime survival paramount. Defies international pressure while extracting concessions. Poses direct threat to South Korea, Japan, and increasingly the US homeland.

## Key Facts
- **Official Name**: Democratic People's Republic of Korea
- **Capital**: Pyongyang
- **Population**: ~26 million
- **GDP**: ~$30 billion (CIA estimate)
- **Leader**: Kim Jong Un (2011-present)
- **Ideology**: Juche (self-reliance) + Songun (military-first)

## Nuclear Program

### Capabilities (Estimated)
- 50-60 nuclear warheads
- Plutonium and uranium programs
- Thermonuclear (H-bomb) claimed
- Miniaturization achieved

### Delivery Systems
| System | Range | Target |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Hwasong-15/17 | 13,000 km | US mainland |
| Hwasong-12 | 4,500 km | Guam |
| Nodong | 1,300 km | Japan |
| KN-23/24 | 400-600 km | South Korea |

### Recent Developments
- Hypersonic missiles
- Solid-fuel ICBMs
- Tactical nuclear weapons
- Submarine-launched missiles
- "Nuclear force" constitutional status

## Regime Goals
1. **Survival**: Kim family rule preservation
2. **Recognition**: Acceptance as nuclear state
3. **Sanctions relief**: Economic pressure reduction
4. **US withdrawal**: From Korean Peninsula
5. **Unification**: Under DPRK terms (rhetorical)

## Key Relationships

### China
- Treaty ally (1961)
- Economic lifeline (90% trade)
- Buffer state value
- Sanctions enforcement (selective)
- Limited influence

### Russia
- Renewed cooperation
- Arms sales (Ukraine war)
- Labor exports
- Diplomatic support
- Sanctions evasion

### South Korea
- Technically at war (armistice only)
- Periodic engagement/tension cycles
- Current: relations frozen
- Unification rhetoric abandoned (Kim)

### United States
- No diplomatic relations
- Nuclear talks (failed)
- "Maximum pressure" campaigns
- Deterrence posture
