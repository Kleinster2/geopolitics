# Israel

## Overview
Militarily dominant Middle Eastern democracy with undeclared nuclear arsenal. Faces existential threats from Iran while normalizing relations with Arab states. Ongoing Palestinian conflict remains central challenge.

## Key Facts
- **Capital**: Jerusalem (claimed; disputed internationally)
- **Population**: ~9.5 million
- **GDP**: ~$520 billion
- **Military Budget**: ~$24 billion
- **Nuclear Status**: Undeclared (~90 warheads estimated)
- **Government**: Parliamentary (frequent elections)

## Geopolitical Position

### Security Doctrine
- Qualitative military edge (QME)
- Preemption when threatened
- Nuclear ambiguity
- Intelligence superiority
- Rapid mobilization

### Existential Threat Perception
- Iran nuclear program - "never again" mentality
- Surrounded by hostile actors
- Small territory, no strategic depth
- Demographic concerns

## Strategic Priorities
1. Prevent Iranian nuclear weapon
2. Counter Iran's proxy network
3. Maintain military superiority
4. Expand normalization (Abraham Accords)
5. US relationship
6. Manage Palestinian issue

## Key Relationships

### Allies
- [[United States]] - "Unbreakable" alliance, $3.8B annual aid
- Abraham Accords states - UAE, Bahrain, Morocco
- [[Egypt]] - Cold peace (1979)
- [[Jordan]] - Peace treaty (1994)

### Adversaries
- [[Iran]] - Existential threat, shadow war
- Hezbollah - Northern front
- Hamas - Gaza
- Palestinian Islamic Jihad
- Houthis - Missile threat

### Complex
- [[Saudi Arabia]] - Unofficial ties, normalization discussions
- [[Turkey]] - Deteriorated, cautious engagement

## Military Capabilities
- Qualitative edge in region
- Advanced air force (F-35s)
- Iron Dome, Arrow defense systems
- Cyber capabilities (Unit 8200)
- Undeclared nuclear deterrent
- Intelligence services (Mossad, Shin Bet)

## Palestinian Issue

### Current Status
- West Bank: Occupation, settlements, PA cooperation
- Gaza: Blockade, Hamas control, periodic conflicts
- Two-state solution: Moribund
- One-state reality: Emerging debate

### Challenges
- International criticism
- Domestic division
- Demographic trends
- Democratic character tensions
