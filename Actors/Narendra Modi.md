# Narendra Modi

## Overview
Prime Minister of India (2014-), leader of the Bharatiya Janata Party. Longest-serving non-Congress PM. Hindu nationalist politics combined with economic modernization agenda. Pursuing "multi-alignment" foreign policyâ€”strategic partnerships with US, Russia, and others simultaneously. Key figure in [[BRICS]] and Global South leadership.

## Key Facts
- **Born**: September 17, 1950
- **In office**: 2014-
- **Party**: Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP)
- **Background**: RSS activist, Gujarat Chief Minister (2001-2014)

## Domestic Policy

### Political
- Hindu nationalist agenda (Hindutva)
- Centralized power in PMO
- Weakened opposition, press freedom concerns
- Kashmir Article 370 revocation (2019)
- Citizenship Amendment Act (2019)

### Economic
- "Make in India" manufacturing push
- Digital India initiatives
- GST tax reform
- Infrastructure investment
- Mixed results on job creation

## Foreign Policy

### "Multi-Alignment"
- Strategic autonomy from Cold War non-alignment
- Partnerships with competing powers simultaneously
- Transactional, interest-based approach

### US Relations
- Defense partnership strengthened
- Quad participation
- Technology cooperation
- But maintains independence

### Russia Relations
- Traditional defense supplier
- Continued energy purchases despite Ukraine sanctions
- Diplomatic balancing

### China Relations
- Border tensions (Ladakh clashes 2020)
- Economic interdependence
- Strategic rivalry
- BRICS partner but wary neighbor

### Middle East
- Strong Gulf ties (diaspora, energy)
- Israel relationship upgraded
- Balances Arab states and Israel

## Quad and Indo-Pacific
- Active Quad participant with US, Japan, Australia
- Indo-Pacific strategy engagement
- Balances against China while avoiding alliance

## Global South Leadership
- Positions India as developing world voice
- G20 presidency (2023)
- BRICS engagement
- Climate finance demands

## Key Characteristics

### Leadership Style
- Centralized decision-making
- Strong communication/media presence
- Nationalist mobilization
- Pragmatic foreign policy

### Strengths
- Electoral dominance
- Economic growth (world's fastest major economy)
- Demographic dividend
- Strategic location

### Challenges
- Hindu-Muslim tensions
- Press freedom criticism
- Inequality
- China border tensions
