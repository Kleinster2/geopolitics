# Andrei Belousov

Russian Minister of Defense since May 2024, replacing Sergei Shoigu. Economist by training rather than career military officer — a deliberate signal from [[Vladimir Putin|Putin]] that the priority of the role has shifted from military operations to defense-industrial mobilization. Previously First Deputy Prime Minister (2020-2024) and chief economic adviser to Putin.

## Significance

Political custodian of the [[Russian Drone Ecosystem]]. Under his tenure Russia has accelerated unmanned-systems and AI integration into military operations, treating civilian innovation as the primary feeder stock for military capability rather than relying on the legacy military-industrial base. [[Kateryna Bondar]] paraphrases his framework as satisfaction with "garage-level innovation" — the state's job is to find what works in the civilian sector and scale it, not to develop in-house.

The economist-not-soldier appointment is doctrinally meaningful. It reads as Putin choosing to optimize the war economy and the procurement architecture over operational command — an implicit acknowledgment that the binding constraint on Russian military performance is industrial throughput, not battlefield tactics.
