# ZALA

Russian unmanned aerial systems manufacturer. Founded as a private dual-use drone company; majority acquired (51%) by Kalashnikov Concern in February 2015. Produces military-grade ISR and loitering munition systems for the Russian armed forces, most notably the [[Lancet]] loitering munition that has been widely used in [[Ukraine Crisis|Ukraine]].

## Significance

Paradigmatic case in [[Kateryna Bondar]]'s framework of the [[Russian Drone Ecosystem|civil-military pipeline]]: a private dual-use firm absorbed into the state-owned military-industrial base, with civilian product lines feeding military scaling. The 2015 acquisition predates the full-scale Ukraine war but the model — civilian innovation absorbed by Kalashnikov, scaled across the front in Ukraine — is the template Bondar argues Russia is now applying systematically to its broader drone ecosystem.

## Key facts

- **Founded**: Russia, originally private
- **Acquired**: Kalashnikov Concern took 51% in February 2015 (Defense News)
- **Key products**: Lancet loitering munition (operational across Ukraine front); ZALA 421-series ISR drones
- **Status**: Subsidiary of state-owned Kalashnikov Concern; under Western sanctions
