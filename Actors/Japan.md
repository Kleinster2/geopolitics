# Japan

## Overview
World's third-largest economy and key US ally in Asia. Historically pacifist post-WWII, now undergoing significant remilitarization in response to China's rise and regional threats.

## Key Facts
- **Capital**: Tokyo
- **Population**: ~125 million (declining)
- **GDP**: ~$4.2 trillion
- **Military Budget**: ~$50 billion (increasing to 2% GDP)
- **Emperor**: Naruhito (ceremonial)
- **Prime Minister**: Sanae Takaichi (2025-)

## Geopolitical Position

### Strategic Shift
Post-WWII pacifism giving way to "normal" military posture:
- 2022 National Security Strategy: historic shift
- Doubling defense budget (1% to 2% GDP)
- Counter-strike capabilities
- Acquiring Tomahawk missiles

### US Alliance
- Cornerstone of regional security
- US bases in Japan (~54,000 troops)
- Nuclear umbrella
- Integrated operations

## Security Concerns
1. **China**: Senkaku Islands, Taiwan contingency, military modernization
2. **North Korea**: Missiles over/near Japan, nuclear threat
3. **Russia**: Northern Territories dispute
4. **Taiwan**: Critical sea lanes, potential conflict zone

## Strengths
- Advanced technology and manufacturing
- Strong economy (despite challenges)
- US alliance
- Capable military (Self-Defense Forces)
- Soft power (culture, aid)

## Vulnerabilities
- Demographic crisis (aging, shrinking population)
- Energy import dependence
- Limited strategic depth
- Historical tensions with neighbors
- Constitutional constraints (evolving)

## Economic Profile
- Export-oriented (autos, electronics)
- Technological leader
- Massive foreign investments
- Struggling with deflation/stagnation

## Current Issues
- Defense buildup execution
- Taiwan contingency planning
- South Korea reconciliation
- China economic decoupling
- Demographic decline management

## Japan-Philippines Defense Partnership (2025-2026)

### ACSA Agreement (Jan 2026)
- **Acquisition and Cross-Servicing Agreement** signed
- Allows exchange of logistical support, supplies, services
- Supports mutual military visits agreement (2025)

### Security Assistance
- 900 million yen ($5.7M) for FY2025 armed forces modernization
- Previously provided boats, coastal radar systems
- Mindanao aid: 1.63 billion yen for digital connectivity

### Strategic Context
- Both US allies, strengthening trilateral cooperation
- China tensions in East and South China Seas
- PM Takaichi's Taiwan comments angered Beijing
- Japan opposes "unilateral attempts to change status quo by force"

### Broader Cooperation
- Infrastructure development
- Economic security
- Semiconductors, supply chains
- Agriculture trade (Philippine bananas)
