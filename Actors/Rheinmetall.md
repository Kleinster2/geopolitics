# Rheinmetall

## Overview
Germany's largest defense contractor and a key player in Europe's rearmament. Specializes in ground systems, air defense, and ammunition. Major beneficiary of post-2022 defense spending surge. Critical to NATO's drone wall and European air defense initiatives.

## Key Facts
- **Headquarters**: Düsseldorf, Germany
- **Founded**: 1889
- **Employees**: ~30,000+
- **Revenue**: ~€7-8 billion (growing rapidly)
- **Stock**: Listed on Frankfurt Stock Exchange

## Core Products

### Ground Systems
- **Leopard 2** components and upgrades
- **Lynx** infantry fighting vehicle
- **Boxer** armored vehicle
- Military trucks and logistics vehicles

### Air Defense
- **Skyranger** - Radar-directed gun system for drone defense
- **Skynex** - Air defense system
- Ammunition for Gepard anti-aircraft tanks
- Key supplier for NATO drone wall concept

### Ammunition
- Artillery shells (critical Ukraine war supplier)
- Tank ammunition
- Small arms ammunition
- Largest European ammunition producer

### Electronics
- Sensors and optronics
- Fire control systems
- Simulation and training

## Strategic Significance

### Germany's Rearmament
- Primary beneficiary of €500B defense commitment
- Expanding production capacity rapidly
- Building new ammunition plants
- Key to Bundeswehr modernization

### Ukraine War Role
- Major supplier of ammunition to Ukraine
- Leopard 2 component support
- Air defense systems
- Expanding production for NATO stockpiles

### European Air Defense
- Skyranger central to NATO drone wall concept
- Gun-based systems offer cheaper "cost per kill" vs missiles
- Radar systems for detection

## Recent Developments

### Expansion
- Opening new factories for ammunition surge
- Hiring thousands of workers
- Joint ventures with other European firms
- Ukraine-based production discussions

### Stock Performance
- Share price up dramatically since 2022
- Market cap multiplied post-Ukraine invasion
- Among Europe's best-performing defense stocks

## Competitors
- **KNDS** (Germany/France) - Ground systems
- **BAE Systems** (UK) - Diversified
- **General Dynamics** (US) - Armor, vehicles
