# Key Geopolitical Thinkers

## Overview
Index of influential thinkers, scholars, and practitioners who have shaped geopolitical thought. Includes classical theorists, contemporary analysts, and public intellectuals. Understanding their frameworks and biases helps in consuming geopolitical analysis critically.

## Classical Geopolitical Theorists

### Halford Mackinder (1861-1947)
- **Contribution**: Heartland Theory
- **Key Work**: "The Geographical Pivot of History" (1904)
- **Concept**: Who controls the Heartland (Eurasia) commands the world
- **Influence**: Foundation of geopolitics as discipline

### Alfred Thayer Mahan (1840-1914)
- **Contribution**: Sea power theory
- **Key Work**: *The Influence of Sea Power Upon History* (1890)
- **Concept**: Naval dominance determines great power status
- **Influence**: US naval expansion, still relevant

### Nicholas Spykman (1893-1943)
- **Contribution**: Rimland Theory
- **Key Work**: *America's Strategy in World Politics* (1942)
- **Concept**: Rimland (coastal Eurasia) more important than Heartland
- **Influence**: US containment strategy

### Karl Haushofer (1869-1946)
- **Contribution**: German geopolitics (Geopolitik)
- **Concept**: Lebensraum, pan-regions
- **Legacy**: Tainted by Nazi association
- **Note**: Cautionary tale of geopolitics misuse

## International Relations Theorists

### Hans Morgenthau (1904-1980)
- **School**: Classical Realism
- **Key Work**: *Politics Among Nations* (1948)
- **Concept**: Power as central to international politics
- **Influence**: Father of American IR realism

### Kenneth Waltz (1924-2013)
- **School**: Neorealism/Structural Realism
- **Key Work**: *Theory of International Politics* (1979)
- **Concept**: Anarchy and system structure drive behavior
- **Influence**: Dominant IR theory for decades

### John Mearsheimer (1947-)
- **School**: Offensive Realism
- **Key Work**: *The Tragedy of Great Power Politics* (2001)
- **Concept**: Great powers maximize power, conflict inevitable
- **Current**: Controversial on Ukraine, China
- **Affiliation**: University of Chicago

### Robert Keohane (1941-)
- **School**: Neoliberal Institutionalism
- **Key Work**: *After Hegemony* (1984)
- **Concept**: Institutions enable cooperation
- **Influence**: Liberal IR theory

### Alexander Wendt (1958-)
- **School**: Constructivism
- **Key Work**: *Social Theory of International Politics* (1999)
- **Concept**: "Anarchy is what states make of it"
- **Influence**: Ideas and norms in IR

### Joseph Nye (1937-)
- **Concept**: Soft Power, Smart Power
- **Key Work**: *Soft Power* (2004)
- **Affiliation**: Harvard Kennedy School
- **Influence**: Shaped US foreign policy thinking

## Contemporary Strategists

### Henry Kissinger (1923-2023)
- **Role**: Scholar-practitioner
- **Key Works**: *Diplomacy*, *World Order*
- **Concepts**: Balance of power, realpolitik
- **Legacy**: Controversial but influential

### Zbigniew Brzezinski (1928-2017)
- **Role**: NSA under Carter, strategist
- **Key Work**: *The Grand Chessboard* (1997)
- **Concept**: Eurasian geopolitics
- **Influence**: US strategy toward Eurasia

### Graham Allison (1940-)
- **Concept**: Thucydides Trap
- **Key Work**: *Destined for War* (2017)
- **Affiliation**: Harvard Kennedy School
- **Focus**: US-China, great power war

### Robert Kagan (1958-)
- **Orientation**: Neoconservative
- **Key Works**: *The Jungle Grows Back*, *Of Paradise and Power*
- **Concept**: US hegemony necessity
- **Affiliation**: Brookings

## Asia Specialists

### Aaron Friedberg (1956-)
- **Focus**: China, US-China competition
- **Key Work**: *A Contest for Supremacy* (2011)
- **Affiliation**: Princeton
- **Orientation**: Hawkish on China

### Michael Pillsbury (1945-)
- **Focus**: China strategy
- **Key Work**: *The Hundred-Year Marathon* (2015)
- **Concept**: China's long-term strategy
- **Orientation**: China hawk

### Kishore Mahbubani (1948-)
- **Focus**: Asia's rise, Western decline
- **Key Works**: *Has the West Lost It?*, *The Asian 21st Century*
- **Perspective**: Singapore, Asian viewpoint
- **Affiliation**: National University of Singapore

### Hugh White (1953-)
- **Focus**: Australia-China-US triangle
- **Key Work**: *The China Choice* (2012)
- **Affiliation**: Australian National University
- **Argument**: Accommodation of China

## Political Risk/Public Intellectuals

### Ian Bremmer (1969-)
- **Role**: Political risk pioneer
- **Affiliation**: [[Eurasia Group]] founder
- **Concepts**: G-Zero, GZERO world
- **Platform**: Media presence, books

### George Friedman (1949-)
- **Role**: Geopolitical forecaster
- **Affiliation**: [[Stratfor]] founder, Geopolitical Futures
- **Key Work**: *The Next 100 Years*
- **Style**: Geographic determinism

### Peter Zeihan (1973-)
- **Focus**: Demographics, geography, deglobalization
- **Key Works**: *The End of the World Is Just the Beginning*
- **Style**: Provocative predictions
- **Concept**: US advantage, global fragmentation

### Robert Kaplan (1952-)
- **Focus**: Geography and conflict
- **Key Works**: *The Revenge of Geography*, *Balkan Ghosts*
- **Style**: Literary geopolitics
- **Affiliation**: Various think tanks

### Fareed Zakaria (1964-)
- **Role**: Public intellectual, journalist
- **Key Work**: *The Post-American World*
- **Platform**: CNN, Washington Post
- **Focus**: Global order, US role
