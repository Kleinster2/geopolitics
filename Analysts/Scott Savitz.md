# Scott Savitz

## Overview

Senior engineer at RAND Corporation who has led multiple studies for the US Navy on mine warfare. Served on-site for the Navy's mine warfare command and US Naval Forces Central Command (NAVCENT), including in Bahrain (2001-2003). Won the 2017 Mine Warfare Essay Contest from the Mine Warfare Association. Focuses on naval mine threats, countermeasures, and asymmetric maritime warfare.

## 2026 Iran Conflict — Mine Warfare Assessment

On the psychological dimension of mine warfare during the [[2026 Iran conflict]]: "You can have an effective minefield with zero mines, if others perceive it as posing a substantial threat" (FT, March 2026).

Noted that establishing a swept passageway through a minefield would require extended effort, with vessels clearing or using the routes moving slowly and predictably — making them highly vulnerable to Iranian missiles and drones. Even a small number of mines forces both commercial and military traffic to assess how much risk they are willing to take.
