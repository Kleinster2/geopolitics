# Howard Wang

## Overview
Political scientist at RAND focused on Chinese military doctrine and national mobilization. His 2026 Journal of Contemporary China article is the anchor source for the vault's [[Chinese total war doctrine]] note.

## Key Work
- "China Plans for 'Total War': Developing the Capacity to Defeat the 'Strong Enemy' of the United States" - RAND / Journal of Contemporary China, 2026.

## May 2026 Interview Frame
On [[Manoj Kewalramani]]'s *The Great Power Show*, Wang emphasized:
- Chinese assertiveness and risk aversion are both part of escalation control.
- Total war is peacetime civilian institutional mobilization, not an unlimited-war objective.
- INSS&C is broader than military-civil fusion because it directs civilian effects in parallel with PLA effects.
- The PLA's Ukraine lesson is that the United States used civilian systems to supplement Ukraine's war system.
- PLA purges are most plausibly real corruption enforcement, especially around Rocket Force and equipment quality, rather than pure factional politics.
