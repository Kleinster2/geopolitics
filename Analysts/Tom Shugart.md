# Tom Shugart

## Overview

Former US Navy submarine commander (25+ years service; commanded the nuclear-powered fast attack submarine USS Olympia, 2013-2016) and adjunct senior fellow in the Defense Program at the Center for a New American Security (CNAS). Also served on the Navy Staff overseeing the Columbia-class SSBN program and on the Joint Staff advising on nuclear strike planning. Focuses on naval strategy, undersea warfare, and maritime threats.

## 2026 Iran Conflict — Mine Warfare Assessment

On the asymmetry of mine warfare in the [[Strait of Hormuz]] during the [[2026 Iran conflict]]: "There is a huge asymmetry between mine laying and mine clearing, because one is much easier than the other" (FT, March 2026).

The comment underscores why Iran's mine threat persists even after the US destroyed 30+ mine-laying vessels — laying mines requires only basic platforms (trawlers, dhows, submarines), while clearing them demands specialized equipment the US has allowed to atrophy.
