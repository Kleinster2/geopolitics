# Ryan Bohl

## Overview

Senior Middle East and North Africa Analyst at [[RANE]] (Risk Assistance Network + Exchange), where he focuses on strategic, demographic, social, diplomatic and political issues across the MENA region. Also serves as Middle East and North African Analyst at Stratfor (now RANE's Worldview platform). Specializes in GCC state behavior, the Levant, Syria, Turkey, Israel, Yemen, and US Middle Eastern policy.

## Analytical style

Bohl is operationally detailed and empirically grounded. Avoids partisanship — focuses on structural drivers, historical parallels, and scenario mapping rather than advocacy. Draws frequently on regional comparisons (Saddam's Iraq, Gaddafi's Libya, Soviet collapse) to frame analysis. Lived experience in the Gulf gives him granular insight into GCC politics and intra-Gulf rivalries (Saudi-UAE divergences, etc.).

Distinctive contributions:
- **Regime resilience framing**: Emphasizes that authoritarian regimes often outlast expectations — compares Iran to Cuba, Venezuela. "It relies on force rather than legitimacy."
- **Threat deflation**: Pushes back on inflated threat assessments — compared Iran's missile capabilities to pre-war perceptions of Russia's army and Hezbollah's rocket arsenal.
- **Post-war divergence mapping**: Identifies splits that will emerge among nominal allies after shared threats recede (Saudi stability vs UAE/Israel chaos preference re: Iran).

## Notable appearances in vault

### [[2026 Iran conflict]] — March 1, 2026

[Thoughtful Money interview](https://www.youtube.com/live/vTlW011fx9Q) with Adam Taggart. Key assessments:

- Operation Epic Fury was joint US-Israeli from the start (unlike June 2025 where Israel led). Campaign planned for up to 30 days.
- Iran's regime is a **network, not a personalized dictatorship** — decapitation disrupts but doesn't collapse it. IRGC commanders have standing autonomous orders. "Rattled but united."
- Iran's retaliation weaker than feared: Gulf multi-layered air defenses achieving ~85-90% interception rates. "10-foot-tall perception" parallel to Russia pre-Ukraine.
- Post-war Iran: five scenarios — Hezbollah model (restrained), Houthi model (refocused), Saddam model (hunker down), reformist path (best case), nuclear breakout (danger).
- Saudi-UAE cleavage: Saudis want stable Iran (even Islamic Republic), UAE/Israel prefer broken Iran. Will diverge over 1-5 years.
- Trump motivated by legacy — "war of choice." Domestic support weak, "far less popular than Bush invading Iraq in 2003."
- No WW3 mechanism: no mutual defense treaties between Russia/China and Iran. "Nice-to-have, not must-have."
- Energy: scorched earth unlikely, but price squeeze and selective strikes on Gulf infrastructure expected.
