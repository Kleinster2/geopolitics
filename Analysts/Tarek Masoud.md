# Tarek Masoud

## Overview

Tarek Masoud is the Ford Foundation Professor of Democracy and Governance at Harvard Kennedy School and director of the Middle East Initiative at the Belfer Center for Science and International Affairs. His work focuses on Arab political economy, Islamist movements, and Gulf state dynamics.

## Key Facts

- **Position**: Professor of Public Policy, Harvard Kennedy School; Director, Middle East Initiative
- **Affiliation**: [[Harvard Kennedy School]] / Belfer Center
- **Focus**: Arab politics, Gulf states, Islamist movements, political economy of the Middle East
- **Methodological signature**: Combines regional sourcing (Arabic-language primary sources, direct conversations with Gulf officials) with comparative political analysis

## Analysis — 2026 Iran conflict

### CNN GPS Gulf attitudes briefing (April 12, 2026)

On CNN's [[Fareed Zakaria]] GPS (April 12) Masoud pushed back on reporting that Saudi Arabia's Crown Prince [[Mohammed bin Salman]] had helped push Trump into the war alongside [[Benjamin Netanyahu]].

**Disputing the "MBS pushed Trump" narrative:**

> "There was a lot of reporting in the leadup or once the war started when the narrative was that President Trump had been snookered into this war by Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu — that in fact the Saudi Crown Prince, who's the de facto leader of that country, had also been pushing the President to get into this war. My understanding from talking to some of my Saudi sources and talking to Gulf sources more broadly is they actually did not want a war."

**Gulf opinion spectrum on Iran (Masoud's mapping):**

| Pole | Country | Posture |
|------|---------|---------|
| Pro-Iran accommodation | [[Oman]] | Mediator; has "almost sounded a bit like the Iranians' lawyer" |
| Middle | [[Saudi Arabia]], [[UAE]], [[Qatar]], Kuwait | Historically seeking to lower temperature; 2023 China-brokered Iran-Saudi normalization reduced Houthi attacks |
| Anti-Iran | [[Bahrain]] | Large Shia population (~50%) that Iranians periodically incite; no diplomatic relations for 10+ years |

**The post-October 7 / wartime shift:**

The spectrum has compressed toward the anti-Iran end. "Countries like Qatar, UAE — which had a lot of trade and traffic with Iran — they have received a lot of Iranian fire ostensibly because they host US military bases. So everybody's kind of shifted towards the anti-Iranian end of the spectrum."

**Two Gulf red lines — outcomes they want to avoid:**

1. **Iranian control of the [[Strait of Hormuz]] + retained missile/drone capacity** — continuing threat to Gulf states
2. **Iranian failed state** — civil war, refugee flows, warlord rule, narcotics production "like you had in Syria"

Masoud's alarm: Trump's April 7 "joint venture" comment on Hormuz tolls with the Ayatollah, and advisers suggesting arming Iranians/Kurds to topple the regime, signal the US may be comfortable with either of the two outcomes Gulf states most fear.

> "When you have a president who says 'maybe we'll have the Strait of Hormuz be a joint venture between me and the Ayatollah,' makes you think, could America be okay with the first bad case scenario? Or when you have advisers or people adjacent to the current administration saying things like we're going to arm Iranians or we're going to arm the Kurds to bring down the regime — you think it looks like America could accommodate itself to these two outcomes that are absolutely anathema to most of the Gulf countries."

**Shia demographics at risk (Masoud's figures):**

- [[Saudi Arabia]] Eastern Province: ~30% Shia
- [[Bahrain]]: ~50% Shia
- Iranian failed-state scenario could produce domestic instability in both

**The two Gulf camps toward the US (pre-existing cleavage now deepening):**

1. **Pro-American camp** — leadership of most Gulf states; remember 1990-1991 Kuwait defense; "want to put all of their eggs in the American basket" (military, trade, institutional design)
2. **Non-aligned camp** — exists in every Arab country; argues for diversification away from US client status, toward China and others. Masoud: "that latter camp is definitely ascendant."

**On Trump specifically — the Gulf rethink:**

> "Nowhere on this planet except for Trump Tower greeted Trump's victory in his second term with greater joy than in the Gulf. They felt we understand him, he respects us. And now when they see the degree of chaos and also the degree, the lack of concern that the Trump administration seems to have with their views, I think they're starting to rethink. And I think in the future they'll try to diversify their relationships within [beyond] the United States as well."

**Liability frame — US bases as target-magnet:**

Masoud noted the argument gaining traction among Iran-friendly Gulf voices: the US bases, intended as security guarantees, are the reason Gulf states are "taking all of this fire." America-as-asset narrative eroding into America-as-liability narrative. Counter-argument: security situation would likely have been worse without US guarantees — but that counterfactual is hard to prove politically.
