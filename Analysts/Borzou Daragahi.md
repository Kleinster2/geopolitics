# Borzou Daragahi

## Overview

Iranian-American foreign correspondent and analyst focused on the greater [[Middle East]], North Africa, and [[Europe]], with long-running coverage of [[Iran]], Iraq, [[Turkey]], Egypt, [[Syria]], Lebanon, Libya, Afghanistan, and regional conflict politics. Daragahi is currently associated with *The Independent* as an international correspondent and publishes the newsletter [[badlands]] at [borzou.substack.com](https://borzou.substack.com).

Daragahi's value for the vault is field-informed regional interpretation: he combines wire/newspaper reporting background with current newsletter commentary on wars, regime politics, militia networks, and the second-order effects of Middle East conflict.

## Key Facts

- Position: International correspondent, *The Independent*
- Newsletter: [[badlands]] - Substack focused on global affairs commentary and intelligence
- Former roles: Los Angeles Times Baghdad bureau chief; correspondent or contributor for *Financial Times*, BuzzFeed News, Associated Press, *Foreign Policy*, *The Atlantic*, and other outlets
- Institutional affiliation: Former nonresident senior fellow, [[Atlantic Council]] Middle East Programs
- Recognition: Pulitzer Prize finalist three times for international reporting connected to Iraq and Iran coverage
- Geography: Based in Istanbul in recent public bios; prior reporting stints include Tehran, Baghdad, Beirut, and Cairo
- Languages: Persian plus reported working knowledge of several regional / European languages

## Analytical Use

- Middle East conflict interpretation: useful for connecting battlefield events to local political systems, militia economics, and regime-survival incentives
- Iran coverage: especially relevant to [[Iran Protests 2025-2026]], [[Iran Nuclear Program]], and the [[2026 Iran conflict]] cluster, where local political texture matters as much as official statements
- Turkey / Syria / Lebanon lens: valuable for tracking how Iranian, Israeli, Turkish, Kurdish, and Arab-state interests collide across multiple theaters
- Newsletter signal: [[badlands]] is best treated as a curated analyst source rather than a neutral wire feed; preserve attribution when importing Daragahi's judgments

## Caveats

- Daragahi writes as a journalist-analyst, not an academic theorist. Use his work for reporting-driven interpretation and local/regional context, not as a settled framework without corroboration.
- The Substack is partly paywalled. When ingesting, distinguish public text from subscriber-only commentary and avoid laundering unattributed claims into vault facts.
- Because much of his comparative advantage is source-rich regional reporting, unnamed-source claims should be recorded cautiously under the vault's verifiability rules.
