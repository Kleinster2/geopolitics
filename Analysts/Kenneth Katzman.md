# Kenneth Katzman

Senior Fellow at [[The Soufan Center]] and Senior Non-Resident Fellow at the Gulf International Forum. Previously spent 30+ years as Specialist in Middle Eastern Affairs at the Congressional Research Service (CRS), where he was one of the most-cited analysts on Iran and the Persian Gulf. Before CRS, served as a CIA analyst (1985-1989) focused on leadership dynamics in Iran, Iraq, and Gulf states.

## Background

- Ph.D. in Political Science, New York University (1991)
- CIA analyst (May 1985 – June 1989) — prepared written reports on Middle East leadership dynamics for US policymakers
- CRS Specialist in Middle Eastern Affairs (~1990 – late 2022) — briefed Members of Congress on US policy toward Iran, Persian Gulf states, Iraq, and Afghanistan
- Retired from CRS late 2022

## Key publications

- "The Warriors of Islam: Iran's Revolutionary Guard" (Westview Press, 1993) — based on his Ph.D. dissertation; early English-language study of IRGC organizational structure
- Expert working papers for the Rumsfeld Commission on ballistic missile capabilities of Iran and Iraq (1998)
- Atlantic Council monographs on US sanctions on Iran (1999, 2010)
- CRS reports on Iran sanctions, Iran-US policy, Persian Gulf security (numerous, spanning decades)

## Current affiliations

- Senior Fellow, [[The Soufan Center]]
- Senior Non-Resident Fellow, Gulf International Forum
- Senior Research Adviser, Global Insights Group
- Contributor, [[Middle East Institute]] and Wilson Center

## Expertise

Katzman's analytical depth spans the full Iran portfolio: [[IRGC]] structure and operations, sanctions architecture, nuclear program, regional proxy networks, and US legislative proposals on Iran policy. His CRS tenure made him a primary institutional resource for congressional understanding of Iran — rare continuity across administrations from Reagan through Biden.
