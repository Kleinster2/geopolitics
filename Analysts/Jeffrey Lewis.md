# Jeffrey Lewis

Director of the East Asia Nonproliferation Program at the James Martin Center for Nonproliferation Studies (CNS), Middlebury Institute of International Studies at Monterey. Pioneer of open-source intelligence (OSINT) in nuclear analysis. Creator of the Arms Control Wonk blog and podcast.

## Key assessments

### 2026 Iran conflict — escalate or negotiate

Assessed that Iran's diminished but persistent attack capability forces a strategic choice on the Trump administration:

> "I think the regime will continue to conduct infrequent but steady missile and especially drone attacks. And then the Trump people are going to have a big choice. Do they escalate by getting involved on the ground? Or do they negotiate a settlement that allows them to declare victory?"

The assessment identifies the core dilemma: air power alone cannot stop Iran's residual capability (especially Shahed drones, which require minimal infrastructure), but ground involvement dramatically escalates US commitment and casualties.

*Source: Bloomberg (March 10-11, 2026)*
