# Martin Plaut

## Overview
Senior Research Fellow, Institute of Commonwealth Studies, University of London. Born 1950, Cape Town. BBC World Service (1984-2013), became Africa editor. Previously Labour Party secretary on Africa/Middle East; former Associate Fellow at Chatham House. Has advised FCO, US State Department, European Parliament. Key publications: *Understanding Ethiopia's Tigray War* (Hurst, 2023, with Sarah Vaughan); *Understanding Eritrea: Inside Africa's Most Repressive State* (Hurst, 2016). Active blog at martinplaut.com.

## Key Positions
- The Nile is the foundational driver of Egypt-Ethiopia rivalry: "The Nile is at the heart of everything, when you go back to the beginning"
- Egypt has sponsored Eritrean separatists since the 1950s specifically to prevent Ethiopian control of Nile headwaters
- This historical depth means current tensions are not new but an intensification of century-old dynamics
