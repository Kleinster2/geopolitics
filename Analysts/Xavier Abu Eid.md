# Xavier Abu Eid

Chilean-Palestinian political scientist and Communications Adviser to the [[PLO]] Negotiations Affairs Department, specializing in settlements, Jerusalem, and Christian issues. Regular Al Jazeera contributor (op-eds and Inside Story panelist). Was adviser to the late Saeb Erekat. Part of PLO negotiating team since ~2007.

## Background

- Born Santiago, Chile (1985), to a Palestinian family from Beit Jala. Grandson of 1948 Nakba diaspora.
- BA Political Science, Diego Portales University (Chile)
- MA Diplomatic Studies, Leicester University (UK)
- Master's in Mediation and Peace Processes, ETH Zurich
- PhD candidate, theology/religion/peace studies, Trinity College Dublin
- Played for Club Deportivo Palestino (Chilean football club founded by Palestinian diaspora)
- Spokesperson, General Union of Palestine Students (while in Chile)
- Languages: English, Spanish, Arabic

## Key positions

### Gaza ceasefire and Mladenov appointment (January 2026)

On [[Nikolay Mladenov]]'s appointment as High Representative for Gaza, acknowledged his competence while flagging perceived bias:

> "He was always seen as someone very serious, someone who knew the files very well. He was not the kind of envoy or diplomat that depended too much on advisers or people telling him what to say."

But added: "He cared about Palestinians, but he cared more about Israelis."

*Source: CNN (January 18, 2026)*

### West Bank de facto annexation (2025-2026)

Described Israeli land registration policies as "de facto annexation" that will "profoundly reshape the civil and legal landscape" of the West Bank.

*Source: Al Jazeera (February 15, 2026)*

### France and Palestinian statehood (April 2025)

Argued France's recognition of Palestinian statehood was insufficient without concrete policy changes — diplomatic recognition alone does not alter the balance of power on the ground.

*Source: Al Jazeera opinion (April 13, 2025)*

## Publications

- "Rooted in Palestine: Palestinian Christians and the Struggle for National Liberation 1917-2004" (Dar al-Kalima University Press, 2022) — documents the role of Palestinian Christians in national liberation, from WWI through the post-1967 occupation period.

## Analytical profile

Abu Eid occupies an unusual position: formal PLO institutional access combined with public-facing commentary. His Chilean-Palestinian background gives him a Latin American diaspora perspective rare among English-language Palestine analysts. His specialization in Palestinian Christian issues fills a gap in coverage that often treats the Palestinian national movement as monolithically Muslim.
