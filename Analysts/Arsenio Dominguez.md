# Arsenio Dominguez

## Overview

Secretary-General of the International Maritime Organization (IMO), the UN specialized agency that sets rules for international shipping. Panamanian official.

## 2026 Iran Conflict — Hormuz Assessment

Told the FT (March 17, 2026) that naval escorts through the [[Strait of Hormuz]] will "not 100 per cent guarantee" the safety of ships and that military protection is "not a long-term or sustainable solution."

On geography: the strait's deep-water shipping lanes are just 2 nautical miles (~4km) per direction, bounded on the Iranian side by mountains that favor attackers who "can strike ships from on high with little notice."

Raised alarm over ships stranded in the Gulf running out of food, water, and fuel. Port access limited because facilities are being targeted. Called on shipowners "not to sail and not to put seafarers at risk."

Called IMO extraordinary council for March 19-20 to discuss operational risks and de-escalation.

"We are collateral damage of a conflict when the root causes have nothing to do with shipping."
