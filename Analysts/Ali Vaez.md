# Ali Vaez

## Overview
Iranian-American analyst and track-two diplomacy practitioner specializing in Iran's nuclear program, US-Iran relations, and sanctions policy. Directs the International Crisis Group's Iran project and serves as Senior Adviser to Crisis Group's president. Unusual background: trained as a scientist (PhD in biomedicine and nuclear physics) before pivoting to policy — gives him technical credibility on Iran's nuclear program that most policy analysts lack.

## Major Works

### *How Sanctions Work: Iran and the Impact of Economic Warfare* (2024)
Stanford University Press. Co-authored with Narges Bajoghli, [[Vali Nasr]], and Djavad Salehi-Isfahani.

Core thesis: after four decades, sanctions strengthened the Iranian state, impoverished the population, increased state repression, and escalated Iran's military posture toward the US — the opposite of intended effects.

## Analytical Framework

### On sanctions
- Four decades of evidence show sanctions empowered the Iranian state apparatus rather than weakening it
- Population bears the costs; regime adapts and uses sanctions to consolidate control
- Sanctions escalated rather than deterred Iran's military posture

### On track-two diplomacy
- Led Crisis Group's efforts bridging gaps between Iran and P5+1, contributing to the 2015 JCPOA
- Maintains direct access to Iranian officials and Western policymakers
- Substantive practitioner, not just commentator

### On the Islamic Republic's structure
- New supreme leader will be functionally dependent on IRGC — "not going to be supreme"
- Real power in governance, not title: the system distributes authority across multiple power centers
- Survival capacity built into institutional design

## 2026 Iran Conflict Analysis (March 8, Channel 4 News)

Key arguments on Day 8:

**War of attrition logic:**
- Both sides believe they can force the other to blink — "very hard to be optimistic about any exit ramps"
- Iran pacing itself for attrition; expects global economic/energy pressure to force Trump to seek an exit in ~2 weeks
- "If the regime is still standing in Iran, that is a victory for them"
- Trump believes infrastructure targeting will weaken regime to collapse

**Infrastructure escalation:**
- Water and oil are the two critical resources — targeting them "would escalate very quickly"
- Current disruption is primarily to shipments, not production infrastructure
- If production capacity is destroyed: "scorched earth at the end of this crisis" requiring years to recover

**Civilian casualties:**
- Drew "terrible parallel" with Gaza — same human shields arguments being used
- Iran has 92 million people and few shelters
- Historical warning: 1953 coup → 1979 revolution line. Generational blowback from civilian deaths.

**Supreme leader succession:**
- Mojtaba Khamenei expected but won't be operationally present — "in a bunker"
- Power with Ali Larijani (national security adviser, day-to-day affairs) and Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf (parliament speaker, war effort)
- Both former IRGC commanders with long governance experience
