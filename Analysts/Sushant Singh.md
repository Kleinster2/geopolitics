# Sushant Singh

## Overview
Lecturer in South Asian Studies at Yale University, consulting editor at *Caravan* magazine (India), and a former Indian military officer with two decades of service. Writes on Indian foreign policy, civil-military relations, South Asian security, and the politics of the [[Narendra Modi|Modi]] era. Among the sharpest English-language analysts of how Pakistan's diplomatic positioning during the [[2026 Iran conflict]] has reshaped the Modi government's regional strategy.

## Background
- Two decades of service in the Indian Army before transitioning to journalism
- Currently lecturer in South Asian Studies, Yale University
- Consulting editor, *Caravan* (Delhi-based long-form magazine)
- Former associate editor at *The Indian Express*
- Writes for *Foreign Policy*, *Caravan*, and other international outlets

## Core Argument (April 2026)

In a *Foreign Policy* essay and *One Decision* podcast interview (April 23, 2026), Singh argued that [[Pakistan]]'s emergence as the diplomatic broker between Washington and Tehran has "completely stunned" the Modi government and exposed the unraveling of a decade-long Indian foreign policy of diplomatically isolating Islamabad.

> "Mr. Modi's government has been completely stunned by this turnaround that Pakistan has executed over the last one year." — Singh

> "Mr. Modi is trying to be friends to everyone, and I think at the times of a major global crisis, that is the point at which you realize that you are friends with no one." — Singh

### Key claims
- Pakistan has become "a new kind of Geneva or a new kind of Switzerland which nobody ever expected"
- [[Asim Munir|Field Marshal Munir]] continues a Pakistani military tradition of bold, risky bets ([[Ayub Khan|Ayub]], Yahya, Zia, Musharraf) — Singh ranks the current play as comparable in audacity, and high-risk
- Modi's domestic standing depends on the narrative that he is "this great world leader" — when that narrative cracks, political damage follows
- For the first time, the BJP-led government lost a parliamentary bill (constitutional amendment) — Singh reads this as an indicator of political weakening flowing from foreign-policy embarrassment
- The [[Quad]] is "essentially dead" under [[Donald Trump|Trump]] 2.0 — no summit, no India visit, no Indo-Pacific China-balancing impetus from Washington
- India therefore must accommodate [[China]] (more flights, trade, meetings) because external balancing is unavailable

### India's three vulnerabilities (Singh framework)
Singh's most quotable analytical contribution: India has three structural dependencies that constrain its diplomatic room:
1. **United States** — strategic benevolence required for "great-power status"
2. **Russia** — 60-70% of military equipment is Russian-origin; spare-parts and ammunition leverage means Moscow can squeeze Delhi at will
3. **China** — extensive trade dependency

> "When everything is going hunky dory, you can be friends with everyone... but it's only in a time of a crisis that you realize that you can't afford to be friends with everyone." — Singh

See [[India's Three Vulnerabilities]].

## On India-Israel Relations
Singh confirmed and detailed the public deepening of [[India]]-[[Israel]] ties under [[Narendra Modi|Modi]]:
- Diplomatic recognition since 1950s; full ties since early 1990s
- Israeli intelligence/military support to India during 1971 and 1976 wars (per Singh)
- Post-Mumbai 2008: Israeli urban-security expertise consulted
- Mr. Modi visited Israel for two days immediately before the Israeli "decapitation strike" on Iran (June 2025); publicly backed [[Benjamin Netanyahu|Netanyahu]]
- India has not condemned Israeli actions in [[Gaza]], [[Lebanon]], or the Iran strike
- "[[Gautam Adani|Adani]] has invested money in Haifa Port" — concrete economic anchoring of the relationship
- Singh ranks Israel + UAE as India's two strongest regional partners

## On Modi's US Relationship
Singh detailed how the Trump 2.0 reset has cooled what was a personally-friendly Modi-Trump relationship:
- Modi held Trump rallies in Houston (2019, 100k diaspora) and Ahmedabad (cricket stadium)
- Indian government openly bet that Trump 2.0 would be friendlier than Biden — bet has not paid off
- India received among the highest US tariffs
- Trump has "more than 100 times" claimed personal credit for the May 2025 [[India-Pakistan Four-Day War (May 2025)|India-Pakistan ceasefire]] — a claim Delhi flatly rejects
- Modi has been actively avoiding shared multilateral venues (APEC, etc.) to avoid being publicly contradicted
- Per Singh: Indian elite trust in the US — "you cannot trust the Americans anymore" — has cratered, comparable to European disillusionment

> "Whoever replaces President Trump would have to really go out of his way to convince the Indian people that America is still a trustworthy country." — Singh

## On Pakistan's Mediator Calculus
Singh framed Munir's diplomatic gambit as classic Pakistani military risk-taking:
- Pakistan was "in deep trouble" — economic distress, [[Imran Khan]] imprisoned, Afghanistan border conflict, Baluchistan / Khyber Pakhtunkhwa terrorism
- Mediator role offers domestic political resurrection for Munir vis-à-vis Khan
- "Risk of failure is huge... he may completely fall on his face... but the rewards are also going to be high"
- The whole edifice depends on Munir's personal access to Trump — if Trump turns, Pakistan collapses back to prior position
- See [[Pakistan as US-Iran Mediator]]

## "One Thing to Watch"
Singh's answer: Will India and Pakistan resume direct dialogue?

His worry — and the rationale for the [[2026 Hungary parliamentary election|whole interview's strategic stakes]] — is that two nuclear-armed states without [[Cuban Missile Crisis|Cuban-Missile-style]] guardrails sit one minute of missile flight apart. The May 2025 conflict exposed how thin the escalation buffer is. Singh wants back-channels, third-party mediation, anything that creates a structural mechanism — none currently exists.
