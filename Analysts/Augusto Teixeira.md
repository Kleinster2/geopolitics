## Notable Analysis

### New START Expiration (February 2026)
On the security dilemma:
- Actors have incentives to increase offensive and defensive capabilities
- This decreases others' security perception
- Result: everyone ends up less secure
- Particularly dangerous for non-nuclear powers

On extended deterrence:
- US nuclear umbrella over Europe is now "torn" (rasgado)
- Trump administration undermined confidence in extended deterrence
- Europeans doubt US would aid them in nuclear escalation scenario

On European nuclear limitations:
- France and UK have nuclear weapons but lack the nuclear triad
- Their arsenals small compared to Russia (world's largest)
- Russia has sophisticated delivery systems (hypersonic, multiple reentry vehicles)
- No proven countermeasures exist

On structural uncertainty:
- "Primacy of structural uncertainty from systemic anarchy"
- New START expiration adds fuel to uncertainty fire
- Great powers still communicate informally on nuclear issues
- But formal mechanisms collapsing since pandemic

On regime collapse pattern:
- Open Skies denounced
- INF denounced
- New START now expiring
- Trump betting heavily on bringing China to table, even if New START lapses
- "Admirável mundo novo" (Brave new world)
