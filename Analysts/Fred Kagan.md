# Fred Kagan

Senior fellow at the **American Enterprise Institute** and director of the **Critical Threats Project**, which monitors open-source intelligence on Iran and other threat environments at a professional level. Longtime scholar of military science, history, and Iran.

## Notable positions

### Iran regime resilience (Commentary, ~2017-2018)
Published a cover story in *Commentary* cautioning against overconfidence in maximum pressure bringing about regime change during Trump's first term. Core argument: Iran is not the Soviet Union at the end of the 1980s — it is more like China on the run-up to Tiananmen Square, in the sense that "these guys will gun everyone down in the streets." The Soviets had a collapse of spirit and self-confidence embodied in Gorbachev; Iran's rulers showed no such weakness.

### Updated assessment (March 3, 2026)
Kagan revised his assessment significantly in a *School of War* podcast appearance (Ep. 282). Key shift: "The situation has changed. This is a much weaker Islamic Republic." Factors driving the revision:
- Axis of resistance largely stripped away (Hezbollah degraded, Hamas destroyed)
- Multiple protest waves have become a structural threat to regime survival
- Israeli campaign eliminated most of the upper echelon of the IRGC and political leadership
- Khamenei killed — Iran without a supreme leader for the first time since 1989

Still cautioned: "the likeliest outcome of collapse of this regime in a campaign of this variety is a large-scale civil war in a country with a population of 90 million people."
