# Rana Foroohar

## Overview

Global business columnist and associate editor at the [[Financial Times]], and CNN global economic analyst. Specializes in the intersection of geopolitics and economics — what she terms "geoeconomics." Author of multiple books on American economic decline and the financialization of the economy. One of the most prominent voices applying financial-transmission analysis to geopolitical conflicts.

## Key Facts

- **Position**: Global business columnist and associate editor, Financial Times; CNN global economic analyst
- **Education**: BA, Barnard College, Columbia University
- **Notable books**: *Makers and Takers: The Rise of Finance and the Fall of American Business* (2016); *Don't Be Evil: The Case Against Big Tech* (2019); *Homecoming: The Path to Prosperity in a Post-Global World* (2022)
- **Focus areas**: Geoeconomics, supply chain restructuring, industrial policy, financialization, US-China economic competition

## Analytical Approach

Foroohar's distinctive contribution is mapping the financial transmission mechanisms through which geopolitical events create economic consequences — and how those economic consequences feed back into constraining geopolitical actors. This is not market commentary; it is the application of financial-system structural analysis to foreign policy.

Key frameworks:
- Inflation cascade analysis (first-order energy → second-order food/fertilizer → third-order upstream materials)
- Bond market vulnerability as a constraint on state action
- Supply chain geography as a geopolitical variable
- The "homecoming" thesis: post-globalization restructuring creating new economic geography

## On the 2026 Iran conflict (March 9, 2026)

In "Iran war will leave a complex geoeconomic legacy" (FT, March 9), Foroohar argued the war's trajectory would be determined less by military operations than by supply chains, inflation, and bond markets. See [[2026 Iran conflict -- analyst perspectives]] for full analysis.

Key positions:
- Cited Luke Gromen's formulation: "Iran does not have to defeat the US military; it just has to defeat the UST market."
- Mapped the inflation cascade: fuel → food (fertilizer) → upstream materials (petrochemicals, plastics, aluminium).
- Identified bond market structural vulnerability: Treasury bills at 15% of debt stock, hedge funds as price-sensitive holders who liquidate during crises.
- Flagged China's shipping/port leverage as a potential force multiplier for Iran's disruption.
- Predicted the war would last "longer than you would wish" — ceasefire came on Day 39 (vs. Trump's initial "short war" promise).
