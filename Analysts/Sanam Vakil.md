## Analytical contributions

### 2026 Iran conflict — Iraq 1991 parallel (FT, March 14, 2026)

Vakil argues the most likely outcome of the war is not regime change but an **Iraq 1991 scenario**: Iran militarily defeated and economically debilitated, yet still governed by a reconstituted form of the same regime. Key arguments:

- Washington's objectives are incoherent — nuclear capabilities, military weakening, and regime change are "very different goals that point to very different endgames"
- IRGC plays the same role as Saddam's Republican Guard: combining military, economic power, and ideological commitment to regime survival
- New US intelligence estimates suggest military success unlikely to produce political change in Tehran
- The Iraq 1991 precedent produced a decade of containment (sanctions, no-fly zones, inspections, periodic strikes) that resolved nothing and ultimately culminated in the 2003 invasion
- Iran's civilians will bear the costs: currency collapse, hyperinflation, migration flows
- Frozen conflicts drift back toward war — the 1991-2003 cycle warns that containment creates pressure to "finish the job"
