# Brandon Weichert

## Overview
Conservative geopolitical analyst and author specializing in Iran and space policy. Regular commentator on NTD and other outlets. Author of "The Shadow War: Iran's Quest for Supremacy."

## Key Facts
- **Position**: Geopolitical analyst, columnist
- **Outlets**: The American Spectator, Washington Times, NTD
- **Book**: "The Shadow War: Iran's Quest for Supremacy"
- **Focus**: Iran, space policy, great power competition

## Analytical Framework
- Hawkish perspective on Iran
- Emphasizes military risk assessment and operational constraints
- Concerned with post-conflict planning gaps
- Skeptical of US readiness for sustained Middle East operations

## Analysis: US-Iran Strike Readiness (February 1, 2026)

From NTD interview, February 1, 2026:

### On Strike Imminence
- Assesses US and Israel are "primed to attack" Iran
- Claims Trump administration "wants to go" but keeps pausing
- Attributes delays to insufficient air defense assets in region
- "Every time they get close to pulling the trigger, they pull back"

### On Military Risks (Claimed - Unverified)
- Claims carrier strike group positioned "behind Oman" due to safety concerns
- Claims Iranian anti-ship ballistic missiles are "serious threats" to US carrier
- Claims Chinese satellite intelligence assisting Iranian targeting in real time
- Assesses Saudis have said they won't assist in shooting down Iranian missiles

**Caveat**: These operational claims cannot be independently verified.

### On Venezuela Comparison
Key analytical contribution - why Iran differs from Venezuela:
- **Geography**: Iran is larger, more expansive, targets spread out
- **Ideology**: Iranian leadership "far more ideologically strict or extreme"
- **Air defenses**: Iranian air defenses "better than what Venezuela had"
- **Post-conflict**: No identified successor who would cooperate like Delscy Rodriguez
- Rejects "lop off the head and take over like a parasite" approach

### On Post-Conflict Risks
- Invokes "Pottery Barn rule" - "you break it, you own it"
- Warns Iran could become "the new Lebanon" - multi-sided civil war for decades
- Doubts protesters have "capacity to formulate a post-regime system"
- Warns sectarian conflict or reversion to authoritarianism likely
- "Some people in the United States and Israel would love to see weak countries all over the Middle East... but ultimately, that will breed terrorism"

### On Talks
- Dismisses US-Iran talks as "another ruse"
- Cites prior pattern: talks used to "lull Iranians into a false sense of security" before 12-day war
- Believes Trump is "buying time" while building up assets

### Assessment Framework
- "Strike package waiting" - believes attack posture real but constrained
- Three blocking factors: US military caution, Israeli concerns, Arab non-cooperation
- Once any of these change, "we will go"
