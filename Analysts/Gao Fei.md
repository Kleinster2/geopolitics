# Gao Fei

## Overview

Gao Fei is a Chinese foreign-policy academic associated with China Foreign Affairs University. People's Daily described him as the university's president in the May 27, 2026 article on [[Constructive strategic stability]]. The university's English leadership page, published in 2016, lists him as Vice President and Academic Dean, so the current title should be treated as source-dated rather than assumed from the older English page.

## May 2026 strategic-stability article

Gao's May 27, 2026 People's Daily article is useful because it gives an establishment-friendly explanation of the new US-China formula after Donald Trump's May 13-15 state visit to Beijing.

Core claims:

- The US has recognized, after multiple rounds of contestation, that containment and suppression of China do not work.
- The two sides should treat constructive strategic stability as the new positioning for the relationship over the next three years and beyond.
- The relationship should preserve cooperation as the mainstay, keep competition bounded, make differences controllable, and maintain peace as foreseeable.
- Practical channels include a Trade Council, an Investment Council, agricultural market-access resolution, reciprocal tariff reductions, and discussion of Middle East, Ukraine, and Korean Peninsula issues.
- The long-run horizon is a new type of major-country relationship, connected to the shared-future / global-governance language of Xi-era diplomacy.

The article is not neutral analysis. It is a state-media argument for why China can seek dialogue without framing that dialogue as weakness. That makes it valuable as a signal of official rhetorical intent.
