# Trita Parsi

## Overview
Executive Vice President of the Quincy Institute for Responsible Statecraft. Leading voice for diplomatic engagement with Iran and restraint in US Middle East policy. Founder of the National Iranian American Council (NIAC). Author of multiple books on US-Iran relations.

## Key Facts
- **Position**: Executive Vice President, Quincy Institute for Responsible Statecraft
- **Previous**: Founder, National Iranian American Council (NIAC)
- **Focus**: Iran, US-Iran relations, Middle East diplomacy
- **Perspective**: Restrainer, pro-diplomacy, skeptical of military intervention
- **Books**: "Treacherous Alliance," "A Single Roll of the Dice," "Losing an Enemy"

## Analytical Framework
- Advocates direct diplomatic engagement over military pressure
- Skeptical of regime change strategies
- Emphasizes regional consequences of US military action
- Focuses on Iranian domestic calculations and strategic logic
- Views Israeli influence on US Iran policy critically

## Analysis: US-Iran Crisis (February 2, 2026)

From Al Jazeera interview, February 2, 2026:

### On Regional Mediation
- Egypt, Qatar, Turkey, Saudi Arabia, Oman all working to prevent war
- "Most countries in the region with the exception of Israel and potentially the UAE" believe war would be devastating
- War "would not be a quick victory for the US in any way, shape or form"
- Would lead to "major regional destabilization"

### On De-escalation Path
- "The ball is in many ways in Tehran's court"
- Most effective path: "Iranians to talk directly to Trump. Not a negotiation per se, but just initiate that conversation"
- Notes other world leaders have successfully diffused situations by engaging Trump directly
- Could happen as 3-way or 4-way call with regional leaders
- "I don't see what the Iranians have to lose by trying this card"

### On Khamenei's Warning
- "I don't think it is a false threat"
- Primary audience is Trump himself
- Iranian calculation: "Trump has an exaggerated view of Iran's current weakness"
- White House view "perhaps informed by efforts by the Israelis"
- Trump "driving a maximalist bargain... thinking that through this military mobilization he will be able to force the Iranians to capitulate"

### On Iranian Strategic Calculus
Parsi's characterization of Iranian thinking among "those who are skeptical about diplomacy":

**The "small war" theory:**
- Not symbolic, but "unprecedented response in the region and against US forces"
- Goal: "quickly dispel Trump of any belief he may have had that this could be a quick glorious victory similar to Venezuela"
- Force Trump "to step back and then come back to the negotiating table, but with a much more realistic assessment"

**The political calculation:**
- "If they can inflict significant damage on Trump, Trump will recognize that this is ultimately a losing proposition for him as well"
- "He may win the war, but he will lose his presidency"
- Midterm elections factor: "the last thing he wants is to be embroiled in a never-ending forever war in the Middle East"

**Current balance of opinion in Tehran:**
- "So little faith in negotiations with Trump right now that that argument is the one that seems to be prevailing"

### On Iranian Military Capability
- Missile capabilities "proven not only during the 12-day war with Israel, but also in the strikes that they did against Doha"
- "Can penetrate all layers of Israeli air defense systems as well as American air defense systems"
- US bases, assets, and Navy at risk
- Not about winning: "Obviously, they recognize how much they are outgunned"
- About inflicting enough damage to change Trump's calculations

### On Risks
- "No guarantee that this type of calculation could work out"
- "Could and very likely also end up into a much longer war that would be devastating for all sides"
- "Iranians recognize obviously they will take huge hits even in the best case scenario"
- Hopes "regional efforts will make sure that this is never tested in the first place"

## Caveats
- Quincy Institute is restrainer-oriented, institutionally skeptical of military intervention
- NIAC background - some critics view him as sympathetic to Iranian government
- His characterization of Iranian strategic thinking is analysis, not confirmed intelligence
- Provides important counterpoint to hawkish analysts but has clear policy preferences
