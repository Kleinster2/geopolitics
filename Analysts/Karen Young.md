# Karen Young

## Overview

Dr. Karen Young is a Senior Fellow at the [[Middle East Institute]] and professor at Columbia University, specializing in the political economy of the Gulf states and energy markets. Her analysis bridges energy economics and geopolitical risk, particularly for GCC countries.

## Analysis — 2026 Iran conflict

### MEI briefing (March 8, 2026)

In a [[Middle East Institute|MEI]] virtual briefing alongside Gen. [[Joseph Votel]], Young assessed the energy and economic dimensions of the conflict approximately one week into Operation Epic Fury:

**Gulf energy disruption:**
- Iraq has shut in more than 1.5 mb/d, Kuwait following. If Gulf shutins reach 9 mb/d, that exceeds 10% of global supply.
- Saudi East-West pipeline bottleneck: 5 mb/d capacity but Yanbu loading limited to approximately 3 mb/d.
- UAE Fujairah pipeline only 400,000 bpd; tankers reluctant because Fujairah was itself an Iranian target.

**Russia benefiting:** US relaxed sanctions on Russian crude exports to India in the last 48 hours to ensure Indian supply. Russia squeezing Europe harder on gas.

**Tanker escort politics:** "Whose tankers are you escorting? Are we doing this as a favor to the Chinese?" — 80% of strait LNG goes to Asia. Politically loaded question for US domestic debate.

**GCC existential threat:** Young characterizes Iranian strikes on Gulf data centers and airports as "the greatest threat... really existential that the Gulf states are facing" — challenging their identity as international business/finance hubs. 90% of UAE/Qatar populations are non-nationals; airport disruptions are most psychologically damaging for expat populations.

**Energy diversification:** Will be THE priority for 5+ years — not just diversifying sources but accelerating renewables and domestic production.
