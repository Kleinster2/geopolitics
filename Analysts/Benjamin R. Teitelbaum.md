# Benjamin R. Teitelbaum

## Overview

Benjamin R. Teitelbaum is an American ethnographer and CU Boulder associate professor focused on the intersections of music, politics, reactionary ideology, aesthetics, Western esotericism, political theology, and radical-nationalist movements. In this vault, his main use is as an interpreter of [[Traditionalism]] as it moved from an esoteric anti-modern current into parts of the contemporary populist right.

He is not primarily a geopolitical actor. Treat him as an analyst/source whose value is ethnographic access: he interviewed and studied figures around Steve Bannon, Aleksandr Dugin, Olavo de Carvalho, and adjacent right-populist networks for [[War for Eternity]].

## Key Facts

- Current role: Associate Professor of Ethnomusicology and International Affairs, University of Colorado Boulder; Faculty Director, Conference on World Affairs.
- Training: doctorate from Brown University; Bachelor of Music in nyckelharpa performance from Bethany College.
- Institutional connections: former Head of Nordic Studies at CU Boulder; visiting-scholar roles at Karlstad University in Sweden and at Unicamp in Brazil.
- Major books: *Lions of the North: Sounds of the New Nordic Radical Nationalism* (Oxford University Press, 2017) and [[War for Eternity]] (Penguin Press / Dey Street / Penguin, 2020 editions).
- Research terrain: radical nationalism, music and politics, reactionary ideology, Western esotericism, political theology, Scandinavian folklore, and research ethics.

## Analytical Use

- Map the elite-network version of [[Traditionalism]] rather than treating it as a mass ideology.
- Understand how Bannon, Dugin, Olavo, and similar figures translate anti-modern spiritual narratives into metapolitical strategy, media work, and civilizational geopolitics.
- Separate lowercase traditionalism from capital-T Traditionalism: ordinary preference for inherited norms is not the same as the esoteric doctrine built around cyclical decline, hierarchy, initiation, and rejection of modern progress.
- Use Teitelbaum when the question is: how did a marginal intellectual current become legible inside twenty-first-century right-populist politics?

## Caveats

- Teitelbaum's comparative advantage is ethnographic access and interpretive synthesis. Attribute his readings, especially when assigning ideological affiliation or motive to political figures.
- [[War for Eternity]] is a secondary source about a network, not a primary doctrinal text of Traditionalism.
- Do not flatten all right populism, nationalism, or anti-liberal politics into Traditionalism. The current overlaps with those movements but is narrower and more esoteric.
- Some public claims about Traditionalist influence are contested by the figures being described; preserve the disagreement when it matters.
