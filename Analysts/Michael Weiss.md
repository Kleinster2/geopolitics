# Michael Weiss

## Overview

American journalist and editor specialising in Russian intelligence operations, disinformation, and authoritarian regimes. Editor-in-chief of the English-language edition of [[The Insider]] (theins.ru) — the Russia-focused investigative outlet — and a contributing editor at [[New Lines Magazine]] since its October 2020 launch. Earlier founded [[The Interpreter]] (2013), a Russian-media translation and analysis platform. Co-author of *ISIS: Inside the Army of Terror* (2015, with Hassan Hassan), chosen by The Wall Street Journal as one of 10 must-read works on the evolution of Middle East terrorism.

Runs his own Substack newsletter "Foreign Office by M. Weiss" at [macspaunday.substack.com](https://macspaunday.substack.com) and hosts the weekly *Foreign Office with Michael Weiss* podcast (jointly produced by [[New Lines Magazine]] and [[Free Russia Foundation]]) on Russian intelligence operations and active measures — guests include historians, journalists, diplomats, and ex-spies. A history of the GRU is forthcoming: *Trouble with the Neighbors: A History of Russia's Most Secretive Spy Agency*.

The substack URL slug "macspaunday" is a literary reference to the 1930s British leftist poet group (W.H. Auden + Stephen Spender + Cecil Day-Lewis + Louis MacNeice — the "MacSpaunday" group). Signals the historical / literary register Weiss prefers.

## Analytical Framework

- Treats Russian intelligence-service operations as the central analytical lens for Russia foreign policy — operations are not adjuncts to diplomacy but a parallel coercive instrument
- Investigative methodology relies on document analysis, source cultivation inside hostile services, and collaboration with [[Bellingcat]]-adjacent open-source investigators
- Disinformation framework: Russian active measures aim to degrade Western institutional trust, not to persuade — the goal is corrosion, not conversion
- On Syria / ISIS: rise of ISIS attributable to Assad regime decisions (releasing Sunni militants from Sednaya, allowing ISIS to consolidate east) more than to US policy choices; argument developed in the 2015 book

## Why he matters

Weiss occupies the unusual intersection of working investigative journalism (FSB/GRU operations exposed in near-real-time) and editorial leadership of multiple Russia-focused publications. For the vault's [[Russia]] and [[Ukraine]] threads, his output is one of the higher-density sources for Russian intelligence-service activity — including activity that bears on shadow-fleet logistics, sanctions evasion, oligarch finance, and Kremlin pressure on European parties.

The dual role (editor-in-chief of The Insider English + contributing editor of New Lines) gives him an unusual mix of investigative output and broader geopolitical commentary. Treat investigative pieces under his byline at The Insider as primary-source-tier reporting; New Lines pieces are more interpretive/analytical.

## Caveats

- Strongly anti-Kremlin perspective; analysis is interpretive in addition to investigative. The investigative-document layer is generally well-sourced; the interpretive framing is opinionated
- Operationally tied to a coalition of Russia-investigative journalists (Bellingcat, [[Roman Dobrokhotov]], etc.) — the framing is consistent across the network but doesn't represent independent corroboration
- Russia-focus is strong; coverage of other regions ranges narrower
