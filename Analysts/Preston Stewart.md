# Preston Stewart

Preston Stewart is a military and geopolitics commentator who publishes the Substack and video/podcast feed "Preston Stewart." His Substack About page describes him as a former U.S. Army officer, a West Point graduate with a degree in international relations, and a former officer in the 101st Airborne Division, Texas National Guard, and U.S. Army Reserve in Tennessee.

## Relevance

Stewart is useful for fast operational interpretation: connecting military incidents, weapons, geography, and diplomatic timing without turning every battlefield item into a standalone geopolitical thesis.

## May 2026 Hormuz mining episode

In "Iran Just Tried Mining The Strait of Hormuz" (May 26 2026), Stewart interpreted reported U.S. self-defense strikes on Iranian mine-laying boats and a Bandar Abbas SAM site as evidence that the late-May ceasefire/reopening track remained tactically fragile. The value of the piece is the operational distinction: reopening the [[Strait of Hormuz]] requires continuous mine-control, coastal-threat suppression, and shipowner/insurer confidence, not only an agreement headline.

He separated the claim set carefully. The U.S. publicly described strikes on missile launch sites and boats attempting to emplace mines. Additional defense reporting gave the two-IRGC-boat and Bandar Abbas SAM-site detail. Iranian claims about an MQ-9 shootdown and an F-35 being forced away remained unverified in the sources reviewed here. The same-day tanker explosion off Oman was also unclaimed and Reuters said the cause was unknown.
