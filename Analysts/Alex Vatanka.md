# Alex Vatanka

Senior Fellow at the Middle East Institute (MEI) in Washington, D.C. Focus on Iran, Gulf state dynamics, and US-Iran relations.

## Key assessments

### 2026 Iran conflict — Gulf complicity calculus

On Gulf states covertly allowing US HIMARS launches from their territory while publicly claiming neutrality:

> "If the Americans could show them a way to finish off the regime, then they would be more likely to take risks in terms of openly siding against the regime than they are today. At the same time, they aren't going to say no to the U.S. in the midst of a shooting war."

The assessment captures the Gulf states' two-track position: operational cooperation with Washington while maintaining political deniability toward Tehran — a stance sustainable only as long as Iran lacks the capability or will to retaliate specifically against host-nation complicity.

*Source: WSJ (March 18, 2026)*
