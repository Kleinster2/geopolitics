# Paul R. Pillar

## Overview

Retired CIA senior analyst and intelligence officer who has become one of the most prominent critics of US military entanglement with Israel's regional wars. Directs much of his post-government writing at the "entrapment" dynamic: Israel initiating escalation, then drawing the US into conflicts that serve Israeli but not American interests.

## Key Facts
- **Position**: Non-resident Senior Fellow, Center for Security Studies, Georgetown University
- **Position**: Non-resident Fellow, Quincy Institute for Responsible Statecraft
- **Previous**: 28-year CIA career, rising to National Intelligence Officer for the Near East and South Asia
- **Education**: PhD, Princeton University (1978); B.Phil., Oxford University (1971); AB, Dartmouth College (1969)
- **Military**: US Army Reserve officer, active duty 1971-73 (Vietnam)
- **Books**: *Negotiating Peace* (1983); *Terrorism and U.S. Foreign Policy* (2001); *Intelligence and U.S. Foreign Policy: Iraq, 9/11, and Misguided Reform* (2011); *Why America Misunderstands the World* (2016); *Beyond the Water's Edge: How Partisanship Corrupts U.S. Foreign Policy* (2023)
- **Landmark article**: "Intelligence, Policy, and the War in Iraq," *Foreign Affairs* (March/April 2006) — as the former NIO for the region, publicly accused the Bush administration of politicizing intelligence to justify the Iraq invasion. Walter Pincus (Washington Post) called it "one of the most severe indictments of White House actions by a former Bush official since Richard A. Clarke."
- **Regular contributor**: Responsible Statecraft, The National Interest

## Analytical Framework

### The entrapment thesis
Pillar's central argument across 2024-2026: Israel has systematically escalated confrontation with Iran, creating situations where the US is drawn into conflicts that serve Israeli strategic interests but damage American ones.

**Core logic:**
- Israel initiates escalation (Syria strikes, consulate bombing, assassinations)
- Iran responds proportionally but with restraint
- Israel frames Iranian response as aggression requiring US support
- US involvement validates and deepens the entrapment
- Each cycle narrows the space for diplomacy and increases the risk of full war

### On Israeli motivations
Pillar argues Israel's Iran policy serves multiple purposes beyond stated security concerns:
- Weakening a rival for regional influence
- Diverting international attention from the Palestinian occupation
- Inhibiting US-Iran diplomatic engagement
- Maintaining Israel's regional nuclear monopoly
- Netanyahu's personal political survival (coalition maintenance, corruption charges)

### On US complicity
- Biden's "bear hug" strategy emboldened rather than restrained Israel
- Expressing support without using material leverage ensures Israeli escalation continues
- US soft power suffers through association with Israeli military operations
- Each conflict undermines US credibility as a negotiating partner

## Israel-Iran Analysis (2024-2026)

### "Biden is letting Israel trap the US into war with Iran" (October 7, 2024)

Published in Responsible Statecraft on the anniversary of the Hamas attack. Key arguments:

**On Iranian restraint:**
- Iran "does not want war and has been remarkably restrained in trying to avoid it, in the face of repeated Israeli provocations"
- Damascus consulate strike (April 2024) elicited a response only because Israel escalated to attacking a diplomatic compound
- Iran's April 2024 response was "designed and telegraphed in a way to make a show of defiance but — with most of the projectiles certain to be shot down — to cause minimal damage"
- After Israel assassinated Haniyeh in Tehran (July 2024), Iran waited months before responding — and then only after Israel killed Nasrallah

**On Israeli escalation pattern:**
- Israel is "the strongest military power in the Middle East... throwing its armed might around in seemingly every direction but the Mediterranean Sea"
- A nation "drunk on the use of force and stumbling into still more use of it"
- Each tactical success (killing Nasrallah, etc.) "only seems to deepen the inebriation"
- Israeli attacks designed "at least in part to goad Iran into entering a wider war, preferably one that also involves the United States"

**On Netanyahu's political incentives:**
- Dependent on extreme coalition members to stay in power and delay corruption charges
- A wider war serves the rally-around-the-flag effect
- Escalation before the US election could hurt Harris, benefiting Trump (whom Netanyahu preferred)

### "Israel is luring the US into a trap" (June 20, 2025)

Published during the Twelve-Day War (Operation Rising Lion). Key arguments:

**On false pretexts:**
- "The nuclear issue was not the main motivation behind Israel's attack" — target list goes far beyond nuclear facilities
- Israel's objectives include "sabotaging U.S. diplomacy with Iran" and distracting from the Palestinian occupation
- Iran was "willingly negotiating with the United States, with serious intent" before the attack

**On nuclear counterproductivity:**
- The war will not make an Iranian bomb less likely — it may make it more likely
- "No event is more likely to lead Iranian policymakers to take the decision they had not so far taken — to build a nuclear weapon — than an armed attack on their sovereign territory"
- Centrifuge cascades can be reconstructed; the knowledge base survives assassinations
- Without international inspections (derailed by the war), Iran's nuclear activities move out of view

**On regime change folly:**
- US record of regime change in the Middle East is "miserable" — Iraq, Libya, Iran 1953
- If political change occurs, it would "at least as likely to strengthen regime hardliners" as moderates
- Israel "probably would be satisfied with a Libya-type situation of chaos and weakness"
- "One of the last things the Netanyahu government would want to see emerge in Iran is a stable, moderate democracy that enjoys good relations with the United States"

**On endless war risk:**
- "Trump may believe he can do a one-and-done... but this is unlikely to be the end of U.S. combat with Iran"
- Prolonged search-and-destroy mission for dispersed nuclear materials
- Another instance of Israel "mowing the lawn" — only now involving the United States

## Wider Adoption of the Entrapment Framework

**Farah Jan** (War on the Rocks, March 12, 2026): Formalized the alliance entrapment framework in "The Blank Check for Israel and the War with Iran." Used the 1914 analogy — Germany's "blank check" to Austria-Hungary: "Washington chose war over diplomacy not because diplomacy failed, but because the junior partner's objectives required more than any deal could deliver." Rubio's own admission (Israel was going to attack; the US joined preemptively) essentially confirmed the mechanism Pillar had been warning about.

**Post-Epic Fury (March 2026):**
- Published "With focus on Iran and Gaza, Israel is quietly annexing the West Bank" (Responsible Statecraft, March 3) — while attention is on the war, Israel creating irreversible facts on the ground
- Contributed to Responsible Statecraft symposium on post-decapitation Iran alongside Parsi, Barbara Slavin, and Daniel Brumberg

## Caveats
- Quincy Institute affiliation places him in the restrainer camp — institutionally skeptical of military intervention
- Post-CIA career has been consistently critical of US-Israeli security relationship
- Analytical blind spot: may underweight genuinely threatening Iranian behavior by emphasizing restraint
- Provides essential counterweight to hawkish analysts but conclusions are predictable
