# Seth Cropsey

**Seth Cropsey** is a senior fellow at the [[Hudson Institute]] and founder/president of the Yorktown Institute, a think tank focused on maritime strategy and naval power. He served as Deputy Undersecretary of the Navy under both Reagan and George H.W. Bush.

## Background

- Deputy Undersecretary of the Navy (Reagan and George H.W. Bush administrations)
- Director of the International Broadcasting Bureau (Voice of America, Radio Free Europe/Radio Liberty)
- Senior fellow at Hudson Institute since 2010s
- Author of *Mayday: The Decline of American Naval Supremacy* (2013) and *Seablindness: How Political Neglect Is Choking American Seapower and What to Do About It* (2017)
- PhD in political theory

## Analytical framework

Cropsey's work centers on the argument that American global power depends on maritime supremacy, and that the US Navy's decline — in fleet size, shipbuilding capacity, and strategic attention — threatens the entire American-led international order.

He is a consistent advocate for military escalation against perceived US adversaries, particularly [[Iran]] and [[China]]. His analytical framework treats military credibility as indivisible: any perceived failure to demonstrate "unquestionable supremacy" in one theater invites challenge in all others.

## 2026 Iran war commentary

In a WSJ op-ed (March 22-23, 2026), "American Credibility Is on the Line in Iran," Cropsey argued:

- Trump "must put boots on the ground to open the [[Strait of Hormuz]] and demonstrate the unquestionable supremacy of American power"
- Called for "several thousand special-ops forces to southern Iran" backed by regular troops
- Warned halting the campaign would be "a cataclysmic mistake with repercussions well beyond the Middle East"
- Invoked the 1956 [[Suez Crisis]] as the moment Britain and France psychologically became medium powers — argued the US faces the same fate if it withdraws without securing Hormuz
- Claimed failure "could trigger a Chinese move against [[Taiwan]] or a Russian move against NATO"

The piece was characterized by Breitbart as part of a Murdoch-press "globalist" pattern of pressuring Trump toward escalation, noting ongoing friction between the WSJ editorial page and the administration's America First wing.

The Cropsey argument represents the maximalist position in the [[2026 Iran conflict]] ground troops debate: that air power alone cannot achieve US objectives, and that anything short of physical occupation of the strait's approaches constitutes strategic defeat.
