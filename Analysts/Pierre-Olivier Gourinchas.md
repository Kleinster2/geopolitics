# Pierre-Olivier Gourinchas

Pierre-Olivier Gourinchas was the [[IMF]]'s chief economist (Economic Counsellor and Director of the Research Department) from 2022 until July 1 2026, when he returned to the University of California, Berkeley. As author of record for the Fund's World Economic Outlook through the pandemic aftermath, the 2022-23 inflation surge, and the Trump tariff era, he is a central voice on [[Geoeconomic Fragmentation]] and the politics of the dollar system ([[Dollar Dominance]]).

In a June 2026 exit interview (Financial Times, Claire Jones), he warned that the defining risk to the world economy is "tit-for-tat" economic warfare — states exploiting chokepoints (critical minerals, the [[Strait of Hormuz]]) for advantages that are "ultimately self-defeating" because rivals build around them — and argued that trade fragmentation will eventually force financial fragmentation as dollar weaponization drives states to build alternative payment systems.
