# Mike Kuiken

US national-security and US-China policy analyst. Distinguished visiting fellow at the Hoover Institution and vice-chair of the [[US-China Economic and Security Review Commission]]. Former senior congressional national-security staffer (Senate Armed Services Committee).

## Key argument

[[Data as a Factor of Production]] — Kuiken argues (FT, February 9, 2026) that the US should follow China in formally recognizing data as a balance-sheet asset: China has doctrine (2020), accounting rules (2024), and exchanges, while the US has a $200bn data-broker market and no accounting recognition at all. His formulation: the gap reveals "strategic intent versus strategic indifference." Prescriptions: FASB recognition standards, SEC disclosure rules for material data holdings, mandatory federal data valuation.
