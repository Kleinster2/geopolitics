# Chinese Foreign Policy

## Overview
Hub note connecting [[China]]'s foreign policy frameworks, diplomatic structures, and global strategy. Under Xi Jinping, Chinese foreign policy has become more assertive, ideologically articulated, and globally ambitious.

## Core Frameworks

### [[Community with a Shared Future]]
Xi Jinping's signature foreign policy vision (人类命运共同体). Presents alternative to US-led order:
- Rejects Western universalism
- Emphasizes win-win cooperation
- Non-interference principle
- Development-first approach

### [[Comprehensive Strategic Partnership]]
China's tiered partnership diplomacy system:

| Tier | Level | Examples |
|------|-------|----------|
| 1 | Comprehensive Strategic Partnership of Coordination | Russia |
| 2 | Comprehensive Strategic Partnership | Pakistan, Vietnam |
| 3 | Strategic Partnership | Various |
| 4 | Comprehensive Cooperative Partnership | Lower tier |

### [[Belt and Road Initiative]]
Global infrastructure and investment program (一带一路):
- Launched 2013
- 140+ participating countries
- Ports, railways, digital infrastructure
- Criticized as debt-trap diplomacy

## Diplomatic Styles

### Wolf Warrior Diplomacy
Assertive, nationalist diplomatic posture (战狼外交):
- Named after 2015/2017 action films
- Combative social media presence
- Quick response to perceived slights
- Associated with Xi era

### Charm Offensive (Earlier Era)
Pre-Xi approach emphasizing:
- "Peaceful rise"
- Reassurance diplomacy
- Hide capabilities, bide time (Deng)

## Key Principles

### Five Principles of Peaceful Coexistence (1954)
1. Mutual respect for sovereignty
2. Mutual non-aggression
3. Non-interference in internal affairs
4. Equality and mutual benefit
5. Peaceful coexistence

Still cited but increasingly tension with assertive behavior.

### Core Interests
Non-negotiable issues:
- Taiwan
- South China Sea
- Tibet
- Xinjiang
- CCP rule

## Regional Strategies

### Asia-Pacific
- ASEAN engagement, South China Sea pressure
- [[Comprehensive Strategic Partnership]] with [[Vietnam]]
- Economic integration while asserting territorial claims

### Europe
- Divide and engage strategy
- BRI investments (Piraeus port, etc.)
- 16+1 format (Central/Eastern Europe)
- Strained post-COVID, post-Ukraine

### Global South
- Development assistance alternative to West
- BRI infrastructure
- UN voting bloc cultivation
- "South-South cooperation" rhetoric

### United States
- Strategic competition
- Managed tensions, avoid war
- Technology decoupling response
- [[Trade War 2025-2026]] dynamics

## Institutional Structure

### Foreign Ministry
- Formal diplomatic channel
- Spokespersons for daily messaging
- Increasingly assertive under Xi

### CCP International Liaison Department
- Party-to-party relations
- Often parallel to state diplomacy
- Important for some countries

### United Front Work Department
- Overseas Chinese engagement
- Influence operations
- Academic/cultural outreach

## Xi Jinping's Signature

Xi has centralized and personalized foreign policy:
- "Xi Jinping Thought on Diplomacy"
- Personal summits with leaders
- Ideological articulation
- Long-term thinking (2049 goals)

## Current Challenges (2026)

- US decoupling pressure
- European wariness post-Ukraine
- BRI debt sustainability questions
- Demographic headwinds affecting economic model
- Taiwan tensions
