# American AI Export Program

US government program to promote the export of the full American AI technology stack to allied and partner countries — the proliferation half of US AI strategy, the counterpart to [[Export controls]] (which deny the stack to adversaries). Established by **Executive Order 14320, "Promoting the Export of the American AI Technology Stack"** (July 23, 2025); the Department of Commerce / International Trade Administration launched the program on October 21, 2025.

## What it covers

The EO defines "full-stack" export packages spanning the whole layer cake:

| Layer | Examples |
|-------|----------|
| Hardware | Chips, servers, accelerators |
| Software | AI models, security/cybersecurity systems |
| Technology | Data pipelines, labeling systems |
| Services | Data-center storage, cloud, networking |

Commerce solicits proposals from **industry-led consortia** to assemble these packages, evaluated by the Secretary of Commerce with State, War, Energy, and OSTP. A call for proposals opened April 1, 2026 (submissions through June 30, 2026, via AIexports.gov), with approved packages backed by the interagency Economic Diplomacy Action Group.

## Strategic logic

The program is the "American models everywhere" strategy: lock allies and partners into the US AI stack so that American standards, formats, and platforms become the default — a de facto standard-setting play (see [[Technical Standards]]) executed through export promotion rather than invention or regulation. It runs in deliberate tension with denial policy: the same period saw the US place [[Anthropic]]'s top models under [[Export controls]] (the [[Anthropic model export ban (June 2026)]]), even toward allies — cutting against the proliferation logic and exposing the unresolved seam between spreading US AI and controlling it.
