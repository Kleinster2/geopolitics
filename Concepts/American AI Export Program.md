# American AI Export Program

US government program to promote the export of the full American AI technology stack to allied and partner countries — the proliferation half of US AI strategy, the counterpart to [[Export controls]] (which deny the stack to adversaries). Established by **Executive Order 14320, "Promoting the Export of the American AI Technology Stack"** (July 23, 2025); the Department of Commerce / International Trade Administration launched the program on October 21, 2025.

## What it covers

The EO defines "full-stack" export packages spanning the whole layer cake:

| Layer | Examples |
|-------|----------|
| Hardware | Chips, servers, accelerators |
| Software | AI models, security/cybersecurity systems |
| Technology | Data pipelines, labeling systems |
| Services | Data-center storage, cloud, networking |

Commerce solicits proposals from **industry-led consortia** to assemble these packages, evaluated by the Secretary of Commerce with State, War, Energy, and OSTP. A call for proposals opened April 1, 2026 (submissions through June 30, 2026, via AIexports.gov), with approved packages backed by the interagency Economic Diplomacy Action Group.

## Strategic logic

The program is the "American models everywhere" strategy: lock allies and partners into the US AI stack so that American standards, formats, and platforms become the default — a de facto standard-setting play (see [[Technical Standards]]) executed through export promotion rather than invention or regulation. It runs in deliberate tension with denial policy: the same period saw the US place [[Anthropic]]'s top models under [[Export controls]] (the [[Anthropic model export ban (June 2026)]]), even toward allies — cutting against the proliferation logic and exposing the unresolved seam between spreading US AI and controlling it.

## The Gulf: flagship destination

The Gulf is where the program is most visible on the ground. The UAE and Saudi Arabia are converting sovereign-AI ambition into the largest data-center build-out outside the US — and building it on the American stack.

**UAE.** [[Stargate UAE]] — a 5 GW, $30B+ campus in Abu Dhabi (a 1 GW first cluster), run by OpenAI and Oracle on Nvidia GB300 — is the flagship. [[G42]] holds 60%, the sovereignty condition: the infrastructure stays under national control while running US models and silicon. The enabling bargain is the program's logic in miniature — G42 divested its Chinese technology (including Huawei equipment) and took a $1.5B [[Microsoft]] investment in 2024 to qualify for US accelerators. Export promotion and [[Export controls]] meeting in a single transaction: access granted in exchange for choosing the US stack over China's.

**Saudi Arabia.** [[HUMAIN]], the Public Investment Fund's ~$23B AI company, is assembling a 6.4 GW pipeline with DataVolt on Nvidia/AMD chips, alongside the NEOM Oxagon campus. The same pattern: US silicon, sovereign ownership.

The strategic prize is lock-in. If the Gulf's compute runs on American chips, models, and cloud formats, the region's stated bid to be a **neutral host between the US and China** resolves in Washington's favor by default — [[Digital Sovereignty]] purchased with US technology becomes US standard-entrenchment (see [[Technical Standards]]). The seam is the program's own: these are the chips and models the US restricts elsewhere, so every Gulf approval is a judgment that allied proliferation outweighs the diffusion risk — the same tension as the [[Anthropic model export ban (June 2026)]].
