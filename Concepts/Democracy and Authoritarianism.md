# Democracy and Authoritarianism

## Overview
The fundamental divide in global governance between systems based on popular sovereignty, individual rights, and competitive elections versus those based on concentrated power, limited participation, and state control. This divide increasingly shapes international alignments, alliances, and competition.

## Definitions

### Democracy
Government by the people, characterized by:
- Free and fair elections
- Rule of law
- Individual rights
- Political pluralism
- Separation of powers
- Free press

### Authoritarianism
Concentrated political power, characterized by:
- Limited political competition
- Weak rule of law
- Restricted civil liberties
- State control of media
- Regime security priority

### Spectrum
```
Full Democracy → Flawed Democracy → Hybrid → Competitive Auth → Closed Auth
```

## Democratic Foreign Policy

### Strengths
- Soft power, attraction
- Reliable alliances (shared values)
- Economic dynamism
- Innovation advantage

### Weaknesses
- Slow decision-making
- Public opinion constraints
- Electoral cycles
- Hypocrisy charges
- Polarization

### Democratic Peace Theory
- Democracies rarely fight each other
- Shared norms, transparency
- But: requires mature democracies
- And: democracies do fight non-democracies

## Authoritarian Foreign Policy

### Strengths
- Fast decision-making
- Long-term planning
- Ignore public opinion
- Risk tolerance

### Weaknesses
- Information problems
- Poor feedback
- Succession crises
- Legitimacy deficits
- Unreliable partners

### Russia Example
- Putin's long tenure
- Information bubble
- Ukraine miscalculation
- Demonstrates limits
