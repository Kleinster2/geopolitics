# INSTEX

## Overview
INSTEX (Instrument in Support of Trade Exchanges) was a European special purpose vehicle created in 2019 to facilitate trade with Iran while bypassing US sanctions. It was designed to keep the [[JCPOA]] alive after the US withdrew, but largely failed due to limited scope, US pressure, and bank reluctance.

## Key Facts
- **Created**: January 2019
- **Headquarters**: Paris, France
- **Founders**: France, Germany, United Kingdom (E3)
- **Iranian counterpart**: STFI (Special Trade and Finance Instrument)
- **Status**: Effectively defunct
- **Result**: Failure

## How It Was Supposed to Work

### The Problem
After Trump withdrew from JCPOA (May 2018):
- US reimposed sanctions on Iran
- Secondary sanctions threatened European companies
- European banks refused Iran transactions (fear of US penalties)
- Even legal humanitarian trade became impossible
- JCPOA benefits for Iran evaporated

### The Solution (Theoretical)
INSTEX was a barter-like mechanism:

```
European Company A wants to sell medicine to Iran
Iranian Company B wants to sell pistachios to Europe
                    â†“
Instead of money crossing borders:
- INSTEX credits European company
- INSTEX debits Iranian company
- No actual Iran-Europe bank transfer
- US sanctions theoretically not triggered
```

### Why This Might Work
- No dollars involved
- No SWIFT messages to Iran
- No direct bank-to-bank transfers
- Humanitarian goods (exempt from sanctions)
- European sovereignty assertion

## Why It Failed

### 1. Limited Scope
Originally restricted to:
- Food
- Medicine
- Medical devices

Never expanded to oil, manufacturing, or meaningful trade volumes.

### 2. US Pressure
- Trump administration threatened sanctions on INSTEX itself
- Treasury warned European companies
- Made clear US would retaliate
- Europeans backed down

### 3. Bank Reluctance
Even with INSTEX:
- Banks still needed to process domestic transactions
- Compliance departments refused
- Fear of any Iran connection
- Reputational risk too high

### 4. Insufficient Volume
- Only a handful of transactions completed
- Amounts trivial (millions, not billions)
- Couldn't offset sanctions damage
- Symbolic rather than substantive

### 5. Iranian Frustration
Iran saw INSTEX as:
- Too little, too late
- European weakness exposed
- Failure to deliver JCPOA benefits
- Justification for own non-compliance

## Lessons

### On European Autonomy
INSTEX exposed limits of European sovereignty:
- EU cannot protect companies from US sanctions
- Dollar dominance gives US extraterritorial power
- European financial system dependent on US
- Political will insufficient without financial independence

### On Sanctions Evasion
Showed how difficult it is to work around US financial sanctions:
- Even allies struggle
- Banks prioritize US market access
- Compliance culture deeply embedded
- Alternative systems lack scale

### On JCPOA
INSTEX failure contributed to JCPOA collapse:
- Iran got no economic benefit from compliance
- Hardliners vindicated ("West cannot be trusted")
- Moderates discredited
- Iran resumed nuclear activities
