# Nationalism

## Overview
Political ideology centering on loyalty to one's nation, often defined by shared ethnicity, language, culture, or history. Powerful force in international relations—driving state formation, conflict, and resistance to external influence. Currently resurging globally.

## Definitions

### Nation vs. State
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Nation | Cultural/ethnic community |
| State | Political/legal entity |
| Nation-state | When boundaries align |
| Nationalism | Ideology prioritizing nation |

### Types of Nationalism
1. **Civic nationalism**: Based on shared political values
2. **Ethnic nationalism**: Based on ancestry, blood
3. **Religious nationalism**: Nation defined by faith
4. **Economic nationalism**: Prioritize national economic interests

## Nationalism and Geopolitics

### As Mobilizing Force
- Rally populations for conflict
- Resist foreign intervention
- Justify territorial claims
- Build state cohesion

### As Destabilizing Force
- Irredentism (reclaiming "lost" territory)
- Minority persecution
- Resistance to cooperation
- Protectionism

## Historical Waves

### 19th Century
- Nation-state formation (Germany, Italy)
- Decline of empires
- Self-determination principle

### Post-WWI
- Empire dissolution
- Wilsonian self-determination
- New states created

### Decolonization
- Anti-colonial nationalism
- New states in Asia, Africa
- Pan-movements (Arab, African)

### Post-Cold War
- Soviet/Yugoslav dissolution
- Ethnic conflicts
- "End of history" proved wrong

### 21st Century Resurgence
- Populist nationalism
- Anti-globalization
- Immigration backlash
- Sovereignty emphasis

## Contemporary Examples

- Historical grievance narrative
- Ukraine as "not real nation"
- Justifies expansion

### China

- Taiwan as "sacred territory"
- Assertive foreign policy
- Party leverages nationalism

### United States

- Immigration restriction
- Alliance skepticism

### India

- Pakistan rivalry

### Turkey
- Neo-Ottomanism
- Regional assertiveness

- Diaspora mobilization

### European Populism
- Brexit

## Nationalism and Conflict

### Territorial Disputes
- Nationalist claims to territory
- "Historic rights" arguments
- Difficulty of compromise
- Public pressure

### Examples
- China-Japan (Senkaku/Diaoyu)
- India-Pakistan (Kashmir)
- Russia-Ukraine
- Israel-Palestine

### Ethnic Conflict
- Minorities as threats
- Irredentism
- "Protect compatriots" abroad
- Cleansing, genocide risk

## Economic Nationalism

### Manifestations
- Protectionism
- Industrial policy
- National champions
- Resource nationalism

### Current Trends
- "Reshoring" manufacturing
- Supply chain nationalism
- Tech decoupling
- See: [[Supply Chains]]

## Nationalism vs. Internationalism

### Tension
| Nationalism | Internationalism |
|-------------|------------------|
| Sovereignty | Cooperation |
| Self-interest | Common good |
| Borders | Openness |
| Independence | Interdependence |

### Institutions Under Strain
- UN (sovereignty vs. intervention)
- EU (integration vs. sovereignty)
- WTO (free trade vs. protection)
- Climate agreements

## Strategic Implications

### For Great Powers
- Nationalism constrains compromise
- Public opinion matters
- "Rally around flag" effect
- Escalation risks

### For Smaller States
- Resist great power pressure
- Build legitimacy
- But: can't compromise

### For International Order
- Challenges multilateralism
- Sovereignty emphasis
- Rules under strain
- Cooperation harder
