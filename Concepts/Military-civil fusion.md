# Military-Civil Fusion

## Overview
Military-civil fusion is China's doctrine for eroding the boundary between civilian technology development and military modernization. It channels commercial firms, universities, state labs, logistics assets, and industrial capacity into the [[People's Liberation Army]] modernization pipeline.

## Mechanism
- civilian firms build dual-use technology
- universities and laboratories supply talent
- state and military-civil fusion funds supply capital
- commercial cover gives plausible deniability
- PLA modernization absorbs useful outputs

## Relation to Total War

Howard Wang's 2026 total-war work makes the hierarchy clearer. Military-civil fusion is not the final form of China's mobilization doctrine; it is one layer inside a broader national-security architecture. MCF gives the PLA better technology and logistics. [[Chinese total war doctrine]] and [[Integrated national strategic system and capabilities]] go further by treating civilian systems themselves - finance, satellites, cyber, information operations, energy, food, and industrial production - as effects that can be directed by the party center in wartime.

The distinction matters: MCF asks how civilian capacity can strengthen the military. Total war asks how civilian capacity can fight alongside the military.
