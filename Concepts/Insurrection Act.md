# Insurrection Act

## Definition
US federal law (originally 1807) that allows the president to deploy active-duty military forces domestically to suppress civil disorder, insurrection, or rebellion. One of the few exceptions to the Posse Comitatus Act's prohibition on domestic military deployment.

## Key Provisions
- President can deploy military to enforce federal law
- Military can conduct arrests, detain civilians
- No congressional approval required
- President has broad discretion on when conditions warrant invocation

## Historical Uses
- **Civil rights era**: Eisenhower deployed 101st Airborne to Little Rock (1957)
- **1992 LA riots**: George H.W. Bush deployed federal troops
- **Rarely invoked**: Most presidents avoid it

## 2020 Near-Invocation
Trump came close to invoking it during George Floyd protests:
- Defense Secretary Mark Esper and AG Bill Barr pushed back
- Both tried to redirect/distract Trump from going through with it
- Marked rare institutional resistance

## Civil-Military Relations
The Act highlights tension between:
- Presidential authority to maintain order
- Military's apolitical professional identity
- Posse Comitatus tradition
- Constitutional limits on executive power

Service members take oath to Constitution, not president. Using military as political enforcement tool fundamentally contradicts military culture and professional ethics.
