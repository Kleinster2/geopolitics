# Military Power

## Overview
The capacity to use organized violence to achieve political objectives. Central to geopolitics since states ultimately rely on force to defend territory, deter threats, and project influence. Military power enables and constrains all other forms of statecraft.

## Clausewitz's Insight
> "War is the continuation of politics by other means."

Military power is not separate from politics but an instrument of it. Geopolitics is fundamentally about how states use or threaten force to achieve goals.

## Relationship to Geopolitics

```
Geopolitics
    ├── Military Power (coercion, deterrence, war)
    ├── Economic Power (sanctions, trade, investment)
    ├── Diplomatic Power (alliances, institutions)
    └── Soft Power (culture, values, attraction)
```

### Why Military Power Matters
1. **Ultimate arbiter**: When other tools fail, force decides
2. **Deterrence backbone**: Threats must be credible
3. **Alliance foundation**: Security guarantees require capability
4. **Territorial control**: Borders exist because defended
5. **Coercive leverage**: "Speak softly, carry big stick"

## Components of Military Power

### Hard Capabilities
- **Personnel**: Active duty, reserves, conscription base
- **Equipment**: Weapons systems, platforms, technology
- **Readiness**: Training, maintenance, morale
- **Logistics**: Sustainment, supply chains, basing
- **C4ISR**: Command, control, communications, computers, intelligence, surveillance, reconnaissance

### Force Structure
| Domain | Elements |
|--------|----------|

| Air | Air force, fighters, bombers, tankers |
| Space | Satellites, anti-satellite, positioning |

- Technology/R&D
- Geographic position

- Economic sustainment capacity

## Types of Military Power

### Conventional
- State-on-state warfare capability
- Combined arms operations
- Measured in divisions, ships, aircraft

### Nuclear
- Strategic deterrent
- Existential stakes
- See: [[Deterrence]], [[Nuclear Weapons]]

### Asymmetric/Irregular
- Guerrilla warfare
- Terrorism
- Hybrid operations
- Non-state actors

### Power Projection
- Ability to apply force far from home
- Carriers, long-range strike, logistics
- See: [[Power Projection]]

## Military Power in Practice

### Deterrence
- Preventing attack through threat of retaliation
- Requires capability + credibility + communication
- Nuclear and conventional deterrence

### Compellence
- Using force/threats to change behavior
- Harder than deterrence
- Coercive diplomacy

### Defense
- Protecting territory and interests
- Denial strategies
- Alliance commitments

### Power Projection
- Applying force at distance
- Expeditionary operations
- Global vs. regional reach

## Limitations of Military Power

### What Force Can't Do
- Win hearts and minds (easily)
- Build stable governance
- Solve political problems
- Guarantee compliance forever

### Diminishing Returns
- Vietnam, Afghanistan, Iraq lessons
- Occupation costs
- Insurgency resilience
- Political sustainability

### Nuclear Paradox
- Ultimate weapon, limited utility
- Can deter but not easily compel
- Escalation risks
