# Integrated National Strategic System and Capabilities

## Overview
China's "integrated national strategic system and capabilities" is the party-state mechanism meant to integrate national development strategy with national security strategy, then give top leaders the ability to direct civilian and military resources toward strategic objectives.

The term is often adjacent to [[Military-civil fusion]], but Howard Wang argues it is broader. Military-civil fusion mainly accelerated PLA modernization by drawing talent, technology, and industrial capacity from the civilian sector into the military. INSS&C is about giving the party center the ability to direct civilian effects themselves alongside PLA operations.

## Difference From Military-Civil Fusion

| Dimension | Military-civil fusion | INSS&C / total war |
|-----------|----------------------|--------------------|
| Core flow | Civilian technology into PLA modernization | Civilian systems directed by party center in parallel with PLA effects |
| Main purpose | Build better military capability | Harmonize national system effects in conflict |
| Examples | Dual-use firms, universities, ports, logistics support | financial pressure, cyber, information, space services, sanctions evasion, industrial mobilization |
| Command problem | procurement and modernization | sequencing national effects across civilian and military systems |

## Organizational Ambiguity

Public party and PLA documents stress the concept's importance, but the bureaucracy remains opaque. Wang notes that hard details on leadership, staffing, meeting cadence, and organizational structure are not available. Given the scope, the likely political fact is that implementation cannot sit below Xi Jinping's level.

## Strategic Meaning

INSS&C is the command-and-coordination problem created by [[Chinese total war doctrine]]. If total war requires civilian systems to become wartime effects, someone must decide which civilian effect is used, in what sequence, against which adversary system, and alongside which PLA operation.
