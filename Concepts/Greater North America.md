# Greater North America

## Overview
Pentagon strategic map unveiled by Secretary of War [[Pete Hegseth]] in March 2026, defining an "immediate security perimeter" stretching from [[Greenland]] to Ecuador and from Alaska to Guyana — everything north of the equator in the Western Hemisphere. Formalizes the geographic scope of the [[Donroe Doctrine]] and the [[2026 National Defense Strategy]]'s Western Hemisphere pivot. The Amazon and the Andes serve as natural dividing lines between the US "immediate security perimeter" and a secondary "burden sharing" zone to the south.

## Rollout

### Americas Counter Cartel Conference (March 5, 2026)
First articulated at the inaugural "Americas Counter Cartel Conference" (A3C) at [[SOUTHCOM]] headquarters in Doral, Florida.

Hegseth's key statement:

> "Every sovereign nation and territory north of the equator, from Greenland to Ecuador and from Alaska to Guyana, is not part of the 'Global South.' It is an immediate security perimeter in this great neighbourhood that we all live in."

He explicitly attributed the concept to Trump:

> "Trump has drawn a new strategic map, from Greenland to the Gulf of America to the Panama Canal and its surrounding countries. At the Department of War we call this strategic map the Greater North America."

Seventeen nations signed a joint security declaration at the A3C, including [[Argentina]], [[Ecuador]], El Salvador, Honduras, and [[Panama Canal Crisis|Panama]].

### WWII Parallel: "Quarter Sphere Defence"
Hegseth invoked a WWII precedent:

> "This is what we did in World War II. We called it the 'quarter-sphere defense,' and we will do this again if we're serious about our national security and if we prioritize geography."

The historical reference: In 1940-41, before Pearl Harbor, the War Department developed a "quarter-hemisphere" strategy concentrating active defense north of a line drawn from the Galapagos through the Atlantic "bulge" of [[Brazil]]. This was the initial US strategic space before it expanded to full hemisphere defense and then global engagement after Pearl Harbor. The framework is documented in the official US Army WWII history series, "The Framework of Hemisphere Defense."

Hegseth selectively invokes the pre-Pearl Harbor defensive perimeter — the most geographically constrained phase of US wartime strategy — as a model for 2026. Historically, this approach was superseded within months.

## Geographic Structure

### Tier 1: Immediate Security Perimeter (North of Equator)
Everything north of the Amazon/Andes line:
- [[United States]], [[Canada]], [[Greenland]]
- [[Mexico]], all Caribbean nations, Central America
- Northern South America: [[Colombia]], Ecuador, Guyana, [[Venezuela]]
- Cuba included geographically but treated as adversary within the perimeter

Hegseth on the dividing line:

> "Each one of these countries sits north of the two basic geographic barriers that exist in this region, the Amazon and the Andes Mountains."

### Tier 2: Burden Sharing Zone (South of Equator)
[[Brazil]], [[Argentina]], Chile, Peru, Bolivia, Paraguay, Uruguay — expected to "take a greater role in defending the South Atlantic and the South Pacific and to secure critical infrastructure and resources in partnership with us and other Western nations."

This places Brazil outside the direct US security umbrella while expecting it to carry defense burden for the southern hemisphere.

### Key Terrain (per 2026 NDS)
The strategic map explicitly incorporates:
- [[Greenland Crisis|Greenland]] — Arctic, missile defense, minerals
- Gulf of America (renamed from Gulf of Mexico) — energy, maritime domain
- [[Panama Canal Crisis|Panama Canal]] — global chokepoint

## Institutional Architecture

### US Army Western Hemisphere Command (USAWHC)
Activated December 5, 2025, at Fort Liberty (formerly Bragg). Serves as theater army for both NORTHCOM and SOUTHCOM — effectively unifying the hemisphere under one Army command structure. Initial operating capability February 2026, full operating capability summer 2026.

### Force Posture
- 11,000 troops surged to the Caribbean (8x baseline)
- 101st Airborne placed in "operational control" of the southern US border
- 44+ strikes against suspected drug smugglers in Caribbean and Eastern Pacific, killing at least 157 people across 47 vessels
- USS Gerald Ford deployed for Operation Southern Spear

### Ideological Framing
Hegseth added an ideological dimension, declaring the nations within the perimeter must be "Christian nations under God" opposing "radical narco-communism." This blends security architecture with civilizational rhetoric.

## Monroe Doctrine Lineage

Hegseth made the connection explicit:

> "President Trump has reestablished the Monroe Doctrine. The Trump Corollary... you can call it the Donroe Doctrine."

He quoted Monroe's 1823 declaration directly:

> "We should consider any attempt on malign countries to extend their system to any portion of this hemisphere as dangerous to our peace and safety."

### Evolution
| Era | Scope | Method |
|-----|-------|--------|
| Monroe (1823) | Hemisphere | Declaratory — British Navy enforced |
| Roosevelt Corollary (1904) | Caribbean basin | "International police power" |
| Quarter Sphere (1940-41) | North of Brazil | Pre-war defensive perimeter |
| Cold War | Full hemisphere | Covert + proxy operations |
| Greater North America (2026) | North of equator | Direct military presence + force |

## Reactions

### Latin America
The 17 A3C signatories "framed that document as a reaffirmation of their own 'sovereignty'" — notably defensive framing suggesting discomfort with US unilateral claims.

[[Colombia]]: President Gustavo Petro responded to earlier US strikes in Colombian waters: "The United States has invaded our national territory, fired a missile to kill a humble fisherman, and destroyed his family, his children. This is Bolivar's homeland, and they are murdering his children with bombs."

Ecuador: Operations conducted without congressional authorization despite local referendum opposition. The US partnered with Ecuador on "Operation Total Extermination" against alleged cartel targets. A NYT investigation found a bombed site presented as a cartel target was actually a rural dairy farm.

Amazon Watch noted the policies "feed hard-line discourse of MAGA-aligned political movements angling to win presidential elections this year in Peru, Colombia, and Brazil."

### Canada
Polling found 57.9% of Canadians believe a US invasion is likely. The 2026 NDS calls for US control of the Northwest Passage, which [[Canada]] claims as sovereign waters.

### Academic/Analytical
Professor Julia Steinberger compared it to the "Greater Israel" movement: "Right now the US and Israel are realizing 'Greater Israel' by attacking-invading Lebanon and Iran. Hegseth is saying it's Greenland, Cuba, Canada, and Mexico next."

Canadian professor Graeme Garrard: "By 'Greater North America' he means 'Greater United States.' The US is now and has long been a menace to its hemispheric neighbors."

Twenty members of Congress called in February 2026 to "drive a stake through the heart of the obsolete Monroe Doctrine."

### Congressional Scrutiny
- Rep. Eugene Vindman (D-VA) pressed SOUTHCOM commander Gen. Francis Donovan on whether "no quarter, no survivors" orders had been issued
- Sen. Mark Kelly (D-AZ): "'No quarter' isn't some wanna be tough guy line — it means something"
- Six Democratic veteran lawmakers released a video reminding service members they can legally defy unlawful orders

## Implications

### For Brazil
The Amazon/Andes dividing line places [[Brazil]] in the Tier 2 "burden sharing" zone — a partner expected to carry weight, not a nation under the US security umbrella. The administration was reportedly considering designating Brazil's Primeiro Comando da Capital (PCC) and Comando Vermelho as foreign terrorist organizations (reported, NYT). This would give the US legal basis for operations touching Brazilian territory.

### For the Hemisphere
- Formalizes a two-tier Western Hemisphere with the US as uncontested hegemon north of the equator
- Operationalizes the [[Donroe Doctrine]] with geographic precision
- Provides doctrinal justification for interventions already underway
- Creates an institutional architecture (USAWHC) to sustain the posture

### For Global Order
- Largest formal US sphere-of-influence claim since the Cold War
- Invites comparison with Chinese claims in the [[South China Sea]] and Russian claims over the "near abroad"
- Validates [[Hal Brands]]'s thesis that if great powers grab spheres, the US will have "the greatest sphere of them all"
