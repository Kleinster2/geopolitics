# Political Risk

## Overview
The risk that political decisions, events, or conditions will affect an organization's ability to achieve its objectives. Central concern for multinational businesses operating across jurisdictions. Entire industry exists to analyze, quantify, and mitigate political risk. Increasingly important as geopolitics returns.

## Definition

### What Is Political Risk?
- Government actions affecting business
- Instability, conflict
- Regulatory changes
- Expropriation
- Policy shifts

### Versus Other Risks
| Risk Type | Source |
|-----------|--------|
| Political | Government, instability |
| Economic | Markets, currency |
| Country | Combined political + economic |
| Geopolitical | Interstate relations |

## Types of Political Risk

### Macro Political Risk
- Affects all foreign business
- Revolution, war
- Regime change
- Systemic instability

### Micro Political Risk
- Sector/firm specific
- Targeted regulation
- Nationalization of industry
- Discriminatory policy

### Categories
1. **Expropriation**: Asset seizure
2. **Transfer risk**: Capital controls
3. **Violence**: War, terrorism
4. **Regulatory**: Policy changes
5. **Breach of contract**: Government defaults
6. **Corruption**: Governance failures
